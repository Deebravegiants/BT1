### Title
Attacker Can Exhaust the Daily Mint Limit at Near-Zero Net Cost, Temporarily Freezing All L2 Deposits - (File: contracts/pools/RSETHPoolV3ExternalBridge.sol)

### Summary
The `limitDailyMint` modifier in `RSETHPoolV3ExternalBridge`, `RSETHPoolV3`, and `RSETHPoolV3WithNativeChainBridge` enforces a shared `dailyMintLimit` on wrsETH minting. Because the `deposit()` functions are permissionless and the depositor receives wrsETH worth their full principal (minus an optional fee), an attacker can exhaust the entire daily limit in a single transaction at near-zero net cost, blocking all other users from depositing for up to 24 hours.

### Finding Description
All three L2 pool variants maintain a shared daily minting counter (`dailyMintAmount`) that is incremented on every `deposit()` call and reset once per day. The `limitDailyMint` modifier enforces this:

```solidity
// RSETHPoolV3ExternalBridge.sol lines 153-158
if (dailyMintAmount + rsETHAmount > dailyMintLimit) {
    revert DailyMintLimitExceeded();
}
dailyMintAmount += rsETHAmount;
``` [1](#0-0) 

The modifier is applied to both `deposit(string)` (ETH) and `deposit(address,uint256,string)` (ERC-20 tokens): [2](#0-1) [3](#0-2) 

The fee (`feeBps`) can be set to zero by the admin (the only validation is `_feeBps > 10_000`), and even when non-zero, the attacker receives wrsETH worth their deposited principal minus the fee — their capital is not destroyed: [4](#0-3) 

The same pattern exists identically in `RSETHPoolV3` and `RSETHPoolV3WithNativeChainBridge`: [5](#0-4) [6](#0-5) 

### Impact Explanation
Once `dailyMintAmount` reaches `dailyMintLimit`, every subsequent `deposit()` call by any user reverts with `DailyMintLimitExceeded` until the next 24-hour window resets the counter: [7](#0-6) 

This constitutes **temporary freezing of funds** — legitimate users are unable to deposit ETH or LSTs into the L2 pool and receive wrsETH for up to 24 hours. The attacker's own capital is not lost; they hold wrsETH equivalent to their deposit and can later redeem or bridge it.

**Severity: Medium** — Temporary freezing of funds per the allowed impact scope.

### Likelihood Explanation
- The attack requires only a single `deposit()` call with enough ETH/tokens to fill the remaining daily limit.
- On L2 chains (Base, Linea, Optimism, Scroll), gas costs are negligible.
- The attacker's net cost is at most `feeBps / 10_000` of the deposited amount (and zero if `feeBps == 0`). Since they receive wrsETH back, the principal is preserved.
- The attack can be repeated every 24 hours indefinitely.
- No special role or privileged access is required — any address with sufficient capital can execute this.

### Recommendation
1. **Introduce a per-user deposit cap or rate limit** so no single address can consume the entire daily limit in one transaction.
2. **Ensure `feeBps` is always non-zero** and set at a level that makes repeated exhaustion economically irrational (e.g., a fee that is not recoverable via wrsETH redemption).
3. **Consider a minimum deposit cooldown per address** to prevent rapid repeated deposits from a single attacker.

### Proof of Concept
```
Preconditions:
  - dailyMintLimit = 1000 wrsETH (example)
  - feeBps = 0 (or any small value)
  - rsETH/ETH rate = 1.05 (so 1050 ETH mints ~1000 wrsETH)

Attack steps:
1. Attacker calls RSETHPoolV3ExternalBridge.deposit{value: 1050 ether}("")
2. limitDailyMint modifier computes rsETHAmount ≈ 1000e18
3. Check: dailyMintAmount(0) + 1000e18 <= dailyMintLimit(1000e18) → passes
4. dailyMintAmount is now == dailyMintLimit
5. Attacker receives ~1000 wrsETH (principal preserved)

Result:
6. Any subsequent deposit() by any user reverts with DailyMintLimitExceeded
7. All L2 deposits are blocked for up to 24 hours
8. Attack repeats next day at near-zero net cost
```
