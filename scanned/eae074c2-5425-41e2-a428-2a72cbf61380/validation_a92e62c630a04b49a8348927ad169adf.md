### Title
`_calculatePayoutAmount` silently reduces user payout below initiation-time `expectedAssetAmount` on adverse oracle price movements — (`contracts/LRTWithdrawalManager.sol`)

### Summary
`initiateWithdrawal` records an `expectedAssetAmount` computed from oracle prices at initiation. `_calculatePayoutAmount`, called during `unlockQueue`, returns `min(expectedAtInitiation, currentReturn)`. If the oracle price of rsETH falls or the asset price rises between initiation and unlock — through normal oracle operation, no compromise required — the stored `expectedAssetAmount` is overwritten with the lower value and the user receives fewer assets than the amount committed at initiation.

### Finding Description
In `initiateWithdrawal`, `getExpectedAssetAmount` computes `rsETHUnstaked * rsETHPrice / assetPrice` at the current block and stores it as `request.expectedAssetAmount`. [7](#0-6) 

When the operator later calls `unlockQueue`, `_unlockWithdrawalRequests` calls `_calculatePayoutAmount` with the oracle prices at unlock time:

```solidity
uint256 currentReturn = (request.rsETHUnstaked * rsETHPrice) / assetPrice;
return (request.expectedAssetAmount < currentReturn) ? request.expectedAssetAmount : currentReturn;
``` [2](#0-1) 

If `currentReturn < expectedAssetAmount` (rsETH price dropped or asset price rose), the function returns `currentReturn`, and line 804 overwrites the stored field:

```solidity
request.expectedAssetAmount = payoutAmount; // payoutAmount < original expectedAssetAmount
``` [8](#0-7) 

`completeWithdrawal` then transfers the overwritten (reduced) amount to the user. [4](#0-3) 

The operator's `minimumRsEthPrice`/`maximumAssetPrice` bounds only gate whether `unlockQueue` proceeds; they do not protect the user's initiation-time rate within the accepted window.

### Impact Explanation
Users receive fewer assets than the amount committed and locked at initiation without any slashing event. The `expectedAssetAmount` field and `getExpectedAssetAmount` function name imply a guaranteed minimum, but the contract does not honour it when oracle prices move adversely during the withdrawal delay period (~8 days). Impact is **Low**: the contract fails to deliver promised returns, but no funds are lost from the protocol.

### Likelihood Explanation
The withdrawal delay is ~8 days (`8 days / 12 seconds` blocks). [9](#0-8) 

Over an 8-day window, rsETH/asset price ratios routinely fluctuate due to LST rebasing, EigenLayer reward accrual, and market conditions. No privileged action or oracle compromise is needed — only a normal oracle update between initiation and unlock. Likelihood is **Medium** given the long delay window.

### Recommendation
At unlock time, pay out `max(expectedAtInitiation, currentReturn)` rather than `min(...)`, so users always receive at least the rate they were shown at initiation (absent a genuine slashing event). Slashing can be handled as a separate, explicitly documented exception. Alternatively, document clearly that `expectedAssetAmount` is not a floor guarantee and rename it to `estimatedAssetAmount` to avoid misleading users.

### Proof of Concept
