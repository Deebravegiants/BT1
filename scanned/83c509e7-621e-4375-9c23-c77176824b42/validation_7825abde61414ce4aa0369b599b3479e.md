### Title
Paused LST Token Causes `completeWithdrawal` to Revert After rsETH Is Already Burned, Temporarily Freezing User Funds - (File: `contracts/LRTWithdrawalManager.sol`)

---

### Summary

Several LST tokens supported by the protocol (e.g., stETH, ETHx) contain emergency pause functionality. If such a token is paused at the time a user calls `completeWithdrawal`, the final asset transfer reverts. Because the user's rsETH was already burned in the preceding `unlockQueue` step, the user is left with neither their rsETH nor their LST until the token is unpaused.

---

### Finding Description

The withdrawal lifecycle in `LRTWithdrawalManager` is split into two operator/user steps:

**Step 1 — `unlockQueue` (operator-called):** [1](#0-0) 

The operator burns the user's rsETH and redeems the corresponding LST amount from `LRTUnstakingVault` into `LRTWithdrawalManager`. After this call, the user's rsETH is permanently destroyed and the LST sits in the withdrawal manager contract.

**Step 2 — `completeWithdrawal` (user-called):** [2](#0-1) 

This calls `_processWithdrawalCompletion`, which ends with: [3](#0-2) 

Which routes to `_transferAsset`: [4](#0-3) 

For ERC20 LSTs, this executes `IERC20(asset).safeTransfer(to, amount)`. If the LST contract is paused at this moment, `safeTransfer` reverts. The function has no fallback, no pull-pattern, and no timestamp-based decoupling. The user cannot receive their funds until the LST is unpaused.

The same revert path also blocks `completeWithdrawalForUser`: [5](#0-4) 

A secondary freeze point exists in `NodeDelegator.completeUnstaking`, where a paused LST blocks the transfer from the NDC to the unstaking vault, stalling the entire pipeline upstream: [6](#0-5) 

---

### Impact Explanation

**Medium — Temporary freezing of funds.**

After `unlockQueue` executes, the user's rsETH is burned and the LST is held in `LRTWithdrawalManager`. If the LST is paused, `completeWithdrawal` reverts on every call. The user holds no rsETH and cannot receive their LST. The funds are inaccessible for the duration of the pause. This matches the "temporary freezing of funds" impact class.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**Medium.**

Both stETH (Lido) and ETHx (Stader) — two of the primary LSTs supported by the protocol — have documented emergency pause mechanisms callable by their respective governance/admin roles. While pauses are infrequent, they are a known operational reality (e.g., Lido has paused stETH transfers during security incidents). The protocol has no whitelist or pause-awareness check on supported assets, so any paused LST triggers this path.

---

### Recommendation

Decouple the LST transfer from the completion step using a pull-payment pattern. When `completeWithdrawal` is called and the transfer fails, record the owed amount in a claimable mapping rather than reverting. Alternatively, introduce a separate `rescueWithdrawal` function that allows users to claim their LST once the token is unpaused, without requiring the operator to re-run `unlockQueue`.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. User calls `initiateWithdrawal(stETH, rsETHAmount)` — rsETH transferred to `LRTWithdrawalManager`.
2. Operator calls `unlockQueue(stETH, ...)`:
   - Line 305: `rsETH.burnFrom(address(this), rsETHBurned)` — user's rsETH is permanently destroyed.
   - Line 307: `unstakingVault.redeem(stETH, amount)` — stETH moved into `LRTWithdrawalManager`.
3. Lido governance pauses stETH transfers (emergency scenario).
4. User calls `completeWithdrawal(stETH)`:
   - Reaches `_transferAsset(stETH, user, amount)` at line 734.
   - `IERC20(stETH).safeTransfer(user, amount)` reverts because stETH is paused.
5. Every subsequent call to `completeWithdrawal` reverts. The user has no rsETH and no stETH. Funds remain frozen in `LRTWithdrawalManager` until stETH is unpaused.
