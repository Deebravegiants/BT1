### Title
Donated LSTs Inflate `_getTotalEthInProtocol()`, DoS-ing `updateRSETHPrice()` via `DailyFeeMintLimitExceeded` and Enabling Stale-Price rsETH Over-Minting — (`contracts/LRTOracle.sol`)

---

### Summary

`LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice()` is an unrestricted public function. Its price calculation reads raw ERC-20 balances of protocol contracts, which any external actor can inflate by transferring supported LSTs (e.g. stETH) directly to `LRTDepositPool` without going through `depositAsset()`. A sufficiently large donation causes the computed protocol fee to exceed `maxFeeMintAmountPerDay`, making `_checkAndUpdateDailyFeeMintLimit` revert inside `_updateRsETHPrice()`. Because both the public `updateRSETHPrice()` and the manager-only `updateRSETHPriceAsManager()` call the same internal function, **neither path can update the price**. The stored `rsETHPrice` is frozen at a stale (below-actual) value. An attacker who also deposits ETH during the freeze window mints rsETH at the artificially low price, extracting value from existing rsETH holders.

---

### Finding Description

**Step 1 — Public price update entry point**

`updateRSETHPrice()` carries only a `whenNotPaused` guard; no role check exists. [1](#0-0) 

`updateRSETHPriceAsManager()` is role-gated but calls the identical internal function. [2](#0-1) 

**Step 2 — TVL computed from raw token balances**

`_getTotalEthInProtocol()` iterates supported assets and calls `ILRTDepositPool.getTotalAssetDeposits(asset)`, which resolves to `getAssetDistributionData()`. For every LST the deposit pool balance is read as:

```solidity
assetLyingInDepositPool = IERC20(asset).balanceOf(address(this));
``` [3](#0-2) 

For ETH the raw native balance is used:

```solidity
ethLyingInDepositPool = address(this).balance;
``` [4](#0-3) 

Both values are freely inflatable: ERC-20 tokens can be `transfer()`-ed to any address without the recipient's consent; ETH can be force-sent via `selfdestruct`. `LRTDepositPool` also has a `receive()` function (confirmed present in the file), making direct ETH donation trivial.

**Step 3 — Inflated TVL triggers oversized protocol fee**

Inside `_updateRsETHPrice()`, if `totalETHInProtocol > previousTVL` the protocol fee is:

```solidity
uint256 rewardAmount = totalETHInProtocol - previousTVL;
protocolFeeInETH = (rewardAmount * lrtConfig.protocolFeeInBPS()) / 10_000;
``` [5](#0-4) 

A large donation makes `rewardAmount` arbitrarily large, so `protocolFeeInETH` can be made to exceed any finite `maxFeeMintAmountPerDay`.

**Step 4 — Daily fee limit reverts for everyone, including the manager**

```solidity
if (currentPeriodMintedFeeAmount + feeAmount > maxFeeMintAmountPerDay) {
    revert DailyFeeMintLimitExceeded(...);
}
``` [6](#0-5) 

This revert is inside `_updateRsETHPrice()`, which is the only code path for both `updateRSETHPrice()` and `updateRSETHPriceAsManager()`. Neither the public caller nor the manager can bypass it. The stored `rsETHPrice` is therefore frozen.

**Step 5 — Stale price used for rsETH minting**

`LRTDepositPool.getRsETHAmountToMint()` reads the frozen price:

```solidity
rsethAmountToMint = (amount * lrtOracle.getAssetPrice(asset)) / lrtOracle.rsETHPrice();
``` [7](#0-6) 

Because `rsETHPrice` is below the true value, the division yields a larger `rsethAmountToMint` than warranted, over-minting rsETH to the depositor.

---

### Impact Explanation

**High. Theft of unclaimed yield.**

An attacker who donates enough LSTs to freeze the price update, then immediately deposits ETH, receives more rsETH than the true exchange rate entitles them to. The surplus rsETH represents value extracted from existing rsETH holders: their proportional claim on the underlying ETH pool is diluted without compensation. The freeze persists until the daily period resets (up to 24 hours), and the attacker can repeat the donation each day to maintain the condition.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**Medium.**

- The attack requires no special role or privileged access — only the ability to transfer a supported LST.
- The donation amount needed depends on `maxFeeMintAmountPerDay` and `protocolFeeInBPS`. With a 10% fee and a daily limit of, say, 1 000 rsETH at a price of ~1.05 ETH, the attacker must donate roughly 10 500 ETH-equivalent of stETH. This is a non-trivial but achievable sum for a well-capitalised adversary.
- The attacker recovers their cost through the over-minted rsETH if their deposit is large enough, making the attack economically rational at scale.
- No MEV or front-running is required; the attacker controls both the donation and the deposit timing.

---

### Recommendation

1. **Track deposited balances with internal accounting variables** rather than reading raw `balanceOf` / `address.balance`. Increment the counter only inside `depositAsset()` and decrement it on withdrawal. Donated tokens that bypass `depositAsset()` will then be invisible to the oracle.
2. **Alternatively, cap the single-call TVL increase** used for fee computation to a configurable maximum per update, preventing a single large donation from producing an unbounded fee.
3. **Separate the fee-limit revert from the price-update path** so that a fee-limit breach caps the fee at `maxFeeMintAmountPerDay` rather than reverting the entire price update, preserving liveness.

---

### Proof of Concept

```
Setup:
  rsETHPrice (stored)   = 1.05 ETH
  rsethSupply           = 100 000 rsETH
  previousTVL           = 105 000 ETH
  protocolFeeInBPS      = 1 000  (10 %)
  maxFeeMintAmountPerDay = 500 rsETH  (~525 ETH at current price)

Attack:
  1. Attacker calls stETH.transfer(LRTDepositPool, 6 000 ETH-worth of stETH)
     — no depositAsset() call, no rsETH minted to attacker yet.

  2. totalETHInProtocol = 105 000 + 6 000 = 111 000 ETH
     rewardAmount       = 111 000 - 105 000 = 6 000 ETH
     protocolFeeInETH   = 6 000 * 10% = 600 ETH
     newRsETHPrice      = (111 000 - 600) / 100 000 ≈ 1.104 ETH
     rsethFeeToMint     = 600 / 1.104 ≈ 543 rsETH  > maxFeeMintAmountPerDay (500)

  3. updateRSETHPrice() reverts with DailyFeeMintLimitExceeded.
     updateRSETHPriceAsManager() also reverts (same internal path).
     rsETHPrice remains frozen at 1.05 ETH.

  4. Attacker calls LRTDepositPool.depositAsset(ETH, 10 000 ETH, ...)
     rsethAmountToMint = 10 000 / 1.05 ≈ 9 524 rsETH   (stale price)
     Fair amount       = 10 000 / 1.104 ≈ 9 058 rsETH
     Over-mint         ≈ 466 rsETH  (~515 ETH of value stolen from existing holders)

  5. Attacker's net: donated 6 000 ETH of stETH, gained ~515 ETH of excess rsETH value.
     At larger deposit sizes the excess grows proportionally.
     The freeze can be repeated each day.
```
