### Title
Incorrect Fee Basis Tracking Charges Protocol Fees on Loss Recovery — (`contracts/LRTOracle.sol`)

---

### Summary

`LRTOracle._updateRsETHPrice()` uses the last stored `rsETHPrice` as the fee-calculation basis. When the protocol suffers a loss the price is written down; when it subsequently recovers, the fee is charged on the full recovery delta — including the portion that merely restores the prior loss — even though `highestRsethPrice` (the true all-time-high basis) is already tracked in the same function and never used for fee gating.

---

### Finding Description

Inside `_updateRsETHPrice()` the fee trigger is:

```solidity
// contracts/LRTOracle.sol  lines 233-247
uint256 previousTVL = rsethSupply.mulWad(rsETHPrice);   // ← last stored price, not peak price

if (!protocolPaused && totalETHInProtocol > previousTVL) {
    uint256 rewardAmount = totalETHInProtocol - previousTVL;
    protocolFeeInETH = (rewardAmount * lrtConfig.protocolFeeInBPS()) / 10_000;
}

uint256 newRsETHPrice = (totalETHInProtocol - protocolFeeInETH).divWad(rsethSupply);
```

`rsETHPrice` is updated every call, so after a loss it is written down to the depressed value. The contract already maintains `highestRsethPrice` (lines 252–296) as a one-way ratchet, but that variable is used only for pause-threshold checks — never as the fee basis.

**Step-by-step scenario**

| Time | Event | rsETHPrice | totalETHInProtocol | previousTVL | rewardAmount | Fee charged? |
|------|-------|-----------|-------------------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| T0 | Steady state | 1.00 ETH | 100 ETH | 100 ETH | 0 | No |
| T1 | LST depeg / slash | — | 95 ETH | — | — | — |
| T1 | `updateRSETHPrice()` | **0.95 ETH** | 95 ETH | 100 ETH | 0 (loss) | No |
| T2 | Protocol recovers | — | 100 ETH | — | — | — |
| T2 | `updateRSETHPrice()` | — | 100 ETH | **95 ETH** | **5 ETH** | **Yes — fee on loss recovery** |

At T2 the 5 ETH "reward" is not a genuine gain; it is merely the restoration of the 5 ETH that was lost at T1. Because `rsETHPrice` was written down to 0.95 ETH, `previousTVL` is 95 ETH instead of 100 ETH, and the entire recovery delta is treated as profit subject to the protocol fee. The fee is extracted by minting additional rsETH to the treasury (line 306), diluting all existing rsETH holders.

The `highestRsethPrice` variable — which would give the correct "no-fee-until-new-ATH" behaviour — is right there in the same function but is never consulted for the fee gate.

---

### Impact Explanation

**High — theft of unclaimed yield.**

rsETH holders lose a portion of their yield every time the protocol recovers from any downward price movement. The fee is minted as rsETH to the treasury, permanently diluting the share of every existing holder. The magnitude scales with `protocolFeeInBPS` and the size of the loss-recovery cycle; repeated small oscillations compound the dilution over time.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**Medium.**

LST prices fluctuate routinely (stETH/ETH ratio, rETH/ETH ratio), and EigenLayer slashing is an explicit protocol risk. Any price update that follows a prior write-down — even a minor one — triggers the incorrect fee. `updateRSETHPrice()` is a public, permissionless function (line 87–89), so the fee can be crystallised by any caller immediately after a recovery.

---

### Recommendation

Replace the fee basis with `highestRsethPrice` so that fees are only charged on genuine all-time-high gains:

```solidity
// Use peak price as basis, not last stored price
uint256 previousTVL = rsethSupply.mulWad(highestRsethPrice);
```

This mirrors the correct PnL-tracking approach: a fee is earned only when the protocol surpasses its previous peak, not when it merely recovers from a loss.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Deploy with `rsETHPrice = 1e18`, `highestRsethPrice = 1e18`, `rsethSupply = 100e18`, `totalETHInProtocol = 100e18`, `protocolFeeInBPS = 1000` (10 %).
2. Simulate a 5 % loss: `totalETHInProtocol` drops to `95e18`.
3. Call `updateRSETHPrice()` → no fee (loss path), `rsETHPrice` written down to `0.95e18`.
4. Simulate full recovery: `totalETHInProtocol` rises back to `100e18`.
5. Call `updateRSETHPrice()`:
   - `previousTVL = 100e18 × 0.95 = 95e18`
   - `rewardAmount = 100e18 − 95e18 = 5e18`
   - `protocolFeeInETH = 5e18 × 10 % = 0.5e18`
   - `newRsETHPrice = (100e18 − 0.5e18) / 100e18 = 0.995e18` — **below the pre-loss price of 1.0e18**
   - Treasury mints `≈ 0.5e18 / 0.995e18 ≈ 0.503` rsETH at the expense of existing holders.
6. rsETH holders end up with a price of 0.995 ETH after a full recovery, not 1.0 ETH. The 0.5 ETH fee was charged on recovering a loss, not on a genuine gain.
