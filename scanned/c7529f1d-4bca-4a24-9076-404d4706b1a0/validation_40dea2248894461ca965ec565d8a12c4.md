### Title
`collectFees` surplus computation includes `notionalFeeToken0/1Scaled` in the spread-fee base, causing double-payment of notional fees and LP fund drainage — (`metric-core/contracts/MetricOmmPool.sol`)

---

### Summary

When both spread fees and notional fees are active, `collectFees` computes the spread-fee surplus as `actual_balance × scale − binTotals.scaledToken0`. Because `notionalFeeToken0Scaled` is held in the pool's ERC-20 balance but is **not** reflected in `binTotals.scaledToken0`, the surplus is over-inflated by exactly the accumulated notional fee amount. The spread-fee split is then applied to this inflated surplus, and the notional fees are paid out again from `notionalFeeToken0Scaled`. The total attempted payout exceeds the actual fee surplus, and the shortfall is drawn from LP principal.

---

### Finding Description

`BinTotals` tracks only the tokens that belong to liquidity providers: [1](#0-0) 

Notional fees are accumulated in a separate pair of accumulators that live in the pool's ERC-20 balance but are **not** added to `binTotals`: [2](#0-1) 

During a swap, notional fees are credited to these accumulators and the corresponding `amount(0|1)DeltaScaled` is increased so the callback pays them into the pool, but `binTotals` is **not** updated for the notional portion: [3](#0-2) 

When `collectFees` runs, it computes the spread-fee surplus as:

```
surplus0Scaled = actual_balance × TOKEN_0_SCALE_MULTIPLIER − binTotals.scaledToken0
```

Because `notionalFeeToken0Scaled` sits in the pool's ERC-20 balance but not in `binTotals`, the surplus equals `spread_fees + notional_fees`, not just `spread_fees`. The spread-fee split is then applied to this inflated value: [4](#0-3) 

Separately, the full `notionalFeeToken0Scaled` is paid out again: [5](#0-4) 

Total attempted payout = `(spread_fees + notional_fees) + notional_fees`, but only `spread_fees + notional_fees` is available. The excess `notional_fees` is drawn from LP principal.

The protocol's own test suite explicitly documents and asserts this over-allocation: [6](#0-5) 

---

### Impact Explanation

LP principal is drained by the accumulated `notionalFeeToken0Scaled` / `notionalFeeToken1Scaled` amounts on every `collectFees` call when both `spreadFeeE6 > 0` and `notionalFeeE8 > 0`. The drained amount grows with swap volume and the notional fee rate. LPs who subsequently call `removeLiquidity` receive fewer tokens than their share entitles them to, constituting a direct loss of user principal. This matches the **pool insolvency** impact gate: balances fail to cover LP claims.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

Any pool configured with both a non-zero spread fee and a non-zero notional fee is affected on every `collectFees` invocation. The factory admin controls fee configuration and fee collection, so the trigger is a routine administrative action rather than an exotic attack path. The condition is reachable whenever the pool has accumulated notional fees (i.e., after any swap on such a pool).

---

### Recommendation

Subtract `notionalFeeToken0Scaled` and `notionalFeeToken1Scaled` from the surplus before applying the spread-fee split:

```solidity
uint256 surplus0Scaled = (IERC20(TOKEN0).balanceOf(address(this)) * TOKEN_0_SCALE_MULTIPLIER)
    - binTotals.scaledToken0
    - notionalFeeToken0Scaled;   // exclude notional fees from spread base

uint256 surplus1Scaled = (IERC20(TOKEN1).balanceOf(address(this)) * TOKEN_1_SCALE_MULTIPLIER)
    - binTotals.scaledToken1
    - notionalFeeToken1Scaled;
```

This ensures the spread-fee split operates only on the true spread-fee surplus, and the notional-fee split operates on the separately tracked notional accumulator, with no double-counting.

---

### Proof of Concept

The existing test `test_collectProtocolFees_math_overallocates_whenSpreadAndNotionalBothActive` already demonstrates the invariant break: [7](#0-6) 

```
surplus0Scaled = balance × scale − binTotals   // includes notional fees
total0Attempted = spread_split(surplus) + notional_split(notional)
               = (spread_fees + notional_fees) + notional_fees
               > surplus0Scaled                 // LP funds drained by notional_fees
```
