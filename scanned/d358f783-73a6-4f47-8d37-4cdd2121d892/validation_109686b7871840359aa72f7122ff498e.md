### Title
Attacker Can Steal Staking Rewards by Front-Running `FeeReceiver.sendFunds()` Before rsETH Price Update - (File: contracts/FeeReceiver.sol)

### Summary

`FeeReceiver.sendFunds()` is callable by any unprivileged address and moves accumulated MEV/execution-layer rewards from `FeeReceiver` into `LRTDepositPool`. Because the `FeeReceiver` balance is excluded from the rsETH price calculation, an attacker can deposit ETH at a stale (lower) price, then trigger `sendFunds()` to inflate the deposit pool's ETH balance, causing the next `updateRSETHPrice()` call to produce a higher price. The attacker then redeems rsETH at the inflated price, extracting value that belongs to pre-existing rsETH holders.

### Finding Description

**Step 1 — rsETH price does not include FeeReceiver balance.**

`LRTOracle._getTotalEthInProtocol()` calls `ILRTDepositPool.getTotalAssetDeposits(ETH_TOKEN)`, which resolves to `getETHDistributionData()`: [1](#0-0) 

`ethLyingInDepositPool` is set to `address(this).balance` — the deposit pool's own balance. The `FeeReceiver` contract's balance is never included in any of the six returned components. Rewards sitting in `FeeReceiver` are therefore invisible to the oracle.

**Step 2 — `FeeReceiver.sendFunds()` has no access control.** [2](#0-1) 

The function is `external` with no role modifier. Any EOA or contract can call it at any time, instantly moving the entire `FeeReceiver` ETH balance into `LRTDepositPool`.

**Step 3 — rsETH price is a stored state variable updated on demand.** [3](#0-2) 

`updateRSETHPrice()` is also callable by anyone (no role guard, only `whenNotPaused`). The stored `rsETHPrice` is what `LRTDepositPool.getRsETHAmountToMint()` reads at deposit time: [4](#0-3) 

**Step 4 — Deposit mints rsETH at the stale price.**

Because `rsETHPrice` is stale (does not yet reflect rewards sitting in `FeeReceiver`), a depositor receives more rsETH per ETH than they should. After `sendFunds()` is called and the price is updated, those extra rsETH tokens represent a claim on the newly added rewards.

### Impact Explanation

An attacker who deposits ETH before calling `sendFunds()` and `updateRSETHPrice()` receives rsETH minted at a price that does not include pending rewards. After the price update, the attacker's rsETH is worth more ETH than they deposited. The difference is extracted from the yield that should have accrued exclusively to pre-existing rsETH holders. This is a direct theft of unclaimed yield — **High** impact.

### Likelihood Explanation

- `FeeReceiver.sendFunds()` requires no special role and no capital beyond the deposit itself.
- MEV and execution-layer rewards accumulate continuously in `FeeReceiver`; the window is always open.
- The attacker only needs to observe the `FeeReceiver` balance on-chain, deposit, call `sendFunds()`, and wait for the withdrawal delay (~8 days) to complete.
- The `pricePercentageLimit` guard in `_updateRsETHPrice()` may slow the price update if the reward jump is large, but the attacker has already locked in the favourable entry price; they simply wait for the manager's periodic `updateRSETHPriceAsManager()` call.

Likelihood is **Medium-High**: no privileged access is required, the precondition (rewards in `FeeReceiver`) is a normal protocol state, and the attack is straightforward to execute.

### Recommendation

1. **Restrict `sendFunds()`** to a trusted role (e.g., `LRTConstants.MANAGER`) so that reward distribution cannot be timed by an attacker.
2. **Include `FeeReceiver` balance in TVL accounting** inside `getETHDistributionData()` so that pending rewards are always reflected in the rsETH price, eliminating the price-lag window entirely.
3. Alternatively, atomically call `updateRSETHPrice()` inside `sendFunds()` so the price is updated in the same transaction as the reward transfer, preventing any deposit at the stale price.

### Proof of Concept

1. Rewards accumulate in `FeeReceiver` (e.g., 10 ETH from MEV). `rsETHPrice` is stale at `1.05 ETH/rsETH` (does not include these 10 ETH).
2. Attacker calls `LRTDepositPool.depositETH{value: 100 ETH}(minRSETH, "")`. They receive `100 / 1.05 ≈ 95.24 rsETH` at the stale price.
3. Attacker calls `FeeReceiver.sendFunds()`. The deposit pool's ETH balance increases by 10 ETH.
4. Anyone (or the attacker) calls `LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice()`. The new price reflects the extra 10 ETH, rising to approximately `1.055 ETH/rsETH`.
5. After the withdrawal delay, the attacker redeems `95.24 rsETH` and receives `≈ 100.48 ETH` — more than the 100 ETH deposited. The ~0.48 ETH profit was extracted from the yield owed to pre-existing holders.
