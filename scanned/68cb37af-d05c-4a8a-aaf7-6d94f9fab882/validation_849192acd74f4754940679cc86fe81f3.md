### Title
Unbounded Gas Consumption in `depositAssetForL1Vault` Due to Unbounded NDC Loop in `getAssetDistributionData` - (File: contracts/L1Vault.sol)

---

### Summary

`L1Vault.depositAssetForL1Vault` triggers a call chain that iterates over every registered NodeDelegator (NDC) and makes multiple external calls per NDC. Because `maxNodeDelegatorLimit` has no hard ceiling and can be raised by admin as the protocol scales, the gas cost grows linearly with NDC count and can eventually exceed the block gas limit, permanently blocking LST bridging flows.

---

### Finding Description

The full call chain on every `depositAssetForL1Vault` invocation is:

```
L1Vault.depositAssetForL1Vault(token)
  → LRTDepositPool.depositAsset(token, ...)
    → _beforeDeposit(asset, ...)
      → _checkIfDepositAmountExceedesCurrentLimit(asset, amount)
        → getTotalAssetDeposits(asset)
          → getAssetDistributionData(asset)   ← O(N) loop over nodeDelegatorQueue
```

Inside `getAssetDistributionData`, for **each** NDC in `nodeDelegatorQueue`: [1](#0-0) 

Three external calls are made per NDC:
1. `IERC20(asset).balanceOf(nodeDelegatorQueue[i])` — ERC-20 external call
2. `INodeDelegator(nodeDelegatorQueue[i]).getAssetBalance(asset)` — calls into `NodeDelegatorHelper.getAssetBalance`, which itself calls EigenLayer's `DelegationManager.getWithdrawableShares` and `IStrategy.sharesToUnderlyingView`
3. `INodeDelegator(nodeDelegatorQueue[i]).getAssetUnstaking(asset)` — calls `DelegationManager.getQueuedWithdrawals` and iterates over all queued withdrawals for that NDC [2](#0-1) 

`_beforeDeposit` is the gating check inside `depositAsset`: [3](#0-2) 

`_checkIfDepositAmountExceedesCurrentLimit` unconditionally calls `getTotalAssetDeposits`: [4](#0-3) 

The NDC queue is bounded by `maxNodeDelegatorLimit`, which is initialized to 10 but has no hard ceiling — admin can raise it arbitrarily: [5](#0-4) [6](#0-5) 

---

### Impact Explanation

Each NDC iteration costs approximately 25,000–40,000 gas (3 external calls + EigenLayer sub-calls). At 10 NDCs (default limit) this is ~250,000–400,000 gas — manageable. However, as `maxNodeDelegatorLimit` is raised and NDCs are added during normal protocol scaling:

- 30 NDCs → ~750,000–1,200,000 gas
- 100 NDCs → ~2,500,000–4,000,000 gas
- 300+ NDCs → approaches or exceeds Ethereum's 30M block gas limit

When the limit is breached, every call to `depositAssetForL1Vault` reverts out-of-gas, permanently blocking LST-to-rsETH minting from the L1Vault and halting the entire L2 bridging flow until NDCs are removed.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The default limit is 10 NDCs, which is safe today. However, `updateMaxNodeDelegatorLimit` is callable by any LRT admin with no upper bound check, and `addNodeDelegatorContractToQueue` is similarly unrestricted. As the protocol scales and operators are added, the limit will be raised through normal governance. There is no on-chain safeguard that prevents the queue from growing to a gas-breaking size. The protocol's own comment in `LRTUnstakingVault` acknowledges scaling concerns (`ndc count * asset count = 15`), confirming the NDC count is expected to grow. [7](#0-6) 

---

### Recommendation

1. **Introduce a hard cap** on `maxNodeDelegatorLimit` (e.g., 20–30) that cannot be exceeded regardless of admin action.
2. **Cache `getTotalAssetDeposits`** or restructure `_beforeDeposit` to avoid the full NDC scan on every deposit — for example, maintain a running total updated on transfers in/out rather than recomputing from scratch.
3. **Separate the limit check from the full distribution scan**: the deposit limit check only needs to know whether `totalDeposits + amount > depositLimit`, which could be maintained as a storage variable updated incrementally.
