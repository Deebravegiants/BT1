### Title
Synthetix-Style Reward Rounding Allows Any Caller to Permanently Destroy Unclaimed Yield for Small Stakers - (File: contracts/KERNEL/KernelDepositPool.sol)

---

### Summary

`KernelDepositPool` implements a Synthetix-style staking reward mechanism. The `updateReward` modifier, triggered by every call to `getReward()`, `stake()`, and `initiateWithdrawal()`, advances the global `updatedAt` timestamp to `block.timestamp`. When the elapsed time between two such calls is small, the `rewardPerToken()` increment truncates to zero due to integer division. The rewards that should have accrued during that interval are permanently destroyed — they are never credited to any user. Any unprivileged caller can exploit this by calling `getReward()` every block, causing small stakers to permanently lose their yield.

---

### Finding Description

The `rewardPerToken()` function computes the per-token reward increment as:

```solidity
// KernelDepositPool.sol line 412
return rewardPerTokenStored + (rewardRate * (lastTimeRewardApplicable() - updatedAt) * DECIMAL_PRECISION)
    / totalKernelStaked;
```

The `updateReward` modifier, applied to `getReward()`, `stake()`, and `initiateWithdrawal()`, unconditionally writes:

```solidity
// KernelDepositPool.sol lines 233-234
rewardPerTokenStored = rewardPerToken();
updatedAt = lastTimeRewardApplicable();
```

When `(lastTimeRewardApplicable() - updatedAt)` is small (e.g., one block = 12 seconds on mainnet), the numerator `rewardRate * timeDelta * 1e18` can be less than `totalKernelStaked`, causing the division to truncate to zero. In that case:

- `rewardPerTokenStored` does not increase.
- `updatedAt` advances to `block.timestamp`.
- The `rewardRate * timeDelta` reward tokens that should have been distributed are permanently lost — they are never credited to `rewardPerTokenStored` and cannot be recovered in future calls.

An attacker calls `getReward()` once per block. Each call resets `updatedAt` to the current timestamp. The attacker need not be a staker at all: if `rewards[attacker] == 0`, the function still executes the `updateReward` modifier and destroys the accrued-but-unrecorded rewards for that block interval.

A large staker acting as the attacker has an additional incentive: their own `earned()` = `largeBalance * delta / 1e18` does not round to zero (large balance compensates for small delta), so they collect their own rewards normally while small stakers' `earned()` = `smallBalance * delta / 1e18` rounds to zero every block.

---

### Impact Explanation

**Impact: Medium — Permanent freezing of unclaimed yield.**

Rewards that should have been distributed during each griefed block interval are permanently destroyed. They are not deferred or delayed; the `updatedAt` pointer advances past them without recording the corresponding increase in `rewardPerTokenStored`. Small stakers with low `balanceOf` receive zero rewards indefinitely. The total reward tokens locked in the contract exceed what can ever be claimed, creating a permanent shortfall.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**Likelihood: Medium.**

- `getReward()` is a public, permissionless function with no access control.
- The attacker requires no stake, no special role, and no prior relationship with the protocol.
- A large staker is economically incentivized to call `getReward()` every block to maximize their own reward accumulation, inadvertently (or deliberately) destroying small stakers' yield.
- On Ethereum mainnet, gas costs are non-trivial but the attacker can automate one call per block (~12 s). If the reward pool is large, the economic gain from denying competitors' yield can outweigh gas costs.
- The condition `rewardRate * 12 * 1e18 < totalKernelStaked` is realistic: e.g., `rewardRate = 1e6` (small reward rate) and `totalKernelStaked = 1e25` (10 million KERNEL tokens) gives a numerator of `1.2e25` vs denominator `1e25`, which truncates to 1 — but for even smaller `rewardRate` or larger `totalKernelStaked`, the increment is 0.

---

### Recommendation

1. **Accumulate dust before advancing `updatedAt`**: Only advance `updatedAt` when `rewardPerToken()` actually increases (i.e., when the computed increment is non-zero). If the increment is zero, do not update `updatedAt`, so the elapsed time carries forward to the next call.

2. **Alternatively, use a higher-precision accumulator**: Scale `rewardPerTokenStored` by a larger factor (e.g., `1e36` instead of `1e18`) to reduce the likelihood of truncation.

3. **Add a minimum time gate**: Require that `block.timestamp - updatedAt >= MIN_UPDATE_INTERVAL` before allowing `updateReward` to advance the timestamp, preventing per-block griefing.

### Proof of Concept

**Preconditions:**
- `rewardRate` is set such that `rewardRate * 12 * 1e18 < totalKernelStaked` (one block = 12 s).
- Alice has a small KERNEL stake; Eve has a large stake or no stake.

**Attack steps:**

1. Admin calls `notifyRewardAmount(amount)` — sets `rewardRate`, `finishAt`, `updatedAt = block.timestamp`.
2. Alice calls `stake(smallAmount)` — Alice is now a staker.
3. Eve calls `getReward()` at block N. `updateReward` fires:
   - `rewardPerToken()` increment = `rewardRate * 12 * 1e18 / totalKernelStaked` → truncates to 0.
   - `rewardPerTokenStored` unchanged.
   - `updatedAt` = block N timestamp. Rewards for block N interval are permanently lost.
4. Alice calls `getReward()` at block N+1. `updateReward` fires:
   - `rewardPerToken()` increment = `rewardRate * 12 * 1e18 / totalKernelStaked` → truncates to 0 again.
   - `earned(alice)` = `smallBalance * 0 / 1e18 + 0` = 0.
   - Alice receives 0 rewards.
5. Eve repeats step 3 every block indefinitely. Alice never accumulates any rewards.
