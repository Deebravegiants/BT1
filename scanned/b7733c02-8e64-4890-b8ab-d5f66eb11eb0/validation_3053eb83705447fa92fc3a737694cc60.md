### Title
Permissionless `updateRate()` with No Rate Validation Allows Broadcasting Transient Oracle Dips to All Cross-Chain Receivers — (`contracts/agETH/AGETHMultiChainRateProvider.sol`)

### Summary

`MultiChainRateProvider.updateRate()` is callable by any address with no access control and performs zero validation on the value returned by `agETHPriceOracle.getRate()`. An unprivileged attacker can time a call to coincide with a transient oracle dip and permanently commit that low rate to all registered `AGETHRateReceiver` contracts across every destination chain until the next legitimate update.

### Finding Description

`AGETHMultiChainRateProvider.getLatestRate()` delegates unconditionally to the external oracle: [1](#0-0) 

`MultiChainRateProvider.updateRate()` is `external payable` with only a `nonReentrant` guard — no `onlyOwner`, no role check, no rate-bounds check, no deviation limit, no TWAP: [2](#0-1) 

Whatever `getLatestRate()` returns at the moment of the call is immediately written to `rate` and fanned out to every receiver via LayerZero: [3](#0-2) 

There is no minimum-rate floor, no maximum-deviation guard, and no cooldown between calls. Any caller who supplies enough ETH for LayerZero fees can invoke this at any block.

### Impact Explanation

Cross-chain `AGETHRateReceiver` contracts on destination chains will store the attacker-supplied depressed rate and serve it to any consuming protocol until the next `updateRate()` call overwrites it. During that window, any protocol on a destination chain that prices agETH using the receiver's stored rate will underprice agETH, potentially allowing depositors to receive more agETH than the correct rate warrants. No funds are directly stolen from the provider contract itself, matching the **Low** scope: *contract fails to deliver promised returns, but doesn't lose value*.

Note: `AGETHPoolV3` on mainnet reads its own `agETHOracle` directly and is **not** affected by the cross-chain receiver rate. [4](#0-3) 

The impact is confined to destination-chain consumers of `AGETHRateReceiver`.

### Likelihood Explanation

- The attacker needs only ETH for LayerZero fees — no privileged role required.
- Oracle transient dips are a known occurrence during multi-block update windows for any on-chain rate provider.
- The attacker can monitor the oracle off-chain and submit the transaction in the same block as the dip; no oracle compromise is required.
- The cost of the attack is bounded by LayerZero messaging fees.

### Recommendation

1. Add an `onlyOwner` (or a dedicated `RATE_UPDATER_ROLE`) guard to `updateRate()` in `MultiChainRateProvider`, or
2. Enforce a maximum downward deviation limit: revert if `latestRate < rate * (BPS_BASE - MAX_DEVIATION_BPS) / BPS_BASE`, or
3. Enforce a minimum update interval so the rate cannot be pushed faster than the oracle's own update cadence.

The root cause is at: [5](#0-4) 

— no caller restriction and no rate sanity check before committing and broadcasting.
