### Title
Withdrawers Forfeit Yield Accrued During the Queue Delay Period — (File: contracts/LRTWithdrawalManager.sol)

### Summary
When a user calls `initiateWithdrawal()`, their rsETH is transferred to `LRTWithdrawalManager` and their payout is fixed at the rsETH price at that moment. During the mandatory withdrawal delay (default 8 days, max 16 days), the rsETH price continues to appreciate as EigenLayer restaking rewards accrue. At unlock time, `_calculatePayoutAmount()` caps the user's payout at the original `expectedAssetAmount`, so all appreciation that occurred while the rsETH was locked is forfeited by the withdrawer and redistributed to remaining rsETH holders. This is the direct analog of the reported "interest-free loan" pattern.

### Finding Description

`initiateWithdrawal()` transfers the user's rsETH to the contract and records a fixed `expectedAssetAmount` computed at the current oracle price:

```solidity
IERC20(lrtConfig.rsETH()).safeTransferFrom(msg.sender, address(this), rsETHUnstaked);
uint256 expectedAssetAmount = getExpectedAssetAmount(asset, rsETHUnstaked);
// ...
assetsCommitted[asset] += expectedAssetAmount;
_addUserWithdrawalRequest(asset, rsETHUnstaked, expectedAssetAmount);
```

The rsETH is **not burned** at this point — it sits in `LRTWithdrawalManager` and remains part of `IRSETH.totalSupply()`. `LRTOracle._updateRsETHPrice()` computes the rsETH price as:

```solidity
uint256 rsethSupply = IRSETH(rsETHTokenAddress).totalSupply();
// ...
uint256 newRsETHPrice = (totalETHInProtocol - protocolFeeInETH).divWad(rsethSupply);
```

The locked rsETH is included in `rsethSupply`, so as restaking rewards flow in and `totalETHInProtocol` grows, the rsETH price rises — but the withdrawing user's payout is already frozen.

When `unlockQueue()` is eventually called, `_calculatePayoutAmount()` enforces the cap:

```solidity
uint256 currentReturn = (request.rsETHUnstaked * rsETHPrice) / assetPrice;
return (request.expectedAssetAmount < currentReturn) ? request.expectedAssetAmount : currentReturn;
```

The full `rsETHUnstaked` is then burned:

```solidity
rsETHAmountToBurn += request.rsETHUnstaked;
// ...
if (rsETHBurned != 0) IRSETH(lrtConfig.rsETH()).burnFrom(address(this), rsETHBurned);
unstakingVault.redeem(asset, assetAmountToUnlock); // only the capped amount
```

The rsETH is burned in full, but only the original (lower) asset amount is redeemed from the vault. The surplus assets remain in `LRTUnstakingVault`, effectively increasing the asset-per-rsETH ratio for all remaining holders. The withdrawer subsidises remaining holders for the entire duration of the delay.

### Impact Explanation

**High — Theft of unclaimed yield.**

Every user who initiates a standard withdrawal loses all restaking yield that accrues on their rsETH during the delay window (8–16 days). The yield is not destroyed; it is redistributed to remaining rsETH holders. The magnitude scales with the delay length and the current restaking APY. For a 10 ETH withdrawal over an 8-day delay at a 5% annual restaking yield, the user forfeits approximately `10 × 0.05 × 8/365 ≈ 0.011 ETH` of yield with no recourse.

### Likelihood Explanation

**High.** The standard withdrawal path through `initiateWithdrawal()` → `unlockQueue()` → `completeWithdrawal()` is the primary exit mechanism for all rsETH holders. The rsETH price appreciates continuously as EigenLayer rewards accrue. Every queued withdrawal is affected; no special conditions are required.

### Recommendation

1. **Burn rsETH at initiation time** rather than at unlock time. This removes the locked rsETH from `totalSupply()` immediately, so the price appreciation is not diluted by tokens that are already committed to exit.
2. Alternatively, **use the rsETH price at unlock time** (not initiation time) as the basis for the payout, so the user receives the full current value of their rsETH when the queue is processed.
3. If the cap-at-initiation design is intentional (to protect against price manipulation), document it explicitly and consider compensating withdrawers with a separate yield-accrual mechanism during the delay.

### Proof of Concept

1. rsETH price = 1.05 ETH/rsETH. User calls `initiateWithdrawal(stETH, 100e18)`.
   - rsETH transferred to `LRTWithdrawalManager`: 100 rsETH.
   - `expectedAssetAmount` = `100 × 1.05 / 1.0` = 105 stETH (recorded and committed).
2. 8 days pass. EigenLayer rewards push rsETH price to 1.06 ETH/rsETH.
3. Operator calls `unlockQueue(stETH, ...)`.
   - `currentReturn` = `100 × 1.06 / 1.0` = 106 stETH.
   - `_calculatePayoutAmount` returns `min(105, 106)` = **105 stETH**.
   - 100 rsETH burned; only 105 stETH redeemed from vault.
4. User calls `completeWithdrawal(stETH)` and receives **105 stETH**.
5. The 1 stETH difference (≈ 0.95% of the withdrawal) remains in `LRTUnstakingVault`, raising the rsETH price for all remaining holders. The withdrawer provided an 8-day interest-free loan of 100 rsETH worth of restaking yield. [1](#0-0) [2](#0-1) [3](#0-2) [4](#0-3) [5](#0-4)
