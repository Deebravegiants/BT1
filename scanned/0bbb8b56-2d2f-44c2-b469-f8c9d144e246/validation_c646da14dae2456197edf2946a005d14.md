### Title
Stale `rsETHPrice` Used in Deposit and Withdrawal Calculations Allows Theft of Unclaimed Yield - (`contracts/LRTDepositPool.sol`)

---

### Summary

`LRTDepositPool.depositETH()` and `depositAsset()` compute the rsETH amount to mint using the stored `LRTOracle.rsETHPrice` state variable without first calling `updateRSETHPrice()`. Because `rsETHPrice` is only updated by an external keeper, it can be stale (lower than the true price) when rewards have accrued. A depositor who acts in this window receives more rsETH than deserved, diluting existing holders and stealing their unclaimed yield.

---

### Finding Description

`LRTOracle.rsETHPrice` is a stored state variable updated only when `updateRSETHPrice()` is explicitly called (permissionless but not automatic). [1](#0-0) 

`updateRSETHPrice()` recomputes the price as `totalETHInProtocol / rsethSupply`: [2](#0-1) 

When staking rewards accrue (EigenLayer, Lido, etc.), `totalETHInProtocol` increases but the stored `rsETHPrice` is not updated until a keeper calls `updateRSETHPrice()`. During this window, `rsETHPrice` is **stale and lower** than the true price.

`LRTDepositPool.depositETH()` and `depositAsset()` call `_beforeDeposit()` → `getRsETHAmountToMint()`, which reads the stale stored price directly: [3](#0-2) 

Neither `depositETH` nor `depositAsset` calls `updateRSETHPrice()` before computing the mint amount: [4](#0-3) 

Similarly, `LRTWithdrawalManager.initiateWithdrawal()` calls `getExpectedAssetAmount()` which reads the stale price: [5](#0-4) [6](#0-5) 

---

### Impact Explanation

**Impact: High — Theft of unclaimed yield from existing rsETH holders.**

When rewards accrue and `rsETHPrice` is stale (lower than true), a depositor receives:

```
rsethAmountToMint = (depositAmount * assetPrice) / staleLowerPrice
```

This is **more rsETH than deserved**. The excess rsETH represents a claim on the accrued rewards that belong to existing holders. When `updateRSETHPrice()` is eventually called, the new price is diluted by the extra rsETH supply, and existing holders receive less yield than they earned.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**Likelihood: High.**

- `updateRSETHPrice()` is called by off-chain keepers on a schedule, not atomically with every deposit. There is always a window between reward accrual and price update.
- `updateRSETHPrice()` is public and permissionless, but nothing prevents a depositor from front-running the keeper's update call.
- A sophisticated depositor can monitor on-chain reward accrual (e.g., EigenLayer pod balance increases, stETH rebases) and time their deposit to exploit the stale price window.
- No special privileges are required — any depositor can trigger this.

---

### Recommendation

Call `updateRSETHPrice()` (or an internal equivalent) at the beginning of `depositETH()`, `depositAsset()`, and `initiateWithdrawal()` before any share/asset amount is computed. This ensures the exchange rate is always fresh before it is used for minting or redemption calculations.

```solidity
function depositETH(uint256 minRSETHAmountExpected, string calldata referralId)
    external payable nonReentrant whenNotPaused onlySupportedAsset(LRTConstants.ETH_TOKEN)
{
    // Update price first, before computing mint amount
    ILRTOracle(lrtConfig.getContract(LRTConstants.LRT_ORACLE)).updateRSETHPrice();

    uint256 rsethAmountToMint = _beforeDeposit(LRTConstants.ETH_TOKEN, msg.value, minRSETHAmountExpected);
    _mintRsETH(rsethAmountToMint);
    emit ETHDeposit(msg.sender, msg.value, rsethAmountToMint, referralId);
}
```

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Protocol state: 1000 ETH in assets, 1000 rsETH supply → stored `rsETHPrice = 1.0 ETH/rsETH`.
2. 100 ETH in staking rewards accrue across NodeDelegators/EigenLayer pods. True price = `1100/1000 = 1.1 ETH/rsETH`. Stored price is still `1.0`.
3. Attacker calls `depositETH{value: 100 ETH}()` before the keeper calls `updateRSETHPrice()`.
4. `getRsETHAmountToMint` computes: `100e18 * 1e18 / 1.0e18 = 100 rsETH` minted to attacker.
   - Correct amount at true price: `100e18 * 1e18 / 1.1e18 ≈ 90.9 rsETH`.
   - Attacker received **~9.1 extra rsETH** worth of value.
5. Keeper calls `updateRSETHPrice()`. New supply = 1100 rsETH, total ETH = 1200 ETH. New price = `1200/1100 ≈ 1.0909 ETH/rsETH`.
6. Attacker's 100 rsETH is worth `100 * 1.0909 ≈ 109 ETH` — a profit of ~9 ETH on a 100 ETH deposit.
7. Original 1000 rsETH holders' share is diluted: they now hold 1000/1100 of 1200 ETH ≈ 1090.9 ETH instead of the 1100 ETH they earned. They lost ~9.1 ETH of yield to the attacker.
