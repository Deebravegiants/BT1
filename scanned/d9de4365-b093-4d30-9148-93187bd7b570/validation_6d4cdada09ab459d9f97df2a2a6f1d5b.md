### Title
JIT Liquidity Sandwich Steals LP Fees From Existing Bin Providers — (`metric-core/contracts/libraries/LiquidityLib.sol`, `metric-core/contracts/libraries/SwapMath.sol`)

---

### Summary

The LP fee from every swap is deposited directly into the bin's token balance and distributed pro-rata to all current shareholders at removal time. Because `addLiquidity` and `removeLiquidity` carry no lockup period, cooldown, or removal fee, an MEV actor can sandwich any large swap: add shares to the target bin before the swap, let the swap inflate the bin balance with LP fees, then immediately remove shares and pocket a proportional cut of fees that should have accrued entirely to pre-existing LPs.

---

### Finding Description

**How LP fees enter bin balances**

In every `SwapMath` swap-in-bin function the LP fee (total fee minus the protocol spread cut) is added directly to the bin's token balance. For example, in `buyToken0InBinSpecifiedOut`:

```
feeAmountScaled      = ceil(amountInScaled × currBinBuyFeeX64 / ONE_X64)
amountInScaled      += feeAmountScaled
protocolFeeScaled    = feeAmountScaled × spreadFeeE6 / 1e6

binState.token1BalanceScaled +=  amountInScaled − protocolFeeScaled   // LP fee stays in bin
binLpFeeAmount               =  feeAmountScaled − protocolFeeScaled
``` [1](#0-0) 

The same pattern holds in `buyToken1InBinSpecifiedOut`, `buyToken0InBinSpecifiedIn`, and `buyToken1InBinSpecifiedIn`. [2](#0-1) 

**How removal distributes the accumulated balance**

`LiquidityLib.removeLiquidity` computes each token's payout as a simple pro-rata of the current bin balance:

```solidity
uint256 amount0Scaled = _checkedMul(binState.token0BalanceScaled, sharesToRemove) / binTotalSharesVal;
uint256 amount1Scaled = _checkedMul(binState.token1BalanceScaled, sharesToRemove) / binTotalSharesVal;
``` [3](#0-2) 

Any LP fee that accumulated after a new LP joined is therefore shared with that new LP, diluting the original providers.

**No lockup or removal fee**

`addLiquidity` and `removeLiquidity` impose no minimum holding period, no withdrawal fee, and no cooldown: [4](#0-3) 

**The sandwich**

1. Attacker observes a large pending swap that will traverse bin `B`.
2. Attacker front-runs: calls `addLiquidity` for bin `B`, paying tokens proportional to the *current* (pre-fee) bin balance.
3. Swap executes: LP fee is deposited into bin `B`'s balance.
4. Attacker back-runs: calls `removeLiquidity`, receiving `shares / totalShares × (originalBalance + lpFee)`.

The attacker paid for a share of `originalBalance` but receives a share of `originalBalance + lpFee`, extracting LP fees that should belong entirely to pre-existing providers.

---

### Impact Explanation

Existing LPs suffer a direct, quantifiable loss of earned LP fees on every sandwiched swap. The attacker's profit equals `lpFee × attackerShares / totalSharesAfterAdd`. For a bin with 1 000 shares and an attacker who adds 1 000 shares, the attacker captures exactly half the LP fee generated by the swap. The original LP's fee income is halved with no recourse. Repeated across many swaps or bins, this constitutes a sustained drain of LP revenue.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The attack is viable for any MEV searcher with mempool visibility. Critically, the oracle-mediated pricing eliminates price risk: the swap executes at the oracle mid-price regardless of bin state, so the attacker's round-trip (add → swap → remove) carries no directional exposure. The only cost is gas. For swaps generating LP fees above the gas threshold the attack is unconditionally profitable and can be automated.

---

### Recommendation

Implement at least one of the following mitigations:

1. **Minimum holding period**: Record the block number at which each position's shares were last increased; disallow `removeLiquidity` within N blocks of the most recent `addLiquidity` for that position.
2. **Withdrawal fee on short-term liquidity**: Charge a fee proportional to the LP fee earned if liquidity is removed within a configurable window.
3. **Fee-checkpoint accounting**: Track a per-bin `feeGrowthGlobal` accumulator (similar to Uniswap v3's `feeGrowthInside`). Credit LP fees only to shares that existed *before* the swap, so newly minted shares start with a zero fee checkpoint and cannot claim historical fees.

Option 3 is the most robust and eliminates the attack entirely without restricting liquidity timing.

### Proof of Concept

**Setup**

- Bin 4 (above current price, token0-only): `token0BalanceScaled = 1 000`, `binTotalShares = 1 000` (Alice holds all shares).
- Oracle price: 1 token0 = 1 token1.
- LP fee rate: 2 % total; protocol takes 50 %, so LP net fee = 1 %.

**Step 1 — Attacker front-runs**

Bob calls `addLiquidity` for bin 4 with 1 000 shares.  
Payment (proportional): `1 000 × 1 000 / 1 000 = 1 000 token0`.  
Bin state: `token0 = 2 000`, `shares = 2 000`.

**Step 2 — Swap executes**

A trader buys 200 token0 from bin 4, paying token1.  
- Net token1 in (oracle price): 200  
- Total fee: 200 × 2 % = 4 token1  
- Protocol fee: 4 × 50 % = 2 token1  
- LP fee to bin: 2 token1  
- Bin state: `token0 = 1 800`, `token1 = 202`, `shares = 2 000`

(The `202` comes from the 200 net input plus the 2 token1 LP fee deposited into the bin.)

**Step 3 — Attacker back-runs**

Bob calls `removeLiquidity` for 1 000 shares.  
- token0 received: `1 800 × 1 000 / 2 000 = 900`  
- token1 received: `202 × 1 000 / 2 000 = 101`  
- Bob paid 1 000 token0, received 900 token0 + 101 token1.  
- At oracle price 1:1: **net profit = 1 token1** (the LP fee portion).

**Alice's loss**

Without Bob, Alice would have received `1 800 token0 + 202 token1` (net gain of 2 token1 LP fee).  
With Bob, Alice receives `900 token0 + 101 token1` (net gain of 1 token1 LP fee).  
**Alice loses 1 token1** — exactly the amount Bob stole.
