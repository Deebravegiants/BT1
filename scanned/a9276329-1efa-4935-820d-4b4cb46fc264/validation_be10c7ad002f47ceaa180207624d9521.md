### Title
Stale `rsETHPrice` Snapshot Allows Depositors to Mint Excess rsETH at Expense of Existing Holders — (`contracts/LRTOracle.sol`)

---

### Summary

`LRTOracle` stores `rsETHPrice` as a periodically-updated snapshot. Because `updateRSETHPrice()` is never called atomically before minting in `LRTDepositPool`, any depositor can exploit the window between price updates — after staking yield has accrued but before the price is refreshed — to receive more rsETH than their ETH contribution warrants, directly stealing unclaimed yield from existing holders.

---

### Finding Description

`LRTOracle._updateRsETHPrice()` computes the fee baseline as:

```solidity
uint256 previousTVL = rsethSupply.mulWad(rsETHPrice);
// ...
uint256 rewardAmount = totalETHInProtocol - previousTVL;
protocolFeeInETH = (rewardAmount * lrtConfig.protocolFeeInBPS()) / 10_000;
uint256 newRsETHPrice = (totalETHInProtocol - protocolFeeInETH).divWad(rsethSupply);
```

`rsETHPrice` here is the **stored value from the last call** to `updateRSETHPrice()` — a snapshot. Between updates, EigenLayer staking rewards cause `totalETHInProtocol` to grow while `rsETHPrice` remains fixed at the old, lower value.

`LRTDepositPool.getRsETHAmountToMint()` uses this stale price directly to calculate how many shares to mint:

```solidity
rsethAmountToMint = (amount * lrtOracle.getAssetPrice(asset)) / lrtOracle.rsETHPrice();
```

There is no call to `updateRSETHPrice()` anywhere in the deposit path before this division. A depositor who acts during the staleness window receives rsETH calculated at the old, lower price — i.e., more shares than their ETH contribution warrants. The excess shares dilute all existing holders' positions, transferring their accrued-but-not-yet-reflected yield to the late depositor.

This is structurally identical to the Anchor governance finding: a total is snapshotted (`rsETHPrice` ↔ `a_poll.staked_amount`), participants act after the snapshot (`deposit` ↔ `stake`), and the protocol uses the current balance against the stale snapshot total, giving the late actor disproportionate benefit.

---

### Impact Explanation

**High — Theft of unclaimed yield.**

Existing rsETH holders lose a portion of their accrued staking yield proportional to the size of the late deposit and the magnitude of yield earned since the last price update. The attacker's gain is exactly the existing holders' loss: the attacker receives rsETH shares that represent more ETH than they deposited, permanently diluting the redemption value of all pre-existing rsETH.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**Medium.**

EigenLayer staking rewards accrue continuously, so the staleness window always exists between price updates. `updateRSETHPrice()` is a public function called off-chain (e.g., by a keeper bot), not on every deposit. Any unprivileged depositor can monitor on-chain TVL growth (via `LRTDepositPool.getTotalAssetDeposits`) versus the stored `rsETHPrice`, identify when the gap is profitable, and deposit before the next price update. No special access is required.

---

### Recommendation

Call `updateRSETHPrice()` (or an equivalent internal price refresh) at the start of `LRTDepositPool.depositAsset()` before computing `getRsETHAmountToMint()`. This ensures the exchange rate used for minting always reflects the current TVL, eliminating the staleness window.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. **Initial state**: 1 000 ETH in protocol, 1 000 rsETH supply, `rsETHPrice = 1.000 ETH`. [1](#0-0) 

2. **Yield accrues**: EigenLayer rewards add 10 ETH → `totalETHInProtocol = 1 010 ETH`. `rsETHPrice` is **not yet updated** (still `1.000 ETH`). Actual fair price = `1 010 / 1 000 = 1.010 ETH/rsETH`.

3. **Attacker deposits 100 ETH** via `LRTDepositPool.depositAsset()`. `getRsETHAmountToMint()` computes: `100 ETH / 1.000 ETH = 100 rsETH`. Fair amount would be `100 / 1.010 ≈ 99.01 rsETH`. Attacker receives **~0.99 rsETH excess**. [2](#0-1) 

4. **State after deposit**: `totalETHInProtocol = 1 110 ETH`, `rsethSupply = 1 100 rsETH`.

5. **Price update fires**:
   - `previousTVL = 1 100 × 1.000 = 1 100 ETH`
   - `rewardAmount = 1 110 − 1 100 = 10 ETH` (fee correctly charged on yield only)
   - `newRsETHPrice = (1 110 − fee) / 1 100 ≈ 1.009 ETH/rsETH` [3](#0-2) 

6. **Without the attacker**: `newRsETHPrice = (1 010 − fee) / 1 000 ≈ 1.010 ETH/rsETH`.

7. **Result**: Existing holders' rsETH is worth `~0.001 ETH/rsETH` less than it should be. The attacker's 100 rsETH redeems for `~100.9 ETH` — a `~0.9 ETH` profit extracted from existing holders' unclaimed yield. The attack scales linearly with deposit size and the yield gap since the last update.
