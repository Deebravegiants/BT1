### Title
Retroactive `feeInBPS` Update Applied to All Pending Unclaimed Rewards at Claim Time - (File: `contracts/utils/MerkleDistributor/MerkleDistributor.sol`)

---

### Summary

`MerkleDistributor.setFeeInBPS()` updates `feeInBPS` without first checkpointing (collecting) pending rewards. Because `claim()` computes the fee using the **current** `feeInBPS` at the moment of claiming, any increase to `feeInBPS` is retroactively applied to all rewards that accrued since a user's last claim — including rewards earned under a lower fee regime. The same flaw exists identically in `KernelMerkleDistributor.sol` and `KernelTop100MerkleDistributor.sol`.

---

### Finding Description

`MerkleDistributor.claim()` computes the claimable amount as the delta between the new cumulative amount and the previously recorded cumulative amount:

```solidity
uint256 claimableAmount = cumulativeAmount - userClaims[account].cumulativeAmount;
```

It then deducts the fee using the **live** `feeInBPS` value:

```solidity
uint256 fee = (claimableAmount * feeInBPS) / 10_000;
uint256 amountToSend = claimableAmount - fee;
``` [1](#0-0) 

The owner can update `feeInBPS` at any time via `setFeeInBPS()`, with no prior collection of pending rewards:

```solidity
function setFeeInBPS(uint256 _feeInBPS) external onlyOwner {
    if (_feeInBPS > MAX_FEE_IN_BPS) {
        revert InvalidFeeInBPS();
    }
    feeInBPS = _feeInBPS;
    emit FeeInBPSUpdated(_feeInBPS);
}
``` [2](#0-1) 

Because `claimableAmount` can represent rewards accumulated over many merkle root epochs (each `setMerkleRoot()` call advances `currentIndex`), a fee rate increase immediately and silently applies to the entire backlog of unclaimed rewards — not just rewards earned after the rate change.

The identical pattern exists in `KernelMerkleDistributor`: [3](#0-2) 

and in `KernelTop100MerkleDistributor`: [4](#0-3) 

---

### Impact Explanation

**High — Theft of unclaimed yield.**

A fee rate increase from, say, 0% to 10% (the `MAX_FEE_IN_BPS` cap) applied to a user who has not claimed for several epochs causes the protocol treasury to receive 10% of all rewards that accrued during the zero-fee period. Users receive less than the yield they were entitled to under the fee schedule in effect when those rewards were earned. This is a direct, quantifiable loss of yield owed to reward claimants.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The owner can call `setFeeInBPS()` at any time. Even a routine, non-malicious fee adjustment (e.g., raising the fee from 0 to 5%) will retroactively apply to every user who has not yet claimed. Given that merkle-based distributors are typically claimed infrequently (users batch claims across many epochs), the window of exposure is large. No special conditions, front-running, or external dependencies are required.

---

### Recommendation

Mirror the fix recommended for M-5: before updating `feeInBPS`, force-collect (checkpoint) all pending fees so that the new rate only applies to rewards accrued after the update. In the merkle distributor context this means snapshotting the current `currentIndex` as a "fee-rate epoch boundary" and storing the fee rate that was active for each epoch, then applying the correct per-epoch rate when computing `fee` during `claim()`. At minimum, emit a clear event with the old and new rate and document that users should claim before a fee increase takes effect.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Protocol deploys `MerkleDistributor` with `feeInBPS = 0`.
2. Owner calls `setMerkleRoot()` three times over three months, each time increasing users' cumulative allocations. Alice accumulates 1,000 tokens of claimable rewards across these epochs.
3. Owner calls `setFeeInBPS(1000)` (10%) — no checkpoint of pending rewards occurs.
4. Alice calls `claim()`. `claimableAmount = 1000`. Fee = `1000 * 1000 / 10_000 = 100` tokens sent to treasury. Alice receives 900 instead of 1,000.
5. All 100 tokens were earned under a 0% fee regime but are silently redirected to the treasury due to the retroactive rate change. [5](#0-4)
