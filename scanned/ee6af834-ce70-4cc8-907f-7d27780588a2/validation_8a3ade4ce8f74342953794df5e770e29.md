### Title
`collectFees` Double-Counts Notional Fees Against LP Principal When Both Spread and Notional Fees Are Active — (`metric-core/contracts/MetricOmmPool.sol`)

---

### Summary

`collectFees` computes spread-fee payouts from the entire token surplus (`balance * scaleMultiplier − binTotals`), then pays notional fees on top from a separate accumulator. Because notional fees are collected from traders into the pool balance but are **not** credited to `binTotals`, they appear inside the surplus. The function therefore pays out `surplus + notionalFees`, but only `surplus` worth of tokens is available as fees. The shortfall is silently drawn from LP principal stored in `binTotals`.

---

### Finding Description

**Notional fee accounting during swap**

During `_executeSwap`, bin totals are updated first (lines 732–748), then the notional fee is appended to `amount1DeltaScaled` (lines 750–772): [1](#0-0) [2](#0-1) 

Because `binTotals.scaledToken1` is written **before** `notionalFeeScaled` is added to `amount1DeltaScaled`, the notional fee amount is:
- **Included** in the actual ERC-20 balance (the trader pays it), and
- **Excluded** from `binTotals`.

This means `surplus = balance × scaleMultiplier − binTotals` absorbs the full notional fee accumulation.

**Over-allocation in `collectFees`** [3](#0-2) 

The spread-fee split uses `adminSpreadFeeE6 / spreadSumE6` and `protocolSpreadFeeE6 / spreadSumE6` where `spreadSumE6 = adminSpreadFeeE6 + protocolSpreadFeeE6`. Their sum equals 1, so the total spread payout equals the entire `surplus`. Notional fees are then paid **additionally** from `notionalFeeToken0Scaled` / `notionalFeeToken1Scaled`: [4](#0-3) 

Total attempted payout = `surplus + notionalFees`. Available fee balance = `surplus`. The deficit equals the full notional fee accumulation, which is drawn from LP principal.

**The codebase's own test confirms this** [5](#0-4) 

```
assertGt(total0Attempted, surplus0Scaled, "token0 attempted payout exceeds computed surplus");
assertGt(total1Attempted, surplus1Scaled, "token1 attempted payout exceeds computed surplus");
```

The test documents the over-allocation but does not prevent it from executing.

---

### Impact Explanation

**High.** When both `spreadFeeE6 > 0` and `notionalFeeE8 > 0`, every call to `collectFees` transfers `notionalFeeToken0Scaled + notionalFeeToken1Scaled` worth of tokens out of LP principal. LPs who subsequently call `removeLiquidity` receive fewer tokens than their shares entitle them to, up to the full notional fee accumulation since the last `collectFees` call. With high swap volume and a non-trivial notional fee rate, this constitutes a direct, measurable loss of LP principal.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**High.** Both fee types are independently configurable by the factory/admin. Any pool where an operator enables both a spread fee and a notional fee is immediately vulnerable. No special attacker action is required; the loss occurs automatically on every `collectFees` invocation.

---

### Recommendation

Before computing spread-fee payouts, subtract the notional fee accumulation from the surplus so that the two fee streams do not overlap:

```solidity
uint256 spreadSurplus0Scaled = surplus0Scaled > notionalFee0AmountScaled
    ? surplus0Scaled - notionalFee0AmountScaled : 0;
uint256 spreadSurplus1Scaled = surplus1Scaled > notionalFee1AmountScaled
    ? surplus1Scaled - notionalFee1AmountScaled : 0;

uint256 spreadFee0ToAdminScaled = spreadSumE6 == 0 ? 0
    : (spreadSurplus0Scaled * adminSpreadFeeE6) / spreadSumE6;
// … use spreadSurplus* throughout
```

Alternatively, credit notional fees into `binTotals` at collection time so the surplus already excludes them, keeping the two accounting paths consistent.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Deploy a pool with `spreadFeeE6 = 500_000` (50 % admin + 50 % protocol) and `notionalFeeE8 = 1_000_000` (1 %).
2. LP deposits 100 000 scaled units of token1.
3. Trader executes an exact-in swap that generates `notionalFeeToken1Scaled = 1 000`.
4. `surplus1Scaled = balance1 × mul − binTotals.scaledToken1 = 1 000` (the notional fee sits in surplus).
5. `collectFees` is called:
   - `spreadFee1ToAdmin = 1 000 × 500 000 / 1 000 000 = 500`
   - `spreadFee1ToProtocol = 1 000 × 500 000 / 1 000 000 = 500`
   - `notionalFee1ToProtocol = 1 000`
   - Total payout = 2 000 scaled units.
6. Pool balance only holds 1 000 scaled units of surplus; the extra 1 000 is taken from LP principal.
7. LP calls `removeLiquidity` and receives 1 000 fewer scaled token1 units than their shares entitle them to — a direct loss of principal equal to the notional fee accumulation. [6](#0-5)
