### Title
Stale `rsETHPrice` Allows Depositors to Mint Excess rsETH at the Expense of Existing Holders - (File: contracts/LRTOracle.sol, contracts/LRTDepositPool.sol)

---

### Summary

`LRTOracle.rsETHPrice` is a stored state variable updated periodically via `updateRSETHPrice()`. Between updates it is stale. When EigenLayer staking rewards accrue, `totalETHInProtocol` rises but `rsETHPrice` does not. Any depositor can call `LRTDepositPool.depositETH()` or `depositAsset()` at the stale (lower) price, receiving more rsETH than their deposit warrants. After the price update, the attacker's rsETH is worth more ETH than they deposited, diluting the yield owed to pre-existing rsETH holders.

---

### Finding Description

`LRTOracle` stores the rsETH/ETH exchange rate in a mutable state variable: [1](#0-0) 

This variable is updated by calling `updateRSETHPrice()`, which is **publicly callable** with no access restriction: [2](#0-1) 

The price is computed from on-chain data inside `_updateRsETHPrice()`: [3](#0-2) 

Between calls to `updateRSETHPrice()`, `rsETHPrice` is stale. `LRTDepositPool.getRsETHAmountToMint()` reads this stored value directly: [4](#0-3) 

Both `depositETH()` and `depositAsset()` call `_beforeDeposit()` → `getRsETHAmountToMint()`, so every deposit is priced against the stale rate: [5](#0-4) [6](#0-5) 

When EigenLayer validator rewards accrue, `_getTotalEthInProtocol()` returns a higher value than the last stored price reflects: [7](#0-6) 

A depositor who acts before `updateRSETHPrice()` is called receives more rsETH than their ETH is worth at the true (post-reward) rate. After the price update, their rsETH is redeemable for more ETH than they deposited, at the direct expense of pre-existing holders whose accrued yield is diluted.

---

### Impact Explanation

**High — Theft of unclaimed yield.**

Pre-existing rsETH holders have accrued staking rewards that are reflected in `totalETHInProtocol` but not yet in `rsETHPrice`. A late depositor who mints at the stale price captures a proportional share of those rewards without having been staked during the accrual period. The yield stolen equals:

```
stolen_yield ≈ attacker_deposit × (true_rate - stale_rate) / stale_rate
```

This is not a rounding error; it scales linearly with deposit size and with the magnitude of the stale gap. The loss is permanent: once the price is updated and the attacker's rsETH is redeemable at the higher rate, the original holders' share of the TVL is irreversibly diluted.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**Medium.**

EigenLayer staking rewards accrue continuously (every epoch/block). `updateRSETHPrice()` is called off-chain by a keeper or any public caller, meaning there is always a non-zero window between reward accrual and price update. An attacker monitoring EigenLayer pod balances on-chain can detect when `totalETHInProtocol` has grown beyond the value implied by the stored `rsETHPrice` and deposit immediately. Because `updateRSETHPrice()` is itself publicly callable, the attacker can also deposit first and then call the update themselves (provided the price delta is within `pricePercentageLimit`), removing any dependency on front-running a specific transaction. [8](#0-7) 

The `pricePercentageLimit` guard limits the per-update price jump but does not eliminate the attack; it merely caps the per-transaction profit. Repeated small deposits across multiple update cycles compound the theft.

---

### Recommendation

1. **Dynamic price computation at deposit time**: Replace the stored `rsETHPrice` read in `getRsETHAmountToMint()` with an inline call to `_getTotalEthInProtocol() / rsETH.totalSupply()`, so the minting rate always reflects the current TVL. This mirrors Recommendation 1 from the external report.

2. **Atomic price update on deposit**: Call `_updateRsETHPrice()` at the start of every `depositETH()` / `depositAsset()` execution so the price is always fresh before minting.

3. **Minimum holding period**: Require rsETH to be held for at least one oracle update cycle before it can be redeemed, preventing same-block or same-epoch deposit-and-exit.

---

### Proof of Concept

Assume `pricePercentageLimit` is set to 5% (1% = 1e16), no fees, and the withdrawal manager processes requests at the current `rsETHPrice`.

**Step 1 — Baseline**
- `totalETHInProtocol` = 1 000 ETH, `rsETH.totalSupply()` = 1 000, `rsETHPrice` = 1.000 ETH/rsETH.

**Step 2 — Rewards accrue**
- EigenLayer distributes 10 ETH of validator rewards; `totalETHInProtocol` rises to 1 010 ETH.
- `rsETHPrice` is still 1.000 (stale).

**Step 3 — Attacker deposits**
- Attacker calls `depositETH{value: 1000 ETH}()`.
- `getRsETHAmountToMint` computes `1000 × 1e18 / 1.000e18 = 1 000 rsETH`.
- Attacker receives 1 000 rsETH. True fair amount would be `1000 / 1.010 ≈ 990.1 rsETH`.
- New state: `totalETHInProtocol` = 2 010 ETH, `rsETH.totalSupply()` = 2 000.

**Step 4 — Price update**
- Anyone (including the attacker) calls `updateRSETHPrice()`.
- `newRsETHPrice = 2010 / 2000 = 1.005 ETH/rsETH`.

**Step 5 — Attacker withdraws**
- Attacker redeems 1 000 rsETH at 1.005 → receives **1 005 ETH** (profit: 5 ETH).

**Step 6 — Original holders lose yield**
- Original 1 000 rsETH holders redeem at 1.005 → receive 1 005 ETH.
- They should have received 1 010 ETH (the full 10 ETH reward). They lost **5 ETH** of yield to the attacker.

The attacker captured 50% of the accrued rewards despite holding rsETH for effectively zero time, with no risk beyond gas costs and the withdrawal delay.
