### Title
Unbounded Gas Consumption in Public `updateRSETHPrice()` via Nested External-Call Loops in `getAssetUnstaking()` - (File: `contracts/NodeDelegator.sol`)

---

### Summary

The public `LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice()` function triggers a chain of nested loops that call `NodeDelegator.getAssetUnstaking()` once per NDC per supported asset. `getAssetUnstaking()` fetches all queued EigenLayer withdrawals via an external call and then iterates over them with a nested loop that makes additional external calls per strategy. The total gas cost grows multiplicatively with `supportedAssets.length × nodeDelegatorQueue.length × queuedWithdrawals × strategies`, and has no per-call gas guard. Under normal protocol operation with a full NDC queue and active unstaking, this public entry point can exhaust the block gas limit.

---

### Finding Description

**Call chain:**

1. `LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice()` — `public`, only guarded by `whenNotPaused`
2. → `_getTotalEthInProtocol()` — loops over every supported asset
3. → `ILRTDepositPool.getTotalAssetDeposits(asset)` — called once per asset
4. → `LRTDepositPool.getAssetDistributionData(asset)` / `getETHDistributionData()` — loops over every NDC in `nodeDelegatorQueue`
5. → `INodeDelegator(ndc).getAssetUnstaking(asset)` — called once per NDC per asset
6. → `_getDelegationManager().getQueuedWithdrawals(address(this))` — **external call** returning all queued EigenLayer withdrawals for that NDC
7. → nested `for` loop over `queuedWithdrawals.length` × `withdrawal.strategies.length`, calling `strategy.underlyingToken()` and `strategy.sharesToUnderlyingView()` — **two external calls per strategy per withdrawal**

**Gas cost estimate (worst-case, within protocol limits):**

| Parameter | Max value |
|---|---|
| Supported assets | ~5 |
| NDCs (`maxNodeDelegatorLimit`) | 10 |
| Total queued withdrawals (`maxUncompletedWithdrawalCount` ≤ 80) | 80 (concentrated in one NDC) |
| Strategies per withdrawal | 2–5 |

Total `getAssetUnstaking()` invocations: `5 assets × 10 NDCs = 50`
External calls per invocation (worst NDC): `1 (getQueuedWithdrawals) + 80 × 2 × 2 (underlyingToken + sharesToUnderlyingView) = 321`
Total external calls: `50 × 321 ≈ 16,050`
At ~2,100 gas per cold external call: **~33.7M gas** — exceeding Ethereum's 30M block gas limit.

Even at warm-storage prices (~100 gas), the memory allocation and ABI decoding of large `Withdrawal[]` arrays adds substantial overhead.

---

### Impact Explanation

If `updateRSETHPrice()` reverts due to out-of-gas, the stored `rsETHPrice` becomes stale. All price-dependent paths — `initiateWithdrawal`, `instantWithdrawal`, `depositAsset`, `depositETH`, `unlockQueue` — read `rsETHPrice` directly. A stale price causes incorrect rsETH minting amounts and incorrect withdrawal payouts, constituting a temporary freeze of correct protocol operation. The privileged fallback `updateRSETHPriceAsManager()` exists but requires manager intervention; the public path is permanently broken until the queued withdrawal count drops.

**Impact: Medium — Unbounded gas consumption / Temporary freezing of funds.**

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The protocol's own normal operation (operators calling `initiateUnstaking()` and `undelegate()`) fills the queued withdrawal slots. `maxUncompletedWithdrawalCount` is capped at 80 and `maxNodeDelegatorLimit` at 10. With multiple supported assets, the multiplicative gas cost is reached under ordinary high-activity conditions — no attacker action is required. Any unprivileged caller invoking `updateRSETHPrice()` when the protocol is near capacity will trigger the OOG revert. [5](#0-4) [6](#0-5) 

---

### Recommendation

1. **Cache `getQueuedWithdrawals()` once per NDC** and compute all asset amounts in a single pass, rather than calling `getAssetUnstaking()` separately for each supported asset.
2. **Decouple `getAssetUnstaking()` from the price-update path**: store a running `assetUnstaking` tally updated on `initiateUnstaking` / `completeUnstaking` events, similar to how `stakedButUnverifiedNativeETH` is tracked, and read that stored value in `getAssetDistributionData()`.
3. **Add a gas guard** in `updateRSETHPrice()` (e.g., `require(gasleft() > MIN_GAS_RESERVE)`) or split the price update into a two-step commit/reveal pattern.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Deploy protocol with 5 supported assets, 10 NDCs, `maxUncompletedWithdrawalCount = 80`.
2. Operator calls `initiateUnstaking()` 80 times across NDCs, filling the withdrawal queue.
3. Any unprivileged EOA calls `updateRSETHPrice()`.
4. Execution path: `updateRSETHPrice` → `_getTotalEthInProtocol` → 5 × `getTotalAssetDeposits` → 5 × 10 × `getAssetUnstaking` → 50 × `getQueuedWithdrawals` (external) + nested strategy loops with external calls per strategy.
5. Transaction reverts with out-of-gas before `rsETHPrice` is updated.
6. All subsequent calls to `initiateWithdrawal`, `depositAsset`, and `unlockQueue` operate on a stale price until a manager manually calls `updateRSETHPriceAsManager()`.
