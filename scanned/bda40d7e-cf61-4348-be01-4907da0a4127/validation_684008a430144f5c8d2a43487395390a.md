### Title
JIT Deposit Attack Allows Theft of MEV/EL Yield from Existing rsETH Holders - (File: contracts/LRTOracle.sol, contracts/FeeReceiver.sol, contracts/LRTDepositPool.sol)

### Summary
An attacker can atomically deposit ETH at the stale stored `rsETHPrice`, trigger MEV/EL reward ingestion via the permissionless `FeeReceiver.sendFunds()`, and then call the permissionless `LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice()` to capture a disproportionate share of the reward increment — stealing yield that should have accrued exclusively to pre-existing rsETH holders.

### Finding Description
The rsETH exchange rate is a share price: `rsETHPrice = totalETHInProtocol / rsethSupply`. It is only updated when `updateRSETHPrice()` is called. Between updates, MEV and execution-layer rewards accumulate in `FeeReceiver`. The critical permissionless surface is:

1. **`LRTDepositPool.depositETH()`** — permissionless, mints rsETH at the *stored* (stale) `rsETHPrice` via `getRsETHAmountToMint()`. [1](#0-0) [2](#0-1) 

2. **`FeeReceiver.sendFunds()`** — permissionless, pushes all accumulated ETH into the deposit pool, immediately increasing `totalETHInProtocol`. [3](#0-2) 

3. **`LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice()`** — permissionless, recomputes the price from the now-inflated TVL. [4](#0-3) 

The price update logic computes the reward as `totalETHInProtocol - previousTVL` and distributes it proportionally across *all* rsETH holders at the moment of the call — including any rsETH minted in the same block at the old price. [5](#0-4) 

### Impact Explanation
**High — Theft of unclaimed yield.**

Existing rsETH holders earn MEV/EL rewards that accumulate in `FeeReceiver`. When an attacker front-runs the reward ingestion with a large deposit at the stale price, they dilute the reward distribution. The attacker's rsETH is minted at the pre-reward price and immediately appreciates when the price is updated, capturing a fraction of the reward proportional to their injected share. Existing holders receive correspondingly less yield than they are entitled to.

### Likelihood Explanation
**Medium.** The attack requires capital (or a flash loan) but is fully permissionless and can be executed atomically in a single transaction. `FeeReceiver` accumulates ETH continuously from validator MEV and execution-layer rewards, so the opportunity recurs with every reward cycle. The attacker can monitor the `FeeReceiver` balance on-chain and execute when the accumulated reward is large enough to be profitable after gas costs. The `pricePercentageLimit` guard provides partial mitigation only when set to a non-zero value and only when the price jump exceeds the threshold — for typical small reward increments relative to TVL, the guard does not trigger. [6](#0-5) 

### Recommendation
1. **Snapshot-based deposit pricing**: Compute the rsETH mint amount using the *current* TVL at deposit time (i.e., call `_getTotalEthInProtocol()` inline) rather than the stored stale `rsETHPrice`. This ensures a depositor cannot benefit from a price that does not yet reflect pending rewards.
2. **Access-control `sendFunds()`**: Restrict `FeeReceiver.sendFunds()` to a trusted keeper or the manager role so that reward ingestion cannot be triggered atomically by an attacker.
3. **Deposit fee or minimum holding period**: Introduce a small entry fee or a time-weighted exit mechanism to make same-block round-trips unprofitable.

### Proof of Concept
```
Block N (single transaction via a contract):

1. FeeReceiver holds 200 ETH in accumulated MEV rewards.
   Stored rsETHPrice = 1.05 ETH.
   totalETHInProtocol = 10,000 ETH, rsethSupply = 9,523.81 rsETH.
   previousTVL = 9,523.81 × 1.05 = 10,000 ETH.

2. Attacker calls LRTDepositPool.depositETH{value: 5000 ETH}(0, "").
   rsETH minted = 5000 / 1.05 = 4,761.90 rsETH (at stale price).
   New rsethSupply = 14,285.71 rsETH.
   New totalETHInProtocol (before reward) = 15,000 ETH.

3. Attacker calls FeeReceiver.sendFunds().
   200 ETH transferred to DepositPool.
   totalETHInProtocol = 15,200 ETH.

4. Attacker calls LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice().
   previousTVL = 14,285.71 × 1.05 = 15,000 ETH.
   rewardAmount = 15,200 - 15,000 = 200 ETH.
   protocolFeeInETH = 200 × protocolFeeInBPS / 10000 (assume 10% → 20 ETH).
   newRsETHPrice = (15,200 - 20) / 14,285.71 = 15,180 / 14,285.71 ≈ 1.06258 ETH.

5. Attacker's 4,761.90 rsETH is now worth 4,761.90 × 1.06258 ≈ 5,059.8 ETH.
   Attacker profit ≈ 59.8 ETH on a 5,000 ETH deposit.
   Legitimate holders' share of the 180 ETH net reward (after protocol fee):
     - Pre-existing holders (9,523.81 rsETH) receive: 9,523.81/14,285.71 × 180 ≈ 120 ETH
     - Attacker (4,761.90 rsETH) receives: 4,761.90/14,285.71 × 180 ≈ 60 ETH
   Without the attack, pre-existing holders would have received the full 180 ETH.
   Stolen yield = ~60 ETH.
```

The attacker can exit after EigenLayer's withdrawal delay, retaining the yield differential. The attack is repeatable every reward cycle.
