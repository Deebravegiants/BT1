### Title
Unguarded `FeeReceiver.sendFunds()` Enables Yield Theft via Frontrunning Reward Distribution — (`contracts/FeeReceiver.sol`)

---

### Summary

`FeeReceiver.sendFunds()` has no access control, allowing any caller to trigger the transfer of accumulated MEV/execution-layer rewards into `LRTDepositPool` at will. Combined with the publicly callable `LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice()`, an attacker can atomically: deposit ETH to mint rsETH at the current (pre-reward) price, force reward distribution, update the rsETH price upward, and immediately exit via `instantWithdrawal()` — capturing a disproportionate share of rewards that belong to long-term rsETH holders.

---

### Finding Description

**`FeeReceiver.sendFunds()` — no access control:** [1](#0-0) 

The function is `external` with no role check. Any EOA or contract can call it at any time, forcing all accumulated ETH in `FeeReceiver` into `LRTDepositPool` via `receiveFromRewardReceiver()`.

**`LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice()` — publicly callable:** [2](#0-1) 

Any caller (when not paused) can trigger a price update. The new price is computed as: [3](#0-2) 

When `FeeReceiver.sendFunds()` has just pushed rewards into the deposit pool, `totalETHInProtocol` increases while `rsethSupply` is unchanged, so `newRsETHPrice` rises.

**`LRTWithdrawalManager.instantWithdrawal()` — uses live price at call time:** [4](#0-3) 

`getExpectedAssetAmount` reads `lrtOracle.rsETHPrice()` at the moment of the call: [5](#0-4) 

So if the price has just been pumped by forced reward distribution, the attacker redeems rsETH at the inflated price.

**Standard withdrawal path also exploitable (secondary):**

Even without instant withdrawals, an attacker can call `initiateWithdrawal()` immediately after forcing the price update. The `expectedAssetAmount` is locked at the inflated price P2: [6](#0-5) 

At `unlockQueue()` time, `_calculatePayoutAmount` returns `min(expectedAssetAmount, currentReturn)`: [7](#0-6) 

If the price remains at or above P2 after the 8-day delay, the attacker receives the full inflated amount.

---

### Impact Explanation

**Impact: High — Theft of unclaimed yield.**

Honest rsETH holders who have staked for extended periods have their MEV/execution-layer reward share diluted. The attacker mints rsETH at price P1, forces rewards in (raising price to P2), and exits at P2, extracting `rsETH_amount × (P2 − P1)` ETH in profit. This is yield that should have accrued proportionally to all existing rsETH holders. The attack can be repeated every time rewards accumulate in `FeeReceiver`.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**Likelihood: Medium.**

- `FeeReceiver.sendFunds()` is unconditionally callable by anyone with no capital requirement beyond the deposit itself.
- `LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice()` is also unconditionally callable.
- The instant withdrawal path requires `isInstantWithdrawalEnabled[asset] == true` (manager-set), which is a realistic production configuration for user convenience.
- The standard withdrawal path (8-day delay) requires capital lockup but is still profitable when reward amounts are large relative to gas costs.
- MEV rewards accumulate predictably and are visible on-chain, making the timing of the attack straightforward.
- No flash loan is needed; the attacker only needs enough capital to make the reward dilution profitable.

---

### Recommendation

1. **Restrict `FeeReceiver.sendFunds()`** to a privileged role (e.g., `MANAGER` or `LRT_OPERATOR`), preventing arbitrary callers from controlling the timing of reward ingestion:

```solidity
function sendFunds() external onlyRole(LRTConstants.MANAGER) {
    ...
}
```

2. **Restrict `LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice()`** to a privileged role, or at minimum add a cooldown so it cannot be called immediately after a deposit in the same block.

3. **Consider a deposit timelock** before rsETH can be used for withdrawal initiation, analogous to the NFTX report's recommendation. Even a short delay (e.g., 1–2 days) would prevent same-block or next-block exploitation.

4. **For instant withdrawals**, consider snapshotting the rsETH price at deposit time and using `min(depositPrice, currentPrice)` for the payout, preventing price-pump extraction.

---

### Proof of Concept

**Setup**: `FeeReceiver` holds 10 ETH in accumulated MEV rewards. Current rsETH price P1 = 1.05 ETH/rsETH. Total protocol TVL = 1000 ETH, rsETH supply = ~952 rsETH. Instant withdrawals are enabled for ETH.

**Attack**:

1. Attacker calls `LRTDepositPool.depositETH{value: 1000 ETH}(...)` → mints ~952 rsETH at price P1 = 1.05. [8](#0-7) 

2. Attacker calls `FeeReceiver.sendFunds()` → 10 ETH flows into `LRTDepositPool`, TVL becomes 2010 ETH. [1](#0-0) 

3. Attacker calls `LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice()` → new price P2 = 2010 / 1904 ≈ 1.0556 ETH/rsETH. [2](#0-1) 

4. Attacker calls `LRTWithdrawalManager.instantWithdrawal(ETH, 952 rsETH, ...)` → receives 952 × 1.0556 ≈ 1004.9 ETH. [9](#0-8) 

5. **Result**: Attacker deposited 1000 ETH, withdrew ~1004.9 ETH — extracting ~4.9 ETH of the 10 ETH reward that should have been distributed to the original ~952 rsETH holders. The original holders' share of the reward is cut roughly in half.
