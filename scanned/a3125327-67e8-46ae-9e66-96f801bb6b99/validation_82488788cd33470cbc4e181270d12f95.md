### Title
Withdrawal Completion Blocked When Protocol Is Paused, Temporarily Freezing User rsETH - (File: contracts/LRTWithdrawalManager.sol)

### Summary
`completeWithdrawal()` in `LRTWithdrawalManager` carries a `whenNotPaused` modifier. A user who has already called `initiateWithdrawal()` — transferring their rsETH into the contract — cannot recover their assets while the contract is paused. The protocol can be paused either manually by the `PAUSER_ROLE` or automatically by the oracle's downside-protection logic in `LRTOracle._updateRsETHPrice()`. During any pause, rsETH already committed to the withdrawal queue is temporarily frozen.

### Finding Description

`initiateWithdrawal()` transfers the user's rsETH into `LRTWithdrawalManager` at the moment of the call: [1](#0-0) 

After that transfer the user holds no rsETH; the contract holds it on their behalf. The only way to recover value is to call `completeWithdrawal()`: [2](#0-1) 

That function is gated by `whenNotPaused`. The same gate applies to `instantWithdrawal()`: [3](#0-2) 

The contract can be paused in two ways:

1. **Manual pause** — any address holding `PAUSER_ROLE` calls `pause()`: [4](#0-3) 

2. **Automatic oracle-triggered pause** — `LRTOracle._updateRsETHPrice()` pauses both `LRTDepositPool` and `LRTWithdrawalManager` whenever the computed rsETH price falls more than `pricePercentageLimit` below `highestRsethPrice`: [5](#0-4) 

`updateRSETHPrice()` is a public, permissionless function: [6](#0-5) 

So any external caller can trigger the price update that causes the auto-pause. Once paused, `completeWithdrawal()` reverts for every user, regardless of whether their withdrawal request has already been unlocked and the delay has passed.

### Impact Explanation

Users who have already called `initiateWithdrawal()` have irrevocably transferred their rsETH to `LRTWithdrawalManager`. While the contract is paused they cannot call `completeWithdrawal()` or `instantWithdrawal()` to recover any value. Their funds are temporarily frozen for the entire duration of the pause. The `unlockQueue()` operator function is also gated by `whenNotPaused`, so even the unlock step is blocked: [7](#0-6) 

**Impact: Medium — Temporary freezing of funds.**

### Likelihood Explanation

The automatic pause path is reachable by any unprivileged caller via the public `updateRSETHPrice()`. A slashing event on EigenLayer, a large redemption that reduces TVL, or any other event that causes the rsETH price to drop beyond `pricePercentageLimit` relative to `highestRsethPrice` will trigger the auto-pause. The manual pause path is additionally available to any `PAUSER_ROLE` holder. Both paths are realistic during normal protocol operation (e.g., maintenance windows, security incidents, or market stress).

### Recommendation

Remove `whenNotPaused` from `completeWithdrawal()` (and optionally `instantWithdrawal()`), since these functions only release assets that users have already deposited — they do not introduce new risk to the protocol. Alternatively, introduce a separate, narrower pause flag that blocks new deposits and new withdrawal initiations but leaves completion of already-queued withdrawals unaffected.

### Proof of Concept

1. Alice calls `initiateWithdrawal(asset, rsETHAmount, ...)`. Her rsETH is transferred to `LRTWithdrawalManager` (line 166). A `WithdrawalRequest` is recorded.
2. An operator calls `unlockQueue(...)` — or a price drop triggers the auto-pause before this step.
3. The oracle's `updateRSETHPrice()` is called (by anyone). The computed price is more than `pricePercentageLimit` below `highestRsethPrice`. `LRTOracle._updateRsETHPrice()` calls `withdrawalManager.pause()` (line 279 of `LRTOracle.sol`).
4. Alice calls `completeWithdrawal(asset, ...)`. The call reverts at the `whenNotPaused` modifier (line 183 of `LRTWithdrawalManager.sol`).
5. Alice's rsETH remains locked in `LRTWithdrawalManager` for the entire duration of the pause. She cannot recover it via `completeWithdrawal()` or `instantWithdrawal()`.
