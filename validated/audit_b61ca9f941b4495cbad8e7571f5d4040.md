Audit Report

## Title
Permissionless `FeeReceiver.sendFunds()` Enables Yield Theft via Deposit-Before-Reward-Distribution — (File: `contracts/FeeReceiver.sol`)

## Summary
`FeeReceiver.sendFunds()` has no access control, allowing any caller to atomically deposit at a stale rsETH price, push accumulated MEV/execution-layer rewards into the deposit pool, and then trigger a price update — capturing a proportional share of yield that belongs to existing rsETH holders. The three required calls (`depositETH`, `sendFunds`, `updateRSETHPrice`) are all permissionless, and the `pricePercentageLimit` guard is bypassable by sizing the deposit large enough to keep the per-token price increase below the threshold.

## Finding Description

**Root cause — no access control on `sendFunds()`:**

`FeeReceiver.sendFunds()` carries no role modifier: [1](#0-0) 

It pushes the full accumulated balance into `LRTDepositPool.receiveFromRewardReceiver()`, which is a plain payable stub: [2](#0-1) 

**Stale price used at mint time:**

`getRsETHAmountToMint()` divides by the stored `lrtOracle.rsETHPrice()`, which is only updated when `updateRSETHPrice()` is explicitly called: [3](#0-2) 

**`updateRSETHPrice()` is permissionless:** [4](#0-3) 

**TVL includes deposit pool ETH balance:**

After `sendFunds()` pushes rewards, `getETHDistributionData()` counts `address(this).balance` of the deposit pool in TVL, so the new price reflects the pushed rewards: [5](#0-4) 

**`pricePercentageLimit` guard is bypassable:**

The guard reverts only if the price increase exceeds `pricePercentageLimit` relative to `highestRsethPrice`: [6](#0-5) 

Because the price increase per token is `R / (S + X/P)`, the attacker can always choose X large enough to keep the increase below any fixed threshold, making the guard ineffective against this attack.

**Exploit flow:**
1. Observe `FeeReceiver.balance = R` (publicly visible on-chain).
2. Call `LRTDepositPool.depositETH{value: X}(0, "")` at the current stale price `P` → receive `X/P` rsETH.
3. Call `FeeReceiver.sendFunds()` → R ETH enters the deposit pool; TVL becomes `T + X + R`.
4. Call `LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice()` → new price = `(T + X + R) / (S + X/P)`.
5. Attacker's rsETH is now worth `X + R·X/(T+X)` ETH; profit = `R·X/(T+X)`.

## Impact Explanation
**High — Theft of unclaimed yield.** Existing rsETH holders lose a portion of accumulated MEV/execution-layer rewards proportional to `X/(T+X)`. With `X = T`, 50% of all pending rewards are stolen. With `X >> T`, nearly all of R is captured. The stolen amount is concrete and measurable, matching the allowed impact "High. Theft of unclaimed yield."

## Likelihood Explanation
**Medium.** No privileged role, oracle compromise, or governance action is required. All three calls (`depositETH`, `sendFunds`, `updateRSETHPrice`) are callable by any EOA or contract. The `FeeReceiver` balance is publicly visible. The only practical barriers are capital commitment and the withdrawal delay for realizing profit. MEV rewards accumulate continuously, making this a recurring opportunity. The `pricePercentageLimit` guard does not prevent the attack because the attacker controls X.

## Recommendation
1. **Add access control to `FeeReceiver.sendFunds()`** — restrict it to `MANAGER` or `OPERATOR` role so only authorized parties control the timing of reward injection into the deposit pool.
2. **Alternatively**, call `updateRSETHPrice()` atomically at the start of `depositETH()` and `depositAsset()` to ensure the price is always current before rsETH is minted, eliminating the stale-price window entirely.

## Proof of Concept

```
Initial state:
  TVL = 1000 ETH, rsETH supply = 1000, rsETHPrice = 1e18
  FeeReceiver.balance = 10 ETH

Step 1: attacker.depositETH{value: 1000 ETH}(0, "")
  rsethAmountToMint = 1000e18 * 1e18 / 1e18 = 1000 rsETH (stale price)
  TVL = 2000 ETH, rsETH supply = 2000

Step 2: attacker calls FeeReceiver.sendFunds()
  10 ETH pushed to LRTDepositPool
  TVL = 2010 ETH, rsETH supply = 2000

Step 3: attacker calls LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice()
  newRsETHPrice = 2010e18 / 2000 = 1.005e18
  (price increase = 0.5% — below any reasonable pricePercentageLimit)

Result:
  Attacker's 1000 rsETH worth 1005 ETH → profit = 5 ETH
  Existing holders' 1000 rsETH worth 1005 ETH (gained only 5 ETH instead of 10 ETH)
  → 5 ETH of yield stolen from existing holders

Foundry fork test plan:
  1. Fork mainnet/testnet at a block where FeeReceiver has accumulated balance.
  2. Assert rsETHPrice before attack.
  3. Execute steps 1–3 as a single EOA.
  4. Assert attacker rsETH value > deposited ETH.
  5. Assert existing holder rsETH value increased by less than FeeReceiver.balance.
  6. Fuzz over X to show profit scales with deposit size and always bypasses pricePercentageLimit.
```
