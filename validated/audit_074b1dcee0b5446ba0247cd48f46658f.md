### Title
Deposit Refund in Failed Meta-Transaction Inner Receipt Goes to Meta-Tx Sender (Alice) Instead of Relayer Who Paid - (`runtime/runtime/src/actions.rs`)

### Summary

In NEAR's meta-transaction (NEP-366) flow, when the relayer pays a deposit for inner actions and the inner receipt fails on the receiver's shard, the deposit refund is sent to the meta-tx sender (`Alice`, the `predecessor_id` of the inner receipt) rather than the relayer who actually paid the deposit. An unprivileged user can exploit this to drain the relayer's deposited NEAR tokens by crafting a `DelegateAction` with a large deposit that is guaranteed to fail on the receiver's shard.

### Finding Description

In `apply_delegate_action`, the inner receipt spawned from a `DelegateAction` is constructed as a `Receipt::V0` with:

- `predecessor_id: sender_id.clone()` — Alice (the meta-tx sender)
- `signer_id: action_receipt.signer_id().clone()` — the relayer (who paid) [1](#0-0) 

When this inner receipt fails on Bob's shard, `refund_unspent_gas_and_deposits` issues a deposit refund via `Receipt::new_balance_refund(receipt.balance_refund_receiver(), deposit_refund)`. [2](#0-1) 

`balance_refund_receiver()` returns `refund_to` if set, otherwise falls back to `predecessor_id`: [3](#0-2) 

Because the inner receipt is a `Receipt::V0` with `ReceiptEnum::Action` (not `ActionReceiptV2`), `refund_to` is always `None`: [4](#0-3) 

Therefore the deposit refund always goes to `predecessor_id` = Alice, not the relayer. The `refund_to` field exists in `ActionReceiptV2` but is never populated in the delegate action path.

The gas refund correctly goes to `signer_id` (the relayer): [5](#0-4) 

But the deposit refund goes to the wrong party. The protocol documentation explicitly acknowledges this: [6](#0-5) 

The code comment in `apply_delegate_action` also acknowledges it: [7](#0-6) 

### Impact Explanation

The relayer pays the deposit for inner actions in a meta transaction. If the inner receipt fails on the receiver's shard, the relayer permanently loses the deposited NEAR tokens — they are transferred to Alice's account instead. The corrupted protocol value is the `balance` field of Alice's account (incorrectly credited) and the relayer's account (not restored). This is a direct, irreversible financial loss for the relayer.

### Likelihood Explanation

Alice (an unprivileged user) can reliably engineer this:

1. She crafts a `DelegateAction` with a large deposit attached to a function call that will fail on Bob's shard (e.g., calling a non-existent method, or a contract that rejects based on state Alice controls).
2. She submits it to a relayer. The relayer may not be able to predict the failure (race condition: Alice changes state between relayer's verification and on-chain execution).
3. The inner receipt fails. Alice receives the deposit refund.

The attack requires no special privileges — only the ability to sign a `DelegateAction` and submit it to a relayer, which is the normal meta-transaction user flow.

### Recommendation

In `apply_delegate_action`, construct the inner receipt using `ReceiptEnum::ActionV2` (`ActionReceiptV2`) and set `refund_to: Some(relayer_account_id)` — where the relayer is `action_receipt.signer_id()` — so that deposit refunds from failed inner receipts are returned to the party who actually paid, not to Alice. [8](#0-7) 

### Proof of Concept

1. Relayer submits a meta-tx: `relayer → Alice → Bob`, with a `FunctionCall` deposit of 10 NEAR attached to a method that does not exist on Bob's contract.
2. On Alice's shard, `apply_delegate_action` creates an inner receipt: `predecessor_id = Alice`, `signer_id = relayer`, `deposit = 10 NEAR`.
3. On Bob's shard, the function call fails (`MethodNotFound`).
4. `refund_unspent_gas_and_deposits` issues `Receipt::new_balance_refund(receipt.balance_refund_receiver(), 10 NEAR)`.
5. `balance_refund_receiver()` returns `predecessor_id` = Alice.
6. Alice receives 10 NEAR. The relayer's 10 NEAR is permanently lost.
