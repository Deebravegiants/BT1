Audit Report

## Title
Permissionless `sendFunds()` + Stale Price Minting Enables Yield Dilution Sandwich - (`contracts/FeeReceiver.sol` / `contracts/LRTOracle.sol` / `contracts/LRTDepositPool.sol`)

## Summary

`FeeReceiver.sendFunds()` has no access control, and `LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice()` is callable by anyone. Because `LRTDepositPool` mints rsETH using the **stored** `rsETHPrice` rather than a live TVL calculation, an attacker can deposit at the pre-reward price, atomically trigger reward distribution and the price update, and capture a proportional share of MEV/EL yield that belongs to existing rsETH holders.

## Finding Description

**Root cause — three cooperating weaknesses:**

1. `FeeReceiver.sendFunds()` is fully permissionless: [1](#0-0) 

2. `LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice()` has only a `whenNotPaused` guard, no role check: [2](#0-1) 

3. Minting reads the **stored** `rsETHPrice`, not a freshly computed TVL ratio: [3](#0-2) 

**Price update mechanics:** `_updateRsETHPrice()` computes `previousTVL` as `rsethSupply × rsETHPrice` (the stale stored value), then sets the new price from the live `totalETHInProtocol`. Any ETH added to the deposit pool between the last price update and the current call is treated as new yield and reflected in the new price: [4](#0-3) 

**Exploit sequence (single block):**
1. Attacker calls `LRTDepositPool.depositETH{value: X}()` — minted at the stale price before rewards are counted.
2. Attacker calls `FeeReceiver.sendFunds()` — accumulated MEV/EL rewards move into `LRTDepositPool`, raising TVL.
3. Attacker calls `LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice()` — new price is computed over the enlarged TVL, diluting the yield share of pre-existing holders.
4. Attacker's rsETH is immediately worth more than the deposit cost; they sell on secondary markets or redeem after any withdrawal delay.

**Why the `pricePercentageLimit` guard is insufficient:**
The check is only active when `pricePercentageLimit > 0`. It defaults to `0`, which disables it entirely: [5](#0-4) 

Even when set, the attacker can target smaller reward accumulations that stay within the threshold, or split across multiple blocks.

## Impact Explanation

**High — Theft of unclaimed yield.** Existing rsETH holders accumulate MEV/EL rewards over time. An attacker who deposits just before triggering `sendFunds()` + `updateRSETHPrice()` captures a pro-rata share of those rewards proportional to their deposit size relative to total supply, despite having contributed zero time to the protocol. The attacker's rsETH is immediately worth more than the ETH deposited; the shortfall is borne by legitimate holders.

## Likelihood Explanation

**Medium.** The attack requires no privileged access, no flash loan (own capital suffices), and no victim mistakes. The attacker only needs to monitor `FeeReceiver.balance` on-chain (trivially observable) and call two public functions. The only friction is the `pricePercentageLimit` guard, which is disabled by default and bypassable by targeting sub-threshold reward accumulations. The attack is repeatable every time rewards accumulate.

## Recommendation

1. **Restrict `FeeReceiver.sendFunds()`** to `MANAGER` or `OPERATOR` role so the timing of reward distribution cannot be controlled by an attacker: [1](#0-0) 

2. **Alternatively**, call `_updateRsETHPrice()` atomically inside `depositETH`/`depositAsset` before computing the mint amount, so every deposit uses a fresh TVL-based price rather than the stale stored value: [6](#0-5) 

3. Ensure `pricePercentageLimit` is set to a non-zero value at deployment and document it as a required configuration, not an optional one: [7](#0-6) 

## Proof of Concept

**State:** `FeeReceiver` holds 10 ETH in accumulated rewards. TVL = 1000 ETH, rsETH supply = 1000, stored `rsETHPrice` = 1.0 ETH/rsETH. `pricePercentageLimit = 0` (default).

```
// Step 1: deposit at stale price
LRTDepositPool.depositETH{value: 1000 ETH}("") 
  → mints 1000 rsETH (1000 / 1.0), supply = 2000, TVL = 2000 ETH

// Step 2: push rewards into pool
FeeReceiver.sendFunds()
  → 10 ETH transferred to LRTDepositPool, TVL = 2010 ETH

// Step 3: update price
LRTOracle.updateRSETHPrice()
  → previousTVL = 2000 * 1.0 = 2000 ETH
  → rewardAmount = 10 ETH, protocolFee (10%) = 1 ETH
  → newRsETHPrice = (2010 - 1) / 2000 = 1.00450 ETH/rsETH

// Result
Attacker's 1000 rsETH = 1004.5 ETH  (paid 1000 ETH, gained 4.5 ETH)
Existing holders' 1000 rsETH = 1004.5 ETH  (should have received full 9 ETH net yield)
```

**Foundry fork test plan:** Deploy against a mainnet fork with a funded `FeeReceiver`. Assert that `attacker.rsETHBalance * newPrice > attacker.depositedETH` and that `existingHolder.rsETHBalance * newPrice < existingHolder.rsETHBalance * expectedFullYieldPrice` after the three-call sequence in a single block.
