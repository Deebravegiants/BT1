Audit Report

## Title
Unbounded Nested Loop in `LRTOracle._getTotalEthInProtocol()` Causes Unbounded Gas Consumption — (File: contracts/LRTOracle.sol)

## Summary

`LRTOracle._getTotalEthInProtocol()` iterates over every supported asset and, for each asset, delegates to `LRTDepositPool.getAssetDistributionData()`, which itself loops over the entire `nodeDelegatorQueue` issuing three external calls per NDC per asset. The ETH path adds another three external calls per NDC. With no gas guard, no pagination, and no enforced cap on the product `supportedAssets × NDCs`, the gas cost of every `updateRSETHPrice()` invocation grows proportionally with protocol scale until it permanently exceeds the block gas limit.

## Finding Description

`_getTotalEthInProtocol()` fetches the full supported-asset list and loops over it, calling `getTotalAssetDeposits(asset)` for each entry: [1](#0-0) 

`getTotalAssetDeposits` delegates to `getAssetDistributionData`, which loops over the entire `nodeDelegatorQueue` and issues three external calls per NDC per LST asset (`balanceOf`, `getAssetBalance`, `getAssetUnstaking`): [2](#0-1) 

For the ETH token path, `getETHDistributionData` adds another three external calls per NDC (`balance`, `getEffectivePodShares`, `getAssetUnstaking`): [3](#0-2) 

The result is roughly `3 × assets × NDCs + 3 × NDCs` external calls per `updateRSETHPrice()` invocation. `updateRSETHPrice()` is declared `public` with only a `whenNotPaused` guard — no gas check, no pagination: [4](#0-3) 

`maxNodeDelegatorLimit` starts at 10 but is freely raisable by the admin with no upper bound: [5](#0-4) 

`addNewSupportedAsset` pushes to `supportedAssetList` with no cap on list length: [6](#0-5) 

There is no mechanism that prevents the product `supportedAssets.length × nodeDelegatorQueue.length` from growing past the point where the resulting call graph exceeds the 30 M-gas block ceiling.

## Impact Explanation

The concrete allowed impact is **Medium — Unbounded gas consumption**. Once the gas cost of `_getTotalEthInProtocol()` exceeds the block gas limit, both `updateRSETHPrice()` and `updateRSETHPriceAsManager()` revert on every attempt, permanently freezing the rsETH price oracle. Secondary consequences include permanent freezing of unclaimed yield (fee-minting path inside `_updateRsETHPrice()` is never reached) and disabling of the price-drop protection circuit (`isPriceDecreaseOffLimit` can never fire). [7](#0-6) [8](#0-7) 

## Likelihood Explanation

The trigger is normal, expected protocol growth: adding more LST assets and increasing `maxNodeDelegatorLimit` to accommodate more restaking capacity. No malicious actor is required — only the admin exercising routine governance. At 10 assets × 20 NDCs the call count is ~600 external calls (~1.3 M gas cold); at 20 assets × 30 NDCs it is ~1,800 calls (~3.8 M gas cold), and EigenLayer strategy call overhead compounds this. Because there is no safeguard preventing the product from growing past the block gas limit, the risk is latent and worsens with every new asset or NDC added. Likelihood is **Medium**.

## Recommendation

1. **Cache the NDC queue once** outside the asset loop so `getNodeDelegatorQueue()` is not re-fetched per asset.
2. **Separate the ETH and LST accounting loops** so each can be called independently and paginated.
3. **Enforce a hard cap** on `maxNodeDelegatorLimit × supportedAssetList.length` in `addNewSupportedAsset` and `updateMaxNodeDelegatorLimit` that keeps the worst-case gas well below the block limit.
4. **Add a gas-remaining guard** at the top of `_getTotalEthInProtocol()` (e.g., `require(gasleft() > MIN_GAS_RESERVE)`) so the function fails fast with a clear error rather than silently reverting mid-loop.

## Proof of Concept

1. Admin calls `addNewSupportedAsset` 15 times, bringing `supportedAssetList.length` to 17 (including the two genesis assets stETH and ethX).
2. Admin calls `updateMaxNodeDelegatorLimit(25)` and then `addNodeDelegatorContractToQueue` with 25 NDC addresses.
3. Any caller invokes `updateRSETHPrice()`.
4. `_getTotalEthInProtocol()` executes `17 × 25 × 3 = 1,275` cold external calls for LST assets ≈ 2.7 M gas, plus `25 × 3 = 75` ETH-path calls ≈ 158 k gas, plus loop overhead, storage reads, and EigenLayer strategy execution — total approaches or exceeds 30 M gas.
5. The transaction reverts out-of-gas.
6. Every subsequent call to `updateRSETHPrice()` or `updateRSETHPriceAsManager()` also reverts.
7. `rsETHPrice` is permanently frozen at its last stored value; fee minting and price-drop protection are permanently disabled. [1](#0-0) [9](#0-8)

    function _getTotalEthInProtocol() private view returns (uint256 totalETHInProtocol) {
        address lrtDepositPoolAddr = lrtConfig.getContract(LRTConstants.LRT_DEPOSIT_POOL);
        address[] memory supportedAssets = lrtConfig.getSupportedAssetList();
        uint256 supportedAssetCount = supportedAssets.length;

        for (uint16 assetIdx; assetIdx < supportedAssetCount;) {
            address asset = supportedAssets[assetIdx];
            // assetER is in 1e18 precision (1.0 = 1e18)

            totalETHInProtocol += totalAssetAmt.mulWad(assetER);

            unchecked {
                ++assetIdx;
            }
        }
    }
