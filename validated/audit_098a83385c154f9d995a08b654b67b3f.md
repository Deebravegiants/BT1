### Title
Token 2022 Transfer Fee Extension Creates Undercollateralized Vault in `init_transfer` — (`solana/programs/bridge_token_factory/src/instructions/user/init_transfer.rs`)

---

### Summary

`init_transfer` calls `transfer_checked(payload.amount)` on a Token 2022 mint with a transfer fee extension, but the vault only receives `payload.amount - fee` in transferable balance. The Wormhole message is then posted encoding the full `payload.amount`. This breaks the invariant that vault holdings equal the amount serialized in the cross-chain message, permanently locking the fee difference in the vault as withheld tokens.

---

### Finding Description

**Entrypoint:** `InitTransfer::process` in `init_transfer.rs`

**Native path (vault exists):** [1](#0-0) 

`transfer_checked` is called with `payload.amount` as the transfer amount. Under the Token 2022 transfer fee extension, the SPL token program deducts `fee = min(amount * fee_rate / 10_000, max_fee)` from the credited amount at the destination. The vault's **transferable balance** becomes `payload.amount - fee`; the `fee` is placed in the vault account's `withheld_amount` field, which is inaccessible to the bridge (only the fee authority can harvest it via `harvest_withheld_tokens_to_mint`).

Immediately after, the Wormhole message is posted encoding the **original** `payload.amount`: [2](#0-1) 

There is no post-transfer balance check, no query of the transfer fee extension, and no adjustment of the encoded amount to reflect actual vault receipt.

**Vault creation path (`log_metadata`)** creates the vault PDA for any Token 2022 mint with no check for the transfer fee extension: [3](#0-2) 

**`finalize_transfer` (Solana-side redemption)** attempts to release the full `data.amount` from the vault: [4](#0-3) 

If the vault is undercollateralized, this `transfer_checked` call will fail (insufficient transferable balance), permanently blocking redemption of the fee shortfall.

---

### Impact Explanation

For every `init_transfer` on a Token 2022 mint with a transfer fee:

- NEAR credits `payload.amount` tokens to the recipient.
- The Solana vault holds only `payload.amount - fee` transferable tokens.
- The `fee` tokens are withheld in the vault and can only be recovered by the external fee authority — not the bridge.
- Any attempt to `finalize_transfer` back to Solana for the full credited amount will revert.
- Across multiple users, the vault becomes progressively undercollateralized: after N transfers of 100 tokens each with a 5-token fee, NEAR has 100N tokens credited but the vault can only service 95N tokens. The last 5N tokens on NEAR are permanently unclaimable.

This is **irreversible fund lock**: the withheld tokens cannot be recovered by the bridge, and the NEAR-side surplus cannot be redeemed.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

- Token 2022 mints with transfer fee extensions are a standard, widely-deployed feature (e.g., many protocol and governance tokens use them).
- Any unprivileged user can call `log_metadata` to register such a mint and create the vault PDA, then call `init_transfer`.
- No special role, key, or external compromise is required.
- The vulnerability triggers on every single `init_transfer` call for an affected mint.

---

### Recommendation

After `transfer_checked`, read the vault's actual post-transfer balance (or compute the fee via the extension's `calculate_fee` helper) and use the **actual received amount** when constructing the Wormhole payload. Alternatively, reject Token 2022 mints that have a transfer fee extension enabled during `log_metadata` vault creation by inspecting the mint's extensions via `StateWithExtensions::get_extension::<TransferFeeConfig>()`.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Deploy a Token 2022 mint with `TransferFeeConfig`: `fee_rate = 500` (5%), `max_fee = u64::MAX`.
2. Call `log_metadata` to register the mint and create the vault PDA.
3. Call `init_transfer` with `payload.amount = 100`.
4. Observe: vault transferable balance = 95; vault `withheld_amount` = 5.
5. Observe: Wormhole message encodes `amount = 100`.
6. On NEAR, the recipient is credited 100 tokens.
7. Attempt `finalize_transfer` back to Solana for 100 tokens → transaction reverts (vault has only 95 transferable).
8. The 5-token shortfall is permanently locked; the NEAR-side 5 tokens are unclaimable.
