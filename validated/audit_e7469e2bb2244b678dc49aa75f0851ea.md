### Title
Direct Deposits Not Included in Preservation-of-Value Balance Equation, Enabling ADA Creation from Nothing — (`File: eras/dijkstra/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Dijkstra/UTxO.hs`)

---

### Summary

The Dijkstra era introduces a new `directDeposits` field in the transaction body that allows a transaction to credit ADA directly into registered stake accounts. However, the `dijkstraProducedValue` function — which computes the "produced" side of the preservation-of-value (PoV) balance equation — does not include the sum of direct deposits. As a result, the ledger's `consumed == produced` check passes without requiring the transaction to "pay" for the ADA it deposits into accounts. An unprivileged transaction sender can include arbitrarily large direct deposits and have them applied to their own registered accounts at zero cost, creating ADA from nothing.

---

### Finding Description

The Cardano Ledger enforces a **preservation-of-value** invariant on every transaction: the total value consumed must equal the total value produced. In the Dijkstra era this check is performed in `dijkstraUtxoTransition`:

```
{- consumed pp utxo₀ txb = produced pp certState txb -}
runTest $ Shelley.validateValueNotConservedUTxO pp originalUtxo certState txBody
``` [1](#0-0) 

The **produced** side for a Dijkstra top-level transaction is computed by `dijkstraProducedValue`:

```haskell
dijkstraProducedValue pp isRegPoolId txBody =
  conwayProducedValue pp isRegPoolId txBody          -- outputs + fee + cert deposits + treasury donation
    <> foldMap'
      (getProducedValue pp isRegPoolId . view bodyTxL)
      (txBody ^. subTransactionsTxBodyL)             -- sub-tx produced values
``` [2](#0-1) 

The **consumed** side is computed by `getConsumedDijkstraValue`, which delegates to `getConsumedMaryValue`:

```haskell
consumedValue =
  sumUTxO (txInsFilter utxo (txBody ^. inputsTxBodyL))
    <> inject (refunds <> withdrawals)
``` [3](#0-2) 

Neither side includes `directDepositsTxBodyL`. The `dtbrDirectDeposits` field is present in the raw transaction body: [4](#0-3) 

Yet it is never added to the produced value. Meanwhile, the `ENTITIES` rule unconditionally applies direct deposits to account balances:

```haskell
let directDeposits = tx ^. bodyTxL . directDepositsTxBodyL
    accountsAfterCerts = certStateAfterCerts ^. certDStateL . accountsL
failOnJust (directDepositsMissingAccounts directDeposits accountsAfterCerts) $
  injectFailure . DirectDepositsToMissingAccounts

pure $ certStateAfterCerts & certDStateL . accountsL %~ applyDirectDeposits directDeposits
``` [5](#0-4) 

`applyDirectDeposits` adds the specified coin to each targeted account balance:

```haskell
applyDirectDeposits (DirectDeposits dd) =
  updateAccountBalances
    (\amount account -> addCompactCoin amount (account ^. balanceAccountStateL))
    dd
``` [6](#0-5) 

The same omission exists in the sub-transaction path via `dijkstraSubEntitiesTransition`: [7](#0-6)

### Impact Explanation

**Critical — Direct creation of ADA through an invalid ledger state transition.**

An attacker can craft a transaction where:

- **Inputs**: 100 ADA
- **Outputs**: 99 ADA
- **Fee**: 1 ADA
- **Direct deposits**: 1,000,000 ADA → attacker's own registered account

The balance equation evaluates to `100 = 99 + 1 = 100` ✓ and the transaction is accepted. The 1,000,000 ADA is credited to the attacker's account without being deducted from any UTxO or pot. The attacker then withdraws the balance via a standard withdrawal transaction.

This violates the **Full Preservation of ADA** property: `Circulation + Deposits + Fees + Treasury + Rewards + Reserves` is no longer constant after the transition. [8](#0-7) 

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**High.** The entry path requires only:
1. Registering a stake credential (standard, permissionless operation).
2. Submitting a Dijkstra-era transaction with a non-empty `directDeposits` field targeting the attacker's own account.

No privileged access, governance majority, or external dependency is required. The attack is deterministic and repeatable.

---

### Recommendation

Add the sum of all direct deposits to the **produced** side of the balance equation in `dijkstraProducedValue` (and the analogous sub-transaction function):

```haskell
dijkstraProducedValue pp isRegPoolId txBody =
  conwayProducedValue pp isRegPoolId txBody
    <> inject (fold . unDirectDeposits $ txBody ^. directDepositsTxBodyL)  -- ADD
    <> foldMap'
      (getProducedValue pp isRegPoolId . view bodyTxL)
      (txBody ^. subTransactionsTxBodyL)
```

Apply the same fix to the sub-transaction produced-value function. This ensures that any ADA credited to accounts via direct deposits must be explicitly funded by the transaction's UTxO inputs, preserving the PoV invariant.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Register stake credential `C` on-chain.
2. Construct a Dijkstra transaction:
   - Spend a UTxO of 2 ADA.
   - Output 1 ADA to any address.
   - Fee: 1 ADA.
   - `directDeposits`: `{ AccountAddress(C) → 1,000,000,000 ADA }`.
3. Submit the transaction. The `validateValueNotConservedUTxO` check passes (`2 = 1 + 1`).
4. The `ENTITIES` rule applies the direct deposit, crediting 1,000,000,000 ADA to account `C`.
5. Submit a withdrawal transaction claiming the balance of `C`.
6. 1,000,000,000 ADA has been created from nothing. [9](#0-8) [10](#0-9) [11](#0-10)

dijkstraEntitiesTransition = do
  TRC (EntitiesEnv legacyMode certsEnv, certState, certificates) <- judgmentContext
  let Conway.CertsEnv tx pp curEpoch _committee _committeeProposals = certsEnv
      withdrawals = tx ^. bodyTxL . withdrawalsTxBodyL
      accounts = certState ^. certDStateL . accountsL

  runTest $ Conway.validateWithdrawalsDelegated accounts tx

  network <- liftSTS $ asks networkId

  validateWithdrawals legacyMode network withdrawals accounts

  let certStateBeforeCerts =
        certState
          & Conway.updateDormantDRepExpiries tx curEpoch
          & Conway.updateVotingDRepExpiries tx curEpoch (pp ^. ppDRepActivityL)
          & certDStateL . accountsL %~ applyWithdrawals withdrawals
  certStateAfterCerts <-
    trans @(EraRule "CERTS" era) $ TRC (certsEnv, certStateBeforeCerts, certificates)

  let directDeposits = tx ^. bodyTxL . directDepositsTxBodyL
      accountsAfterCerts = certStateAfterCerts ^. certDStateL . accountsL
  failOnJust (directDepositsMissingAccounts directDeposits accountsAfterCerts) $
    injectFailure . DirectDepositsToMissingAccounts

  pure $ certStateAfterCerts & certDStateL . accountsL %~ applyDirectDeposits directDeposits
