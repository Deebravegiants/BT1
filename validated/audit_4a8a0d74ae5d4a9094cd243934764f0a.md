### Title
Voting Thresholds Read from Mutable `ensCurPParams` During Sequential RATIFY Processing Allows Threshold Manipulation to Enact Unauthorized Governance Actions - (File: `eras/conway/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/Rules/Ratify.hs`)

### Summary

The Conway `RATIFY` rule processes governance actions sequentially in priority order. When a `ParameterChange` action is ratified, the `ENACT` rule immediately updates `ensCurPParams` inside the live `RatifyState`. All subsequent governance actions in the same epoch are then evaluated against the **new** (attacker-chosen) voting thresholds rather than the thresholds that were in effect when those actions were submitted. Because `ParameterChange` (priority 4) is always processed before `TreasuryWithdrawals` (priority 5) and does **not** set the `rsDelayed` flag, an attacker can use a ratified `ParameterChange` to lower the `dvtTreasuryWithdrawal` threshold mid-ratification and thereby cause a `TreasuryWithdrawals` action with insufficient votes to pass in the same epoch.

### Finding Description

In `ratifyTransition`, governance actions are sorted by `reorderActions` (which calls `actionPriority`) and then consumed one at a time from the `RatifySignal` sequence:

```
ParameterChange  → priority 4
TreasuryWithdrawals → priority 5
```

When a `ParameterChange` action passes `acceptedByEveryone`, the rule calls `ENACT`, which mutates `ensCurPParams` in place:

```haskell
-- Enact.hs lines 89-92
ParameterChange _ ppup _ ->
  st
    & ensCurPParamsL %~ (`applyPPUpdates` ppup)
    & ensPrevPParamUpdateL .~ SJust (GovPurposeId govActionId)
```

The updated `EnactState` (containing the new `ensCurPParams`) is stored in `st'` and forwarded to the recursive `RATIFY` call:

```haskell
-- Ratify.hs lines 343-352
newEnactState <- trans @(EraRule "ENACT" era) $ TRC ((), rsEnactState, EnactSignal gasId govAction)
let st' = st & rsEnactStateL .~ newEnactState ...
trans @(RATIFY era) $ TRC (env, st', RatifySignal sigs)
```

The threshold functions for all subsequent actions read directly from this now-mutated state:

```haskell
-- Internal.hs lines 498-502
votingDRepThreshold ratifyState =
  toRatifyVotingThreshold . votingDRepThresholdInternal pp isElectedCommittee
  where
    pp = ratifyState ^. rsEnactStateL . ensCurPParamsL   -- reads live, updated PParams
```

Critically, `delayingAction ParameterChange {} = False`, so no `rsDelayed` guard prevents subsequent actions from being ratified in the same epoch after a `ParameterChange` is enacted.

### Impact Explanation

An attacker who can get a `ParameterChange` ratified (requiring the `dvtPPGovGroupL` threshold, which governs changes to `DRepVotingThresholds`) can lower `dvtTreasuryWithdrawal` to an arbitrarily small value. A `TreasuryWithdrawals` action submitted in the same epoch with vote support below the original threshold but above the new threshold will then be ratified in the same epoch, draining treasury funds without the governance support that was required when the action was submitted.

This constitutes an **unauthorized treasury withdrawal enacted through an invalid ledger state transition** — matching the Critical impact category: *"Unauthorized governance, treasury, protocol-parameter, committee, constitution, or hard-fork action is enacted."*

The same mechanism applies to other threshold pairs where the `ParameterChange` threshold for a governance group is lower than the threshold for another action type (e.g., lowering `dvtCommitteeNormal` to bypass the required support for `UpdateCommittee`, or lowering `dvtHardForkInitiation` to enact an unauthorized hard fork).

### Likelihood Explanation

The attack requires:
1. Submitting a `ParameterChange` action that lowers a target threshold (e.g., `dvtTreasuryWithdrawal`).
2. Accumulating enough DRep/SPO/CC votes to ratify the `ParameterChange` under the **original** `dvtPPGovGroupL` threshold.
3. Simultaneously having a `TreasuryWithdrawals` action with vote support between the new (lowered) threshold and the original threshold.

Steps 1–3 are entirely within the reach of an unprivileged on-chain actor who controls or can coordinate a sufficient fraction of DRep stake. No privileged key, supermajority, or off-chain compromise is required. The attack is deterministic and reproducible at any epoch boundary once the conditions are met.

### Recommendation

Snapshot the voting thresholds (or the full `PParams`) at the **start** of each `RATIFY` invocation and use that snapshot for all threshold checks throughout the entire ratification pass, rather than reading thresholds from the live `ensCurPParams` that is mutated by `ENACT` mid-pass. Concretely, the `RatifyEnv` (which is immutable across the recursive calls) should carry the epoch-start `PParams`, and `votingDRepThreshold`, `votingStakePoolThreshold`, and `votingCommitteeThreshold` should read from `RatifyEnv` instead of `RatifyState`.

### Proof of Concept

**Setup (epoch E):**
- Current PParams: `dvtPPGovGroupL = 60%`, `dvtTreasuryWithdrawal = 75%`
- Attacker controls DRep stake representing 65% of active DRep stake.

**Step 1:** Attacker submits `ParameterChange` action `PC` setting `dvtTreasuryWithdrawal := 50%`.

**Step 2:** Attacker submits `TreasuryWithdrawals` action `TW` draining the treasury to attacker-controlled accounts.

**Step 3:** Attacker votes `Yes` on both `PC` and `TW` with their 65% DRep stake. CC and SPO thresholds are also met for `PC` (or are zero for this action type).

**Step 4:** At epoch boundary, `RATIFY` calls `reorderActions`, placing `PC` (priority 4) before `TW` (priority 5).

**Step 5:** `PC` passes `acceptedByEveryone` (65% ≥ 60% `dvtPPGovGroupL`). `ENACT` fires, setting `ensCurPParams.dvtTreasuryWithdrawal := 50%`.

**Step 6:** `TW` is evaluated. `votingDRepThreshold` reads `ensCurPParams` from the now-updated `RatifyState` and returns `50%`. The attacker's 65% DRep support satisfies 65% ≥ 50%. `TW` passes.

**Step 7:** At the epoch boundary, `EPOCH` applies `rsEnacted` (both `PC` and `TW`), draining the treasury.

The attacker achieved a treasury withdrawal that required only 60% DRep support (to pass the `ParameterChange`) rather than the 75% that was the intended barrier for treasury withdrawals.
