### Title
Unprivileged Governance Proposal Submission Resets Global `numDormantEpochs` Counter, Preventing DRep Expiry and Blocking Governance Ratification — (`File: eras/conway/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/Rules/Certs.hs`)

---

### Summary

In the Conway era, any unprivileged user can submit a governance proposal (including a no-effect `InfoAction`) to atomically reset the global `vsNumDormantEpochs` counter to zero and simultaneously bump the stored `drepExpiry` of **every registered DRep** by the accumulated dormant-epoch count. Because this shared counter is manipulable by any transaction sender, an attacker can perpetually prevent inactive DReps from expiring, keeping their stake in the active governance distribution and diluting ratification thresholds — potentially permanently blocking treasury withdrawals and other governance actions.

---

### Finding Description

`VState` tracks a global counter `vsNumDormantEpochs` that counts consecutive epochs with no active governance proposals to vote on. This counter is designed to protect DReps from expiring during dormant periods when there is nothing to vote on. [1](#0-0) 

When any transaction containing at least one `ProposalProcedure` is processed, `updateDormantDRepExpiries` is triggered: [2](#0-1) 

This calls `updateDormantDRepExpiry`, which resets `vsNumDormantEpochs` to zero and bumps the stored `drepExpiry` of **all** registered DReps by the accumulated dormant-epoch count: [3](#0-2) 

The dormant-epoch counter is incremented at each epoch boundary when there are no active proposals: [4](#0-3) 

The critical flaw is that the reset and global DRep expiry bump are triggered by **any** proposal submission — including `InfoAction`, which has no governance effect and requires no special role. The deposit (`ppGovActionDeposit`) is fully refunded after `govActionLifetime` epochs, so the attacker's only cost is temporarily locking up the deposit amount. [5](#0-4) 

The deposit check in the `GOV` rule enforces only that the supplied deposit equals `ppGovActionDepositL` — it does not restrict who may submit: [6](#0-5) 

---

### Impact Explanation

When `numDormantEpochs` has accumulated to `N` and an attacker submits a proposal:

1. Every registered DRep's stored `drepExpiry` is incremented by `N` (unless already expired past `currentEpoch`).
2. `vsNumDormantEpochs` resets to 0.

DReps that have been genuinely inactive (not voting) and were approaching expiry are kept "alive" for `N` additional epochs. Their stake continues to count in the DRep stake distribution used for ratification. Because they do not vote, their weight effectively acts as a permanent abstain, raising the effective threshold needed for ratification. An attacker who repeats this every `drepActivity` epochs (the default inactivity window) can prevent any DRep from ever being pruned.

If a sufficient fraction of the DRep stake is held by inactive DReps kept alive this way, governance proposals — including `TreasuryWithdrawals` — can never reach their ratification threshold, permanently freezing governance-controlled funds. Recovery would require a hard fork to correct the DRep expiry accounting.

**Allowed impact matched**: *High — Permanent freezing of funds, deposits, rewards, or withdrawals where recovery requires a hard fork* (treasury withdrawals blocked); and *Medium — Attacker-controlled transactions modify withdrawals outside design parameters* (DRep expiry extended beyond design intent).

---

### Likelihood Explanation

- **Entry path**: Any transaction sender. No privileged role, no leaked key, no Sybil attack required.
- **Cost**: The attacker must lock up `ppGovActionDeposit` (currently 100,000 ADA on mainnet) per proposal, refunded after `govActionLifetime` epochs (~18 days). Submitting one proposal per `drepActivity` window (default 20 epochs ≈ 100 days) suffices to keep the counter perpetually reset.
- **Detectability**: The proposals appear as ordinary `InfoAction` submissions; no anomaly is visible at the ledger rule level.
- **Precondition**: A meaningful fraction of DRep stake must be held by inactive DReps for the governance-blocking impact to materialize. This is realistic over time as DRep operators become inactive without unregistering.

Likelihood is **Medium**: the deposit cost is non-trivial but affordable for a motivated attacker, and the precondition (inactive DReps with large delegated stake) is plausible on a live network.

---

### Recommendation

Decouple the `numDormantEpochs` reset from the global DRep expiry bump. Specifically:

1. **Do not bump inactive DReps' expiry on proposal submission.** Only DReps that actually vote on a proposal (handled by `updateVotingDRepExpiries`) should have their expiry extended. The dormant-epoch counter should still be reset when a proposal is submitted (to correctly track future dormancy), but the retroactive bump of all DReps' stored expiry should be removed or restricted to DReps that subsequently vote.

2. **Alternatively**, compute DRep "actual expiry" lazily as `storedExpiry + vsNumDormantEpochs` at ratification time (as `vsActualDRepExpiry` already does for queries), without ever writing the bumped value back into `drepExpiry`. This eliminates the attack surface entirely: the counter accumulates naturally, and no proposal submission can manipulate stored expiry values. [7](#0-6)

### Proof of Concept

**Setup**: `drepActivity = 20 epochs`, `govActionLifetime = 5 epochs`, `govActionDeposit = D ADA`. Several DReps register and then stop voting.

**Attack sequence**:

1. Epochs 1–20: No proposals submitted. `numDormantEpochs` increments to 20. Inactive DReps approach expiry (`actualExpiry = storedExpiry + 20`).

2. Epoch 20 (before epoch boundary): Attacker submits `InfoAction` with deposit `D`. The `CERTS` rule calls `updateDormantDRepExpiry(epoch=20, numDormantEpochs=20)`:
   - All DReps' `drepExpiry` is bumped by 20.
   - `vsNumDormantEpochs` resets to 0.
   - Inactive DReps that were about to expire now have `storedExpiry + 20`, keeping them active for 20 more epochs.

3. Epoch 25: Attacker's `InfoAction` proposal expires; deposit `D` is refunded.

4. Epochs 21–40: No proposals. `numDormantEpochs` increments to 20 again.

5. Epoch 40: Attacker repeats step 2. Net cost per cycle: opportunity cost of locking `D` for 5 epochs.

**Result**: Inactive DReps never expire. Their stake permanently dilutes governance ratification. `TreasuryWithdrawals` proposals submitted by legitimate actors fail to reach the DRep threshold. Governance is frozen until a hard fork corrects the expiry mechanism. [8](#0-7) [4](#0-3) [1](#0-0)

updateDormantDRepExpiry ::
  -- | Current Epoch
  EpochNo ->
  VState era ->
  VState era
updateDormantDRepExpiry currentEpoch vState =
  if numDormantEpochs == EpochNo 0
    then vState
    else
      vState
        & vsNumDormantEpochsL .~ EpochNo 0
        & vsDRepsL %~ Map.map updateExpiry
  where
    numDormantEpochs = vState ^. vsNumDormantEpochsL
    updateExpiry =
      drepExpiryL
        %~ \currentExpiry ->
          let actualExpiry = binOpEpochNo (+) numDormantEpochs currentExpiry
           in if actualExpiry < currentEpoch
                then currentExpiry
                else actualExpiry
