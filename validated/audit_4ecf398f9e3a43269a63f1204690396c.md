### Title
Misconfiguration of `minAmountToDeposit` and `depositLimitByAsset` Can Permanently Block All LST Deposits - (File: contracts/LRTDepositPool.sol)

---

### Summary

`LRTDepositPool` enforces two independently configurable bounds on every deposit: a minimum (`minAmountToDeposit`) and a per-asset ceiling (`depositLimitByAsset`). Neither setter validates the relationship between the two. If `minAmountToDeposit` is set above the effective remaining deposit capacity for an asset, every possible deposit amount simultaneously fails one of the two guards, making all new LST deposits permanently impossible until an admin corrects the configuration.

---

`depositLimitByAsset` is set independently in `LRTConfig`. Neither setter cross-checks the other.

### Impact Explanation

All new LST deposits for the misconfigured asset are blocked. Users holding stETH, ETHx, or other supported LSTs cannot enter the protocol to receive rsETH. The deposit pool's core promise â€” accepting LSTs in exchange for rsETH â€” is entirely broken for the affected asset until an admin corrects the configuration. Existing rsETH holders are unaffected, but the protocol fails to deliver its promised deposit service.

**Impact: Low â€” Contract fails to deliver promised returns, but doesn't lose value.**

---

### Likelihood Explanation

Two realistic paths lead to this state:

1. **Direct misconfiguration:** An admin raises `minAmountToDeposit` (e.g., to 100 ETH to filter dust) while the deposit limit for an asset is already set to 50 ETH in `LRTConfig`. Because the two parameters live in different contracts (`LRTDepositPool` vs `LRTConfig`), the admin may not notice the conflict.

2. **Limit reduction:** The deposit limit for an asset is lowered (e.g., to reduce EigenLayer exposure) to a value below the existing `minAmountToDeposit`. No validation prevents this.

Neither setter performs a cross-check, so the misconfiguration is silent until a user's deposit reverts.

---

### Recommendation

1. In `setMinAmountToDeposit`, validate that the new minimum does not exceed the remaining capacity for every supported asset.
2. In `LRTConfig` (or wherever `depositLimitByAsset` is set), validate that the new limit is not below `LRTDepositPool.minAmountToDeposit`.
3. Alternatively, add a view function `isDepositConfigurationValid(asset)` that operators can call after any configuration change to detect the conflict before it affects users.

---
