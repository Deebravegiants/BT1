### Title
Unauthorized `DirectDeposits` Allow Any Sender to Modify Another Account's Balance, Blocking Deregistration Refunds - (File: eras/dijkstra/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Dijkstra/Rules/Entities.hs)

---

### Summary

In the Dijkstra era, the `DirectDeposits` field of the transaction body allows any unprivileged transaction submitter to deposit ADA into any registered account without a witness or authorization from the account owner. This is a structural analog to the external report's vulnerability class: an attacker-controlled action modifies another user's financial state without their consent. The concrete harm is that an attacker can keep a victim's account balance non-zero, permanently blocking the victim's ability to deregister their stake credential and recover their key deposit ‚Äî for as long as the attacker is willing to pay dust.

---

### Finding Description

**Vulnerability class**: Unauthorized deposit / financial-state modification on behalf of another user.

**Root cause ‚Äî `dijkstraEntitiesTransition`**

In `eras/dijkstra/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Dijkstra/Rules/Entities.hs`, the ENTITIES transition rule reads `DirectDeposits` from the transaction body and applies them unconditionally:

```haskell
let directDeposits = tx ^. bodyTxL . directDepositsTxBodyL
    accountsAfterCerts = certStateAfterCerts ^. certDStateL . accountsL
failOnJust (directDepositsMissingAccounts directDeposits accountsAfterCerts) $
  injectFailure . DirectDepositsToMissingAccounts

pure $ certStateAfterCerts & certDStateL . accountsL %~ applyDirectDeposits directDeposits
``` [1](#0-0) 

The **only** predicate checked is `directDepositsMissingAccounts` ‚Äî that the target credential is registered. No witness from the account owner is required, and no check verifies that the depositing party has any relationship to the target account.

**`applyDirectDeposits` adds the amount unconditionally**

```haskell
applyDirectDeposits (DirectDeposits dd) =
  updateAccountBalances
    (\amount account -> addCompactCoin amount (account ^. balanceAccountStateL))
    dd
``` [2](#0-1) 

**`DirectDeposits` type ‚Äî no authorization field**

```haskell
newtype DirectDeposits = DirectDeposits {unDirectDeposits :: Map AccountAddress Coin}
``` [3](#0-2) 

**The blocking mechanism ‚Äî `StakeKeyHasNonZeroAccountBalanceDELEG`**

The Conway DELEG rule (inherited by Dijkstra via `ENTITIES ‚Üí CERTS ‚Üí CERT ‚Üí DELEG`) refuses to deregister a stake credential if its account balance is non-zero:

```haskell
checkStakeKeyHasZeroRewardBalance = do
  accountState <- mAccountState
  let balanceCompact = accountState ^. balanceAccountStateL
  guard (balanceCompact /= mempty)
  Just $ fromCompact balanceCompact
failOnJust
  checkStakeKeyHasZeroRewardBalance
  (injectFailure . StakeKeyHasNonZeroAccountBalanceDELEG)
``` [4](#0-3) 

**`DirectDeposits` is part of both top-level and sub-transaction bodies**

The field is present in both `DijkstraTxBodyRaw TopTx` and `DijkstraTxBodyRaw SubTx`, meaning the attack is reachable from both top-level transactions and nested sub-transactions. [5](#0-4)

### Impact Explanation

An attacker who observes a victim's pending deregistration transaction in the mempool can front-run it with a transaction containing `DirectDeposits` targeting the victim's account with 1 lovelace. The victim's deregistration fails with `StakeKeyHasNonZeroAccountBalanceDELEG`. The victim must then submit a withdrawal transaction (requiring their own signature) to drain the balance to zero before they can deregister.

The attacker can repeat this indefinitely: each time the victim drains their balance and resubmits the deregistration, the attacker front-runs again with another 1-lovelace deposit. The cost to the attacker per round is 1 lovelace plus transaction fees; the cost to the victim is repeated transaction fees and indefinite delay in recovering their stake key deposit.

This matches the **Medium** allowed impact: *"Attacker-controlled transactions‚Ä¶ modify fees, deposits, refunds, rewards, treasury donations, or withdrawals outside design parameters."* The victim's deposit refund is modified (blocked) outside design parameters ‚Äî the victim authorized no deposit to their account, yet their deregistration path is obstructed.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

- The Dijkstra era is implemented in the production codebase and is the next scheduled era.
- The attack requires only mempool observation and a standard transaction submission ‚Äî no privileged access, no key compromise, no governance majority.
- The attacker's cost per round is minimal (1 lovelace + fees).
- The victim has no on-chain recourse other than withdrawing and retrying, which the attacker can always front-run again.

Likelihood: **Medium** (requires Dijkstra era activation; mempool front-running is a standard capability for any node operator or well-connected participant).

---

### Recommendation

Add an authorization check to `DirectDeposits` processing: require that the transaction includes a valid witness (vkey or script) for each target account credential before applying the deposit. This mirrors the existing witness requirement for withdrawals (`validateWithdrawalsDelegated`) and deregistration. Alternatively, restrict `DirectDeposits` so that a deposit to account `cred` is only valid if `cred` is also the subject of a certificate or withdrawal in the same transaction that already carries the credential's witness.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Victim holds a registered stake credential `cred` with zero account balance and a pending deregistration transaction `TxDeReg` in the mempool.
2. Attacker constructs `TxDust`:
   - Inputs: attacker's UTxO covering fees + 1 lovelace
   - `directDepositsTxBodyL` = `DirectDeposits { AccountAddress Mainnet (AccountId cred) ‚Üí Coin 1 }`
   - No witness for `cred` required ‚Äî `DirectDeposits` has no such check
3. Attacker submits `TxDust` before `TxDeReg` is included in a block.
4. `TxDust` is accepted: `directDepositsMissingAccounts` passes (cred is registered); `applyDirectDeposits` sets victim's balance to 1 lovelace.
5. `TxDeReg` is rejected: `checkStakeKeyHasZeroRewardBalance` fires ‚Üí `StakeKeyHasNonZeroAccountBalanceDELEG (Coin 1)`.
6. Victim must submit `TxWithdraw` (requires victim's signature) to drain the 1 lovelace, then resubmit `TxDeReg`.
7. Attacker repeats from step 2 ‚Äî the cycle can continue indefinitely at negligible cost to the attacker.
