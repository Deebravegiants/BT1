### Title
ERC1155 `safeTransferFrom` in `finTransfer` permanently locks bridged assets when recipient is a non-compliant contract — (`File: evm/src/omni-bridge/contracts/OmniBridge.sol`)

---

### Summary

`finTransfer` uses `IERC1155.safeTransferFrom` to deliver ERC1155 tokens to a user-specified `payload.recipient`. If that recipient is a contract that does not implement `onERC1155Received`, the call reverts, the transaction is rolled back, and — because the recipient address is cryptographically bound in the signed payload — the transfer can never be completed. The ERC1155 tokens are permanently locked inside the bridge contract.

---

### Finding Description

In `OmniBridge.finTransfer`, the ERC1155 delivery branch executes:

```solidity
IERC1155(multiToken.tokenAddress).safeTransferFrom(
    address(this),
    payload.recipient,   // user-controlled
    multiToken.tokenId,
    payload.amount,
    ""
);
``` [1](#0-0) 

`safeTransferFrom` in ERC1155 mandates that if the recipient is a contract it must implement `IERC1155Receiver.onERC1155Received` and return the correct magic selector; otherwise the call reverts. The `payload.recipient` is supplied by the user when initiating the transfer on the source chain and is cryptographically committed into the signed message:

```solidity
Borsh.encodeAddress(payload.recipient),
``` [2](#0-1) 

The signature is verified against `nearBridgeDerivedAddress` at line 311, so the recipient field cannot be altered without invalidating the signature. [3](#0-2) 

The nonce guard writes `completedTransfers[payload.destinationNonce] = true` at line 287 **before** the transfer, but because the revert rolls back the entire transaction, the nonce is never consumed. [4](#0-3) 

Every subsequent call to `finTransfer` with the same signed payload will reach the same `safeTransferFrom` and revert again. There is no alternative delivery path, no pull-claim fallback, and no way to change the recipient without a new signature from the bridge authority.

---

### Impact Explanation

**Critical — Irreversible fund lock.**

The ERC1155 tokens remain permanently held by the bridge contract with no mechanism to release them. The user's assets on the source chain (NEAR) are already locked or burned at the time of `initTransfer`. Both sides of the bridge are frozen for that transfer: the source-chain assets are gone and the destination-chain assets are undeliverable.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**High.**

The recipient is freely chosen by the user. Multisigs (Gnosis Safe), DAO treasuries, vaults, and many other widely-deployed contracts do not implement `onERC1155Received`. A user who bridges ERC1155 tokens to any such address — even accidentally — triggers the permanent lock. No privileged access or external collusion is required.

---

### Recommendation

Replace the push-based `safeTransferFrom` with a pull-based claim pattern for ERC1155 deliveries:

1. Record the pending ERC1155 credit in a mapping (e.g., `pendingERC1155[recipient][tokenAddress][tokenId] += amount`) instead of transferring immediately.
2. Expose a separate `claimERC1155` function that the recipient calls to pull their tokens, using `safeTransferFrom` at that point (where a revert only harms the caller, not the protocol).

Alternatively, use a low-level call that catches the revert and falls back to the pull-claim storage, ensuring `finTransfer` always succeeds and the nonce is always consumed.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Alice holds ERC1155 token `T` on NEAR and calls `initTransfer1155` (or the NEAR equivalent) specifying `recipient = VaultContract` on Ethereum, where `VaultContract` is a contract without `onERC1155Received`.
2. The bridge authority signs a `TransferMessagePayload` with `recipient = VaultContract` and `tokenAddress = deterministicToken(T)`.
3. A relayer calls `OmniBridge.finTransfer(signatureData, payload)`.
4. Execution reaches line 324: `IERC1155(multiToken.tokenAddress).safeTransferFrom(address(this), VaultContract, ...)`.
5. `VaultContract` has no `onERC1155Received`; the ERC1155 implementation reverts.
6. The entire transaction reverts; `completedTransfers[nonce]` is rolled back to `false`.
7. Every subsequent call with the same signed payload reverts identically.
8. Alice's source-chain assets are burned/locked; the ERC1155 tokens sit in `OmniBridge` forever with no recovery path.
