### Title
`SendMessageBanned` CLVM Restriction Misses `RECEIVE_MESSAGE` (Opcode 67), Allowing Bypass of Cross-Coin Messaging Ban - (File: `chia/wallet/puzzles/custody/send_message_banned.clsp`)

---

### Summary

The `send_message_banned.clsp` dpuz-validator puzzle, part of the MIPS custody architecture (`PuzzleWithRestrictions`), is intended to prevent delegated puzzles from establishing cross-coin communication channels. However, it only checks for `SEND_MESSAGE` (opcode 66) and completely omits `RECEIVE_MESSAGE` (opcode 67). An unprivileged spender can craft a delegated puzzle that emits `RECEIVE_MESSAGE` conditions, bypassing the restriction entirely while still establishing a cross-coin messaging channel with an attacker-controlled external coin.

---

### Finding Description

The `send_message_banned.clsp` puzzle is loaded and used as a dpuz validator restriction via `SendMessageBanned` in `chia/wallet/puzzles/custody/restrictions.py`. Its sole purpose, as stated in its own comment, is to enforce "the lack of SEND_MESSAGE conditions in the condition set."

The puzzle iterates over all conditions and raises (`(x)`) only if it encounters a condition whose opcode equals `SEND_MESSAGE` (66):

```clsp
(if (= (f (f conditions)) SEND_MESSAGE)
    (x)
    (check_conditions (r conditions) return)
)
```

However, Chia's protocol defines two paired messaging opcodes:
- `SEND_MESSAGE` = 66 (opcode checked)
- `RECEIVE_MESSAGE` = 67 (opcode **not** checked)

Both opcodes are defined in `condition_codes.clib` and `ConditionOpcode` in Python. A `RECEIVE_MESSAGE` condition in a delegated puzzle's output is equally capable of establishing a cross-coin communication channel — it simply does so from the receiving side, requiring a matching `SEND_MESSAGE` from an external coin in the same spend bundle.

Because the check only covers opcode 66, a delegated puzzle that emits `RECEIVE_MESSAGE` (67) passes the `send_message_banned.clsp` validator without triggering the exception.

---

### Impact Explanation

The `SendMessageBanned` restriction is a dpuz validator applied inside `PuzzleWithRestrictions` (the MIPS custody architecture). Its purpose is to prevent delegated puzzles from coordinating with external coins via the messaging system. By emitting `RECEIVE_MESSAGE` instead of `SEND_MESSAGE`, an unprivileged spender can:

1. Craft a delegated puzzle that emits `RECEIVE_MESSAGE` conditions targeting an attacker-controlled external coin.
2. The `send_message_banned.clsp` validator passes (only checks opcode 66).
3. The spend bundle includes the attacker-controlled coin emitting a matching `SEND_MESSAGE`.
4. Cross-coin coordination is established, bypassing the restriction that was meant to prevent it.

This constitutes a bypass of custody authorization in the MIPS architecture — a protected state transition guard is circumvented by an unprivileged spender. Any custody coin relying solely on `SendMessageBanned` to prevent cross-coin messaging is not actually protected against `RECEIVE_MESSAGE`-based coordination.

**Impact: High** — Bypass of custody/wallet authorization enabling unauthorized protected state transitions in singleton-controlled or custody-protected assets.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**Low-to-Medium.** The `SendMessageBanned` restriction must be actively deployed in a `PuzzleWithRestrictions` custody arrangement. The attacker must be a party authorized to submit a delegated puzzle (i.e., a valid signer in the M-of-N scheme) but who is attempting to exceed the restrictions placed on that delegated puzzle. No key compromise or external dependency is required — only knowledge of the incomplete check.

---

### Recommendation

Update `send_message_banned.clsp` to also reject `RECEIVE_MESSAGE` (67) conditions:

```clsp
(if (or (= (f (f conditions)) SEND_MESSAGE)
        (= (f (f conditions)) RECEIVE_MESSAGE))
    (x)
    (check_conditions (r conditions) return)
)
```

Rename the puzzle and the `SendMessageBanned` Python class to `MessagesBanned` or `CrossCoinMessagingBanned` to reflect the complete intent.

---

### Proof of Concept

**Setup:** A custody coin is protected by `PuzzleWithRestrictions` with `SendMessageBanned` as a dpuz validator restriction.

**Attack:**
1. Attacker crafts a delegated puzzle that produces a `RECEIVE_MESSAGE` condition (opcode 67) targeting an attacker-controlled coin.
2. The `send_message_banned.clsp` validator iterates conditions and checks `(= (f (f conditions)) SEND_MESSAGE)` — this is `(= 67 66)` → false. The validator passes without raising.
3. The spend bundle includes the attacker-controlled coin emitting a matching `SEND_MESSAGE` (66).
4. The spend bundle is accepted on-chain. Cross-coin messaging is established despite the `SendMessageBanned` restriction.
