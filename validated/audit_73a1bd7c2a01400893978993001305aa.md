### Title
rsETH Price Inflation via ETH Donation to LRTDepositPool — Direct Theft of Depositor Funds - (File: contracts/LRTDepositPool.sol / contracts/LRTOracle.sol)

---

### Summary

An unprivileged attacker can send ETH directly to `LRTDepositPool` via its open `receive()` fallback, inflating `address(this).balance`. Because `getETHDistributionData()` uses the raw contract balance as the canonical ETH accounting figure, and `LRTOracle._updateRsETHPrice()` derives `rsETHPrice` from that figure, the stored `rsETHPrice` is permanently inflated after the next price update. Subsequent depositors receive fewer rsETH tokens than they are entitled to, while the attacker — who holds rsETH minted before the donation — redeems at the inflated rate, stealing the difference.

---

### Finding Description

**Step 1 — Open ETH receiver**

`LRTDepositPool` declares an unrestricted `receive()` fallback:

```solidity
receive() external payable { }
```

Any address can send ETH to the deposit pool at any time.

**Step 2 — Raw balance used as canonical accounting**

`getETHDistributionData()` reads `address(this).balance` directly:

```solidity
ethLyingInDepositPool = address(this).balance;
```

Donated ETH is indistinguishable from legitimately deposited ETH.

**Step 3 — Price derived from inflated balance**

`LRTOracle._getTotalEthInProtocol()` calls `getTotalAssetDeposits(ETH_TOKEN)` → `getETHDistributionData()`, summing the inflated balance into `totalETHInProtocol`. `_updateRsETHPrice()` then computes:

```solidity
uint256 newRsETHPrice = (totalETHInProtocol - protocolFeeInETH).divWad(rsethSupply);
```

With `rsethSupply` unchanged and `totalETHInProtocol` artificially elevated, `rsETHPrice` is inflated.

**Step 4 — Minting uses the stored inflated price**

`getRsETHAmountToMint()` divides by the stored `rsETHPrice`:

```solidity
rsethAmountToMint = (amount * lrtOracle.getAssetPrice(asset)) / lrtOracle.rsETHPrice();
```

A higher `rsETHPrice` means fewer rsETH tokens minted per unit of ETH deposited by subsequent users.

**Step 5 — No effective guard when `pricePercentageLimit == 0`**

The only price-increase guard in `_updateRsETHPrice()` is:

```solidity
bool isPriceIncreaseOffLimit =
    pricePercentageLimit > 0 && priceDifference > pricePercentageLimit.mulWad(highestRsethPrice);
```

`pricePercentageLimit` is never set in `initialize()`, so it defaults to `0`. The condition short-circuits to `false`, and the price update proceeds with no cap, regardless of how large the donation is.

---

### Impact Explanation

**Critical — Direct theft of user funds.**

An attacker who holds rsETH minted before the donation can redeem at the inflated `rsETHPrice`, recovering more ETH than they deposited. The shortfall comes directly from subsequent depositors, who receive fewer rsETH tokens than the ETH they contributed warrants. In the extreme case (donation >> existing TVL), a subsequent depositor's rsETH amount rounds to zero, causing a total loss of their deposit.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**High.** The attack requires no privileged access:

1. Call `depositETH{value: 1 wei}()` to obtain 1 wei of rsETH.
2. Send a large ETH donation directly to `LRTDepositPool` (via `receive()`).
3. Wait for the next `updateRSETHPrice()` call (public, callable by anyone).
4. Subsequent depositors are harmed immediately.
5. Attacker redeems rsETH through the withdrawal queue at the inflated rate.

The only friction is the withdrawal queue delay, which does not prevent the harm to victims.

---

### Recommendation

1. **Track deposited ETH separately from raw balance.** Maintain an internal accounting variable (e.g., `totalDepositedETH`) that is incremented only through the authorised deposit paths (`depositETH`, `receiveFromRewardReceiver`, `receiveFromNodeDelegator`, `receiveFromLRTConverter`) and use that variable instead of `address(this).balance` in `getETHDistributionData()`.

2. **Set a non-zero `pricePercentageLimit` at initialisation.** This limits the per-update price increase and forces large manipulations to span multiple cycles, giving operators time to detect and respond.

3. **Seed the protocol with an initial deposit at deployment** (analogous to the GoGoPool mitigation) so that the ratio of donated ETH to existing TVL is never large enough to cause meaningful rounding loss for subsequent depositors.

---

### Proof of Concept

```
Initial state:
  rsethSupply = 1 wei (attacker deposited 1 wei ETH via depositETH)
  totalETHInProtocol = 1 wei
  rsETHPrice = 1e18 (1 ETH per rsETH)

Attack:
  Attacker sends 1000 ETH directly to LRTDepositPool (receive())
  Anyone calls updateRSETHPrice()
    → totalETHInProtocol = 1000 ETH + 1 wei
    → rsETHPrice = (1000e18 + 1) / 1 ≈ 1000e18 * 1e18  [divWad]
    → rsETHPrice ≈ 1000 ETH per rsETH

Alice deposits 2000 ETH:
  rsethAmountToMint = 2000e18 * 1e18 / (1000e18 * 1e18)
                    = 2000e18 / 1000e18 = 2 wei rsETH

Total rsETH supply = 1 wei + 2 wei = 3 wei
Total ETH in protocol = 1000 ETH + 1 wei + 2000 ETH = 3000 ETH + 1 wei

Attacker redeems 1 wei rsETH:
  ETH received = (3000e18 + 1) * 1 / 3 ≈ 1000 ETH

Attacker net gain: 1000 ETH received − 1000 ETH donated − 1 wei deposited ≈ 0 ETH
Alice net loss: deposited 2000 ETH, holds 2 wei rsETH worth ≈ 2000 ETH (2/3 of pool)

[With larger donation relative to Alice's deposit, attacker profit is positive and Alice suffers rounding to 0 shares]
```

The attack becomes profitable when the donation is sized so that Alice's rsETH rounds to 0 (total loss) or when the attacker's share of the pool exceeds their proportional contribution. [1](#0-0) [2](#0-1) [3](#0-2) [4](#0-3) [5](#0-4) [6](#0-5) [7](#0-6)

        uint256 supportedAssetCount = supportedAssets.length;
