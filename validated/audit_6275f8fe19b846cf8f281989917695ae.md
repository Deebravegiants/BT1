Audit Report

## Title
Pending Withdrawal Tokens Cease Accruing Rewards During Mandatory Delay Period, Redistributing Yield to Remaining Stakers - (File: contracts/KERNEL/KernelDepositPool.sol)

## Summary
In `KernelDepositPool.sol`, `initiateWithdrawal()` immediately decrements both `balanceOf[msg.sender]` and `totalKernelStaked` at the moment of initiation, even though the withdrawn tokens remain locked in the contract for up to `MAX_WITHDRAWAL_DELAY = 30 days`. Because reward accrual depends entirely on `balanceOf` and `totalKernelStaked`, the withdrawing user earns zero rewards on their locked tokens for the entire delay window, while those rewards are proportionally redistributed to all remaining stakers.

## Finding Description
`initiateWithdrawal()` decrements both accounting variables before the delay elapses:

```solidity
// contracts/KERNEL/KernelDepositPool.sol L325-326
balanceOf[msg.sender] -= _amount;
totalKernelStaked -= _amount;
```

The `updateReward(msg.sender)` modifier runs first (correctly snapshotting rewards accrued up to this point), but after the decrement, the user's `balanceOf` is zero. The `earned()` function computes future accrual exclusively from `balanceOf[_account]`:

```solidity
// L421-423
return (balanceOf[_account] * (rewardPerToken() - userRewardPerTokenPaid[_account]) / DECIMAL_PRECISION)
    + rewards[_account];
```

And `rewardPerToken()` divides by `totalKernelStaked`:

```solidity
// L412-413
return rewardPerTokenStored + (rewardRate * (lastTimeRewardApplicable() - updatedAt) * DECIMAL_PRECISION)
    / totalKernelStaked;
```

Because `totalKernelStaked` is reduced immediately, the reward rate per token for remaining stakers increases. The withdrawing user's tokens are physically held by the contract — they cannot be transferred, staked elsewhere, or used in any way — yet they contribute zero to that user's reward accrual and zero to the denominator. The full reward stream attributable to those tokens is silently redirected to other stakers for the entire delay period (up to `MAX_WITHDRAWAL_DELAY = 30 days`, L35).

This is a meaningful deviation from the Synthetix base design (acknowledged in the contract's NatSpec at L17): the original Synthetix contract has no withdrawal delay, so removing a user from reward accounting simultaneously with token transfer is correct there. Here, the delay was added without a corresponding adjustment to reward accounting, creating a window where the user bears the opportunity cost of locked tokens with no reward compensation.

No existing guard prevents this. The `nonReentrant` modifier and balance checks only protect against double-spending, not against the accounting mismatch. `claimWithdrawal()` (L344) does not call `updateReward` and does not restore any reward entitlement.

## Impact Explanation
**High — Theft of unclaimed yield.**

Any user who calls `initiateWithdrawal()` loses all staking rewards on their pending tokens for the mandatory delay period. Those rewards are not burned or returned to the protocol — they are redistributed proportionally to all remaining stakers. The withdrawing user suffers a direct, quantifiable loss of yield that accrues to other participants. With a 30-day delay and a non-trivial reward rate, the loss per user can be substantial and is fully measurable on-chain.

## Likelihood Explanation
**High.** Every user who calls `initiateWithdrawal()` is unconditionally and automatically affected. This is a standard, expected user action requiring no special preconditions, no attacker coordination, and no privileged access. The delay is mandatory and configurable up to 30 days. The loss occurs deterministically for every withdrawing staker.

## Recommendation
Decouple reward-bearing balance from withdrawal-eligibility accounting. Two concrete approaches:

1. **Preferred:** Introduce a `pendingWithdrawalBalance` mapping. In `initiateWithdrawal()`, move the amount into `pendingWithdrawalBalance[msg.sender]` instead of zeroing it out of `balanceOf`. Include it in `earned()` as `(balanceOf[_account] + pendingWithdrawalBalance[_account]) * ...`. Only remove from `pendingWithdrawalBalance` and reduce `totalKernelStaked` in `claimWithdrawal()` when tokens are actually transferred out.

2. **Alternative:** Do not reduce `totalKernelStaked` or `balanceOf` in `initiateWithdrawal()`. Track the pending amount separately for withdrawal eligibility only, and perform the accounting decrement in `claimWithdrawal()` (with `updateReward(msg.sender)` called there before the decrement).

## Proof of Concept
Minimal Foundry test sequence:

1. Alice stakes 1000 KERNEL. `balanceOf[Alice] = 1000`, `totalKernelStaked = 1000`.
2. Bob stakes 1000 KERNEL. `balanceOf[Bob] = 1000`, `totalKernelStaked = 2000`.
3. Admin calls `notifyRewardAmount(2000e18)` with a 30-day duration → `rewardRate = 2000e18 / 30 days`.
4. Alice calls `initiateWithdrawal(1000)`. Immediately: `balanceOf[Alice] = 0`, `totalKernelStaked = 1000`. Alice's 1000 KERNEL are locked in the contract for 30 days.
5. `vm.warp(block.timestamp + 30 days)`.
6. Assert `earned(Alice) == 0` — Alice earned nothing despite her tokens being locked the entire period.
7. Assert `earned(Bob) ≈ 2000e18` — Bob received the full reward pool, including Alice's share.
8. Alice calls `claimWithdrawal()` and receives her 1000 KERNEL back with zero reward compensation for the 30-day lock.
9. Had Alice not initiated a withdrawal, she would have earned ~1000e18 (50% of the pool). Those rewards went entirely to Bob.
