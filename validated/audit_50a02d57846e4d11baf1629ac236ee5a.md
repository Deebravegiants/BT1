### Title
Stale Reverse DRep Delegation During Bootstrap Phase Causes Permanent Loss of Governance Voting Power on DRep Unregistration - (File: `eras/conway/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/Rules/Deleg.hs`)

### Summary

During the Conway bootstrap phase (protocol version 9), when a stake credential re-delegates its vote from DRep A to DRep B, the old DRep A's `drepDelegs` reverse-delegation set is not cleaned up. If DRep A subsequently unregisters, the `ConwayUnRegDRep` handler uses the stale `drepDelegs` to clear account delegations, incorrectly nullifying the credential's delegation to DRep B. The `updateDRepDelegations` hard-fork fix at PV10 then rebuilds `drepDelegs` from account states, but since the account state was corrupted to `Nothing`, the credential permanently loses its DRep delegation and its stake is excluded from all DRep voting-power calculations post-bootstrap.

### Finding Description

**Root cause — stale reverse delegation not removed on re-delegation (bootstrap phase):**

In `processDelegationInternal`, when `preserveIncorrectDelegation` is `True` (i.e., `pvMajor pv < natVersion @10`), re-delegating to a new credential DRep only *adds* the stake credential to the new DRep's `drepDelegs` without removing it from the old DRep's `drepDelegs`: [1](#0-0) 

The condition at line 363 (`isNothing mAccountState || preserveIncorrectDelegation`) causes the code to skip `unDelegReDelegDRep`, which is the function that would have removed the stale entry from DRep A's `drepDelegs`.

**Exploitation path — DRep A unregisters, corrupting the account state:**

When DRep A unregisters via `ConwayUnRegDRep`, the handler iterates over its `drepDelegs` (which still contains the stale entry) and sets `dRepDelegationAccountStateL` to `Nothing` for every credential in that set: [2](#0-1) 

This incorrectly clears the account delegation of a credential that has already moved to DRep B.

**Hard-fork fix is incomplete — corrupted accounts are not recovered:**

At the PV9→PV10 hard fork, `updateDRepDelegations` rebuilds `drepDelegs` from account states: [3](#0-2) 

However, since the account's `dRepDelegationAccountStateL` was already set to `Nothing` by the DRep A unregistration, `adjustDelegations` sees `Nothing` and does not add the credential to any DRep's `drepDelegs`. The credential permanently has no DRep delegation.

**Governance ratification uses the corrupted account state:**

`computeDRepDistr` (the DRep pulser) reads `dRepDelegationAccountStateL` directly from account state to build the DRep distribution used in ratification: [4](#0-3) 

With `dRepDelegationAccountStateL = Nothing`, the credential's stake is excluded from DRep B's voting power in `dRepAcceptedRatio`: [5](#0-4) 

The test suite explicitly confirms this behavior during bootstrap: [6](#0-5) 

### Impact Explanation

An attacker who registered as a DRep during bootstrap can:
1. Attract delegators.
2. Wait for delegators to re-delegate to a different DRep B.
3. Unregister as DRep A — this clears the delegators' account delegation to `Nothing` via the stale `drepDelegs`.
4. After the PV9→PV10 hard fork, those delegators have no DRep delegation and their stake is excluded from all DRep voting-power calculations.

This reduces DRep B's effective voting power below what it should be. If DRep B's votes were the deciding factor on a governance action (e.g., a `ParameterChange`, `TreasuryWithdrawals`, or `UpdateCommittee`), the action could be incorrectly ratified or blocked, constituting an unauthorized governance state transition. This matches: **Medium — attacker-controlled certificates modify governance voting power outside design parameters**, and potentially **Critical — unauthorized governance action is enacted** if the stake shift crosses a ratification threshold.

### Likelihood Explanation

The attack requires only two unprivileged on-chain transactions: a `ConwayRegDRep` certificate to register as a DRep, and a `ConwayUnRegDRep` certificate to unregister. No privileged access, key compromise, or majority is needed. The attacker only needs to have attracted at least one delegator who subsequently re-delegated. The bootstrap phase (PV9) was live on mainnet, meaning this window existed. Any chain that replays or forks from a PV9 state retains the corrupted accounts.

### Recommendation

1. In `processDelegationInternal`, when `preserveIncorrectDelegation` is `True` and the new delegatee is a `DRepCredential`, still call `unDelegReDelegDRep` to remove the stale entry from the old DRep's `drepDelegs` before adding to the new one.
2. In `updateDRepDelegations` (the PV10 hard-fork migration), additionally scan for accounts whose `dRepDelegationAccountStateL` is `Nothing` but whose credential appears in any DRep's `drepDelegs`, and restore the delegation from the `drepDelegs` entry rather than discarding it.
3. Alternatively, in `ConwayUnRegDRep`'s `clearDRepDelegations`, cross-check each credential in `drepDelegs` against the account's current `dRepDelegationAccountStateL` before clearing — only clear if the account still points to the unregistering DRep.

### Proof of Concept

The test at `eras/conway/impl/testlib/Test/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/Imp/DelegSpec.hs` lines 287–323 already demonstrates the bug: [7](#0-6) 

Extending this test to pass epochs through the PV9→PV10 hard fork and then check `computeDRepDistr` would confirm that the affected credential's stake is absent from DRep B's distribution, reducing DRep B's ratification power below its correct value.

```haskell
updateDRepDelegations :: ConwayEraCertState era => CertState era -> CertState era
updateDRepDelegations certState =
  let accountsMap = certState ^. certDStateL . accountsL . accountsMapL
      dReps =
        -- Reset all delegations in order to remove any inconsistencies
        -- Delegations will be reset accordingly below.
        Map.map (\dRepState -> dRepState {drepDelegs = Set.empty}) $
          certState ^. certVStateL . vsDRepsL
      (dRepsWithDelegations, accountsWithoutUnknownDRepDelegations) =
        Map.mapAccumWithKey adjustDelegations dReps accountsMap
      adjustDelegations ds stakeCred accountState =
        case accountState ^. dRepDelegationAccountStateL of
          Just (DRepCredential dRep) ->
            let addDelegation _ dRepState =
                  Just $ dRepState {drepDelegs = Set.insert stakeCred (drepDelegs dRepState)}
             in case Map.updateLookupWithKey addDelegation dRep ds of
                  (Nothing, _) -> (ds, accountState & dRepDelegationAccountStateL .~ Nothing)
                  (Just _, ds') -> (ds', accountState)
          _ -> (ds, accountState)
   in certState
        -- Remove dangling delegations to non-existent DReps:
        & certDStateL . accountsL . accountsMapL .~ accountsWithoutUnknownDRepDelegations
        -- Populate DRep delegations with delegatees
        & certVStateL . vsDRepsL .~ dRepsWithDelegations
```

```haskell
dRepAcceptedRatio RatifyEnv {reDRepDistr, reDRepState, reCurrentEpoch} gasDRepVotes govAction =
  toInteger yesStake %? toInteger totalExcludingAbstainStake
  where
    accumStake (!yes, !tot) drep (CompactCoin stake) =
      case drep of
        DRepCredential cred ->
          case Map.lookup cred reDRepState of
            Nothing -> (yes, tot) -- drep is not registered, so we don't consider it
            Just drepState
              | reCurrentEpoch > drepExpiry drepState -> (yes, tot) -- drep is expired, so we don't consider it
              | otherwise ->
                  case Map.lookup cred gasDRepVotes of
                    -- drep hasn't voted for this action, so we don't count
                    -- the vote but we consider it in the denominator:
                    Nothing -> (yes, tot + stake)
                    Just VoteYes -> (yes + stake, tot + stake)
                    Just Abstain -> (yes, tot)
                    Just VoteNo -> (yes, tot + stake)
        DRepAlwaysNoConfidence ->
          case govAction of
            NoConfidence _ -> (yes + stake, tot + stake)
            _ -> (yes, tot + stake)
        DRepAlwaysAbstain -> (yes, tot)
    (yesStake, totalExcludingAbstainStake) = Map.foldlWithKey' accumStake (0, 0) reDRepDistr
```
