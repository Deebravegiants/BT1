### Title
Missing `directDeposits` in UTxO Produced-Value Equation Allows Unbounded ADA Creation - (File: `eras/dijkstra/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Dijkstra/UTxO.hs`)

---

### Summary

The Dijkstra era introduces a `directDeposits` field in the transaction body that credits ADA directly to registered account balances. However, `directDeposits` are never included in the **produced** side of the UTxO value-conservation equation. Because the ENTITIES rule unconditionally applies those deposits to account balances, an attacker can include the deposit amount in UTxO outputs (to satisfy the balance check) while simultaneously having the same amount credited to their account, creating ADA out of thin air.

---

### Finding Description

The Dijkstra era adds `dtbrDirectDeposits :: !DirectDeposits` to the top-level transaction body and `dstbrDirectDeposits :: !DirectDeposits` to the sub-transaction body. [1](#0-0) 

The ENTITIES rule validates that every target account is registered and then unconditionally applies the deposits: [2](#0-1) 

The UTxO balance check in `dijkstraUtxoTransition` calls `Shelley.validateValueNotConservedUTxO`, which compares `consumed` against `getProducedValue`: [3](#0-2) 

For Dijkstra, `getProducedValue = getProducedDijkstraValue`: [4](#0-3) 

`dijkstraProducedValue` delegates to `conwayProducedValue` (which includes outputs, fee, deposits, treasury donation) and then aggregates sub-transaction produced values via `dijkstraSubTxProducedValue`: [5](#0-4) 

Neither `conwayProducedValue` nor `dijkstraSubTxProducedValue` includes `directDeposits`. Compare: `treasuryDonationTxBodyL` **is** included in both functions, confirming the omission of `directDeposits` is not intentional design but a missing term. [6](#0-5) 

Similarly, `getConsumedDijkstraValue` does not include `directDeposits` on the consumed side either: [7](#0-6)

### Impact Explanation

**Critical â€” Direct creation of ADA through an invalid ledger state transition.**

Attack trace (simplified, fee omitted):

1. Attacker holds 100 ADA in a UTxO input.
2. Attacker constructs a Dijkstra transaction:
   - `inputs`: the 100 ADA UTxO
   - `outputs`: 100 ADA back to attacker's payment address
   - `directDeposits`: 100 ADA â†’ attacker's own staking account
3. UTxO balance check: `consumed` (100) = `produced` (100) â€” **passes**, because `directDeposits` is absent from `produced`.
4. ENTITIES rule: `applyDirectDeposits` adds 100 ADA to the attacker's account balance â€” **no further check**.
5. Result: attacker holds 100 ADA in UTxO **and** 100 ADA in their account = 200 ADA total from a 100 ADA input.

The attacker can repeat this with arbitrarily large amounts, limited only by the UTxO they control. The ledger state is permanently invalid (total ADA supply exceeds genesis), requiring a hard fork to correct.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

Any unprivileged transaction submitter in the Dijkstra era can craft such a transaction. No special keys, governance majority, or privileged access is required. The only prerequisite is a registered staking account (trivially obtained) and any UTxO to use as input. Likelihood is **high** once the Dijkstra era is active on mainnet.

---

### Recommendation

Include `directDeposits` in the produced-value calculation, analogously to how `treasuryDonationTxBodyL` is already included. In `dijkstraProducedValue` (and `dijkstraSubTxProducedValue`), add:

```haskell
<> inject (fold . unDirectDeposits $ txBody ^. directDepositsTxBodyL)
```

This ensures that ADA flowing from UTxO inputs to account balances via `directDeposits` is accounted for on the produced side of the conservation equation, preventing double-counting.

  ) =>
  PParams era ->
  (Credential Staking -> Maybe Coin) ->
  (Credential DRepRole -> Maybe Coin) ->

  Value era
getConsumedDijkstraValue pp lookupStakingDeposit lookupDRepDeposit utxo txBody =
  withBothTxLevels
    txBody
    ( \topTxBody ->
        txBodyConsumedValue topTxBody <> subTransactionsConsumedValue topTxBody

    txBodyConsumedValue
