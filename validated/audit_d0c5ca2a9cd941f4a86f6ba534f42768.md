### Title
Deposit Refund Misdirection in Meta Transactions: Relayer Loses Deposited NEAR When Inner Action Fails - (`runtime/runtime/src/actions.rs`)

---

### Summary

When a relayer submits a meta transaction (NEP-366 `DelegateAction`) that includes an attached deposit on an inner action, and that inner action fails on the receiver's shard, the deposit refund is sent to the inner sender (Alice) rather than the relayer who actually paid for it. An unprivileged user can deliberately craft a failing meta transaction with a large deposit to drain the relayer's NEAR balance.

---

### Finding Description

In `apply_delegate_action`, the inner receipt spawned from a `DelegateAction` is constructed with `predecessor_id: sender_id.clone()` — i.e., Alice's account ID — not the relayer's: [1](#0-0) 

The comment at lines 471–475 explicitly acknowledges this:

> "If something goes wrong, deposit is refunded to the predecessor, this is `sender_id`/Sender in DelegateAction." [2](#0-1) 

When the inner receipt fails on the receiver's shard, `refund_unspent_gas_and_deposits` computes `deposit_refund = total_deposit` and calls:

```rust
Receipt::new_balance_refund(receipt.balance_refund_receiver(), deposit_refund)
``` [3](#0-2) 

`balance_refund_receiver()` resolves to the receipt's `predecessor_id`, which is Alice — not the relayer who funded the deposit. The relayer's NEAR is gone; Alice receives it.

The architecture documentation explicitly acknowledges this as a known financial risk:

> "there is a financial incentive for Alice to submit meta transactions that have high balances attached but will fail on Bob's shard." [4](#0-3) 

---

### Impact Explanation

The corrupted protocol value is the **relayer's NEAR account balance**. The relayer pays the deposit when the transaction is converted to a receipt (gas purchase step). If the inner action fails, the deposit refund receipt is addressed to Alice, not the relayer. The relayer's balance is permanently reduced by the deposit amount with no recovery path. This is a direct, on-chain balance corruption for any relayer that accepts meta transactions with attached deposits.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

Any unprivileged NEAR account (Alice) can submit a `SignedDelegateAction` to a public relayer. The attack requires only:
1. Crafting a `DelegateAction` with a non-zero deposit on an inner action that will deterministically fail (e.g., calling a non-existent method, targeting a non-existent account, or attaching a deposit to a function-call-permission key that rejects it).
2. Submitting it to any relayer that does not exhaustively simulate execution before accepting.

No validator, node admin, or special privilege is required. The attack is repeatable and scales linearly with the deposit amount the relayer is willing to forward.

---

### Recommendation

The protocol should either:
1. Propagate the relayer's account ID into the inner receipt's `refund_to` field (using `ActionReceiptV2.refund_to`) so that deposit refunds on failure are directed to the relayer rather than Alice.
2. Or, require relayer implementations to simulate the inner action before forwarding and reject any `DelegateAction` whose inner action is predicted to fail with a non-zero deposit.

Option 1 is a protocol-level fix; option 2 is a relayer-level mitigation. The `ActionReceiptV2` struct already has a `refund_to: Option<AccountId>` field that could be used for this purpose: [5](#0-4) 

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Alice creates a `DelegateAction` targeting `bob.near` with a `FunctionCall` action attaching `10 NEAR` deposit to a method `nonexistent_method`.
2. Alice signs the `DelegateAction` and sends it to a relayer.
3. The relayer wraps it in a transaction, paying `10 NEAR` deposit from its own balance.
4. On Alice's shard, `apply_delegate_action` creates an inner receipt with `predecessor_id = alice.near` and `signer_id = relayer.near`.
5. On Bob's shard, the function call fails (`MethodNotFound`).
6. `refund_unspent_gas_and_deposits` issues `Receipt::new_balance_refund(alice.near, 10 NEAR)`.
7. Alice's balance increases by `10 NEAR`; the relayer's balance is permanently reduced by `10 NEAR`.
8. Gas refund goes to `signer_id = relayer.near`, but the deposit (the large amount) goes to Alice.
