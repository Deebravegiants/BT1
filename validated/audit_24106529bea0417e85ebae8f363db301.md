Audit Report

## Title
stETH Share-Rounding Causes rsETH Over-Minting on Deposit - (File: contracts/LRTDepositPool.sol)

## Summary
`LRTDepositPool.depositAsset` computes `rsethAmountToMint` from the caller-supplied `depositAmount` before executing `safeTransferFrom`. Because stETH is a share-based rebasing token, the contract may receive `depositAmount - 1` wei due to integer division in Lido's share accounting. rsETH is minted against the nominal `depositAmount` while the protocol holds one fewer wei of stETH, creating a persistent, cumulative accounting wedge that dilutes existing rsETH holders.

## Finding Description
In `LRTDepositPool.depositAsset` (lines 110–115), the execution order is:

1. `_beforeDeposit(asset, depositAmount, ...)` computes `rsethAmountToMint = getRsETHAmountToMint(asset, depositAmount)` using the caller-supplied `depositAmount`.
2. `IERC20(asset).safeTransferFrom(msg.sender, address(this), depositAmount)` — for stETH, the contract receives `depositAmount - 1` due to Lido's `sharesOf` → `balanceOf` rounding.
3. `_mintRsETH(rsethAmountToMint)` mints rsETH calculated from `depositAmount`, not from the actual received amount. [1](#0-0) 

The rsETH price is later computed in `LRTOracle._getTotalEthInProtocol` via `getTotalAssetDeposits`, which reads `IERC20(asset).balanceOf(address(this))` — the **actual** balance. [2](#0-1) [3](#0-2) 

This creates a permanent wedge: rsETH supply grows by `rsethAmountToMint` (based on `depositAmount`) while the actual stETH backing grows by only `depositAmount - 1`. The `_checkIfDepositAmountExceedesCurrentLimit` guard also uses the nominal `depositAmount`, not the actual received amount, so it does not prevent the discrepancy. [4](#0-3) 

stETH is an explicitly supported asset in the protocol via `ST_ETH_TOKEN` and `stakeEthForStETH`. [5](#0-4) 

## Impact Explanation
**Low — Contract fails to deliver promised returns, but doesn't lose value.**

Each stETH deposit over-mints rsETH by at most 1 wei worth of stETH. The rsETH price (computed from actual balances) is therefore fractionally lower than it should be after each deposit. Existing rsETH holders are diluted by the 1-wei discrepancy per deposit. The effect is cumulative but negligible per transaction; it does not cause a fund freeze or direct theft.

## Likelihood Explanation
**High.** stETH's share-based accounting rounds down on virtually every non-trivial transfer amount due to integer division in Lido's `getSharesByPooledEth`. Any user depositing stETH via `depositAsset` triggers this path. No special conditions, privileged access, or external manipulation are required.

## Recommendation
Measure the actual received amount by comparing balances before and after the transfer, and use that value for both the rsETH mint calculation and the deposit-limit check:

```solidity
uint256 balanceBefore = IERC20(asset).balanceOf(address(this));
IERC20(asset).safeTransferFrom(msg.sender, address(this), depositAmount);
uint256 actualReceived = IERC20(asset).balanceOf(address(this)) - balanceBefore;

uint256 rsethAmountToMint = _beforeDeposit(asset, actualReceived, minRSETHAmountExpected);
_mintRsETH(rsethAmountToMint);
```

This pattern is the standard mitigation for fee-on-transfer and rebasing tokens.

## Proof of Concept
1. Alice holds stETH such that her balance is `1e18 - 1` after a rebase (shares round down).
2. Alice calls `depositAsset(stETH, 1e18, minRSETH, "")`.
3. `_beforeDeposit` computes `rsethAmountToMint` from `1e18`.
4. `safeTransferFrom` credits the contract with `1e18 - 1` stETH (Lido share rounding).
5. `_mintRsETH` mints rsETH for `1e18`.
6. `getTotalAssetDeposits(stETH)` returns `1e18 - 1` (actual balance).
7. `rsETHPrice` is computed from `1e18 - 1` stETH but rsETH supply includes the extra unit — price is fractionally lower than it should be, diluting all prior holders.

**Foundry fork test plan:** Fork mainnet, deploy/configure the protocol with stETH as a supported asset, call `depositAsset` with a stETH amount that produces a non-round share count, then assert that `getTotalAssetDeposits(stETH) < depositAmount` while `IRSETH(rseth).totalSupply()` increased by the full `rsethAmountToMint`. Repeat N times and assert the cumulative gap grows linearly.
