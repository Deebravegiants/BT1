### Title
Pending Withdrawal Tokens Cease Accruing Rewards During Delay Period, Redistributing Yield to Other Stakers - (File: contracts/KERNEL/KernelDepositPool.sol)

### Summary
In `KernelDepositPool.sol`, when a user calls `initiateWithdrawal()`, their `balanceOf` and `totalKernelStaked` are immediately decremented, even though the tokens remain locked in the contract for up to 30 days (`MAX_WITHDRAWAL_DELAY`). Because reward accrual is computed solely from `balanceOf[_account]`, the withdrawing user earns zero rewards on their pending tokens during the entire delay window. Those rewards are instead redistributed to remaining stakers, constituting theft of unclaimed yield.

### Finding Description

`initiateWithdrawal()` immediately reduces both `balanceOf[msg.sender]` and `totalKernelStaked` at the moment of initiation: [1](#0-0) 

The `earned()` function computes rewards exclusively from `balanceOf[_account]`: [2](#0-1) 

And `rewardPerToken()` divides by `totalKernelStaked`: [3](#0-2) 

Because `totalKernelStaked` is reduced immediately, the reward rate per token for remaining stakers increases. The withdrawing user's tokens are still physically held by the contract (they cannot be used elsewhere), yet they contribute zero to that user's reward accrual and zero to the denominator — meaning the full reward stream that would have been attributed to those tokens is silently redistributed to other stakers.

The withdrawal delay can be up to `MAX_WITHDRAWAL_DELAY = 30 days`: [4](#0-3) 

### Impact Explanation

**High — Theft of unclaimed yield.**

A user who initiates a withdrawal of `X` KERNEL tokens loses all staking rewards on `X` for the entire delay period (up to 30 days). Those rewards are not burned or returned to the protocol treasury — they are redistributed proportionally to all remaining stakers. The withdrawing user suffers a direct, quantifiable loss of yield that accrues to other participants. With a 30-day delay and a meaningful reward rate, the loss per user can be substantial.

### Likelihood Explanation

**High.** Every user who calls `initiateWithdrawal()` is unconditionally affected. This is a standard, expected user action with no special preconditions. The delay is mandatory and can be up to 30 days. No attacker coordination is required — the loss occurs automatically for any withdrawing staker.

### Recommendation

Decouple the accounting used for reward accrual from the accounting used for withdrawal eligibility. One approach: introduce a separate `pendingWithdrawalBalance` mapping that tracks tokens in the delay queue, and include it in the reward calculation (`balanceOf[account] + pendingWithdrawalBalance[account]`). Only remove tokens from the reward-bearing balance when `claimWithdrawal()` is called and tokens are actually transferred out. Alternatively, do not reduce `totalKernelStaked` until `claimWithdrawal()` is executed.

### Proof of Concept

1. Alice stakes 1000 KERNEL. `balanceOf[Alice] = 1000`, `totalKernelStaked = 1000`.
2. Bob stakes 1000 KERNEL. `balanceOf[Bob] = 1000`, `totalKernelStaked = 2000`.
3. Admin calls `notifyRewardAmount(2000e18)` with a 30-day duration → `rewardRate = 2000e18 / 30 days`.
4. Alice calls `initiateWithdrawal(1000)`. Immediately: `balanceOf[Alice] = 0`, `totalKernelStaked = 1000`. Alice's 1000 KERNEL are locked in the contract for 30 days.
5. Over the next 30 days, `rewardPerToken()` accrues at `rewardRate / 1000` (only Bob's tokens in denominator). Bob earns the full 2000e18 reward.
6. Alice calls `claimWithdrawal()` and receives her 1000 KERNEL back, but `earned(Alice) = 0` — she received no rewards for the 30-day period despite her tokens being locked in the contract the entire time.
7. Had Alice not initiated a withdrawal, she would have earned ~1000e18 reward tokens (50% of the pool). Those rewards went entirely to Bob. [5](#0-4) [2](#0-1)

```text
    function initiateWithdrawal(uint256 _amount) external nonReentrant updateReward(msg.sender) {
        if (_amount == 0) revert AmountZero();
        if (balanceOf[msg.sender] < _amount) revert InsufficientStakedBalance();
        if (userWithdrawalIds[msg.sender].length >= maxNumberOfWithdrawalsPerUser) revert WithdrawalLimitReached();

        balanceOf[msg.sender] -= _amount;
        totalKernelStaked -= _amount;

        // Create a withdrawal record
        uint256 withdrawalId = ++withdrawalCounter;
        uint256 unlockTime = block.timestamp + withdrawalDelay;

        withdrawals[withdrawalId] = Withdrawal({
            user: msg.sender, amount: _amount, unlockTime: unlockTime, claimed: false, withdrawalId: withdrawalId
        });
        userWithdrawalIds[msg.sender].push(withdrawalId);

        emit WithdrawalInitiated(msg.sender, _amount, withdrawalId, unlockTime);
```
