### Title
`directDeposits` Omitted from Preservation-of-Value Accounting Enables ADA Creation - (File: `eras/dijkstra/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Dijkstra/UTxO.hs`)

---

### Summary

In the Dijkstra era, the `directDeposits` field of a transaction body is applied to staking account balances by the ENTITIES/SUBENTITIES rules, but is never included in the `produced` side of the preservation-of-value (`consumed == produced`) check. An unprivileged transaction author can exploit this omission to create ADA out of thin air by including the direct-deposit amount in UTxO outputs (to satisfy the conservation check) while simultaneously having the same amount credited to account balances via `applyDirectDeposits`.

---

### Finding Description

The Dijkstra era introduces a `directDeposits` field (`DirectDeposits`, a `Map AccountAddress Coin`) in both top-level and sub-transaction bodies. Its purpose is to move ADA from the transaction into registered staking account balances.

**Step 1 – `directDeposits` is applied to account balances.**

In `dijkstraEntitiesTransition`:

```haskell
pure $ certStateAfterCerts & certDStateL . accountsL %~ applyDirectDeposits directDeposits
``` [1](#0-0) 

And identically in `dijkstraSubEntitiesTransition` for sub-transactions:

```haskell
pure $ certStateAfterSubCerts & certDStateL . accountsL %~ applyDirectDeposits directDeposits
``` [2](#0-1) 

**Step 2 – `directDeposits` is absent from the `produced` value.**

For top-level transactions, `dijkstraProducedValue` sums `conwayProducedValue` (outputs + fees + deposits + treasury donation) plus sub-transaction produced values. No `directDeposits` term appears:

```haskell
dijkstraProducedValue pp isRegPoolId txBody =
  conwayProducedValue pp isRegPoolId txBody
    <> foldMap'
      (getProducedValue pp isRegPoolId . view bodyTxL)
      (txBody ^. subTransactionsTxBodyL)
``` [3](#0-2) 

For sub-transactions, `dijkstraSubTxProducedValue` is:

```haskell
dijkstraSubTxProducedValue pp isRegPoolId txBody =
  sumAllValue (txBody ^. outputsTxBodyL)
    <> inject (getTotalDepositsTxBody pp isRegPoolId txBody <> txBody ^. treasuryDonationTxBodyL)
    <> burnedMultiAssets txBody
``` [4](#0-3) 

`directDeposits` is also absent from `getConsumedDijkstraValue`, which delegates to `getConsumedMaryValue` (inputs + withdrawals + refunds + mint): [5](#0-4) 

**Step 3 – The conservation check does not see `directDeposits`.**

`dijkstraUtxoTransition` enforces:

```haskell
{- consumed pp utxo₀ txb = produced pp certState txb -}
runTest $ Shelley.validateValueNotConservedUTxO pp originalUtxo certState txBody
``` [6](#0-5) 

`validateValueNotConservedUTxO` compares `consumed pp certState utxo txBody` against `produced pp certState txBody`. Because `directDeposits` appears in neither, the check is blind to it. [7](#0-6) 

**Step 4 – The only validation on `directDeposits` is account existence.**

The ENTITIES rule only checks that target accounts are registered:

```haskell
failOnJust (directDepositsMissingAccounts directDeposits accountsAfterCerts) $
  injectFailure . DirectDepositsToMissingAccounts
``` [8](#0-7) 

There is no check that the amounts are funded by the transaction's inputs.

---

### Impact Explanation

**Critical. Direct creation of ADA through an invalid ledger state transition.**

Concrete attack scenario (amounts simplified):

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| UTxO input | 1 000 ADA |
| UTxO output (to attacker) | 999 ADA |
| Fee | 1 ADA |
| `directDeposits` → attacker's staking account | 99 ADA |

Conservation check: `consumed = 1 000 ADA`, `produced = 999 + 1 = 1 000 ADA` → **passes**.

After the transaction:
- UTxO: attacker holds 999 ADA
- Account balance: attacker's staking account holds 99 ADA
- Fee pot: 1 ADA
- **Total: 1 099 ADA** (started with 1 000 ADA → **99 ADA created**)

The 99 ADA in `directDeposits` is double-counted: it is included in the UTxO output (to satisfy the conservation check) and simultaneously credited to the account balance by `applyDirectDeposits`. This violates the fundamental preservation-of-value invariant of the Cardano ledger.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**High.** Any transaction author in the Dijkstra era (protocol version 12+) can craft such a transaction. No special privileges, keys, or governance actions are required. The attacker only needs a registered staking account (trivially obtained) and the ability to submit a transaction. The exploit is deterministic and repeatable.

---

### Recommendation

Include `directDeposits` in the `produced` value calculation, analogously to how `treasuryDonationTxBodyL` is already included. In `dijkstraProducedValue` (for top-level transactions) and `dijkstraSubTxProducedValue` (for sub-transactions), add:

```haskell
<> inject (foldMap id . unDirectDeposits $ txBody ^. directDepositsTxBodyL)
```

This ensures that ADA directed to account balances via `directDeposits` is accounted for on the produced side of the preservation-of-value equation, preventing double-counting.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Register a staking credential and obtain `accountAddress`.
2. Construct a Dijkstra `TopTx` with:
   - `inputs` = `{utxoIn}` (containing `N` ADA)
   - `outputs` = `[txOut (N - fee) ADA]`  ← includes the direct-deposit amount
   - `fee` = `fee`
   - `directDeposits` = `{accountAddress: D ADA}` where `D < N - fee`
3. Submit the transaction. The `validateValueNotConservedUTxO` check passes because `consumed = N` and `produced = (N - fee) + fee = N`.
4. Observe that the UTxO output contains `N - fee` ADA **and** the staking account balance has been credited `D` ADA by `applyDirectDeposits`.
5. Total ADA in the system has increased by `D`.

The root cause is the structural omission of `directDeposits` from `dijkstraProducedValue` and `dijkstraSubTxProducedValue` in `eras/dijkstra/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Dijkstra/UTxO.hs`, mirroring the external report's pattern of a value type that participates in state changes but is absent from the critical accounting set.
