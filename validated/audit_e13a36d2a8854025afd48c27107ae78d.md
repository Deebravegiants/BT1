### Title
Last-Minute DRep Stake Delegation Manipulates Governance Ratification Without Temporal Commitment â€” (`File: eras/conway/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/Governance.hs`)

---

### Summary

The Conway governance ratification mechanism computes the DRep stake distribution from the **current** `instantStake` snapshotted at the epoch boundary. Unlike the SPO stake distribution (which uses a 2-epoch-old "markâ†’setâ†’go" snapshot), the DRep distribution has no temporal delay. An attacker holding a large amount of ADA can delegate to a strategically chosen DRep in the last block of epoch *n*, have that stake counted in the ratification snapshot, cause a governance action to be enacted at the epoch *n+1* boundary, and then undelegate in epoch *n+1* â€” all without any long-term governance participation.

---

### Finding Description

**Vulnerability class:** Governance ratification flaw â€” last-minute stake manipulation.

**Root cause â€” `setFreshDRepPulsingState`:**

At every epoch boundary, `setFreshDRepPulsingState` initialises a fresh `DRepPulser` by snapshotting the live ledger state:

```haskell
instantStake = utxoState ^. instantStakeG          -- line 482
...
dpInstantStake = instantStake  -- used as part of the snapshot  -- line 501
dpAccounts    = dState ^. accountsL                -- line 499 (delegation map)
dpProposals   = proposalsActions props             -- line 510 (includes all votes cast so far)
``` [1](#0-0) 

`instantStake` is the `utxosInstantStake` field of `UTxOState`, which is updated **incrementally with every transaction** â€” it is a pure function of the current UTxO:

```haskell
utxosInstantStake =
  deleteInstantStake deletedUTxO (addInstantStake utxoAdd (utxos ^. instantStakeL))
``` [2](#0-1) 

Therefore, a UTxO output with a staking credential delegated to a DRep that is created in the **last block of epoch n** is immediately reflected in `instantStake` and is captured in `dpInstantStake` at the epoch boundary.

**Contrast with SPO stake distribution:**

The SPO pool distribution used for ratification (`dpStakePoolDistr`) is passed in from `nesPd`, which is set to `ssStakeMarkPoolDistr` â€” the mark snapshot from the **previous** epoch boundary:

```haskell
let pd' = ssStakeMarkPoolDistr (esSnapshots es)
...
pure $ src { nesPd = pd' }
``` [3](#0-2) 

The mark snapshot itself is taken at the current epoch boundary and only becomes the "go" (active) snapshot two epochs later. This 2-epoch delay is the standard Shelley "markâ†’setâ†’go" mechanism. **No equivalent delay exists for the DRep distribution.**

**How the snapshot feeds ratification:**

`finishDRepPulser` calls `computeDRepDistr` with the snapshotted `dpInstantStake` and `dpAccounts`, producing `reDRepDistr` in the `RatifyEnv`. `dRepAcceptedRatio` then iterates over `reDRepDistr` to compute the YES/total ratio:

```haskell
dRepAcceptedRatio RatifyEnv {reDRepDistr, reDRepState, reCurrentEpoch} gasDRepVotes govAction =
  toInteger yesStake %? toInteger totalExcludingAbstainStake
  where
    accumStake (!yes, !tot) drep (CompactCoin stake) = ...
    (yesStake, totalExcludingAbstainStake) = Map.foldlWithKey' accumStake (0, 0) reDRepDistr
``` [4](#0-3) 

**No minimum delegation period exists.** The `DelegTxCert` certificate is accepted by the DELEG rule at any time during an epoch, and the resulting delegation is immediately reflected in `dpAccounts` and `dpInstantStake` at the next epoch boundary.

**Vote removal on DRep deregistration does not mitigate this.** The GOV rule removes a DRep's votes when they deregister:

```haskell
cleanupVoters gas = gas & gasDRepVotesL %~ (`Map.withoutKeys` unregisteredDReps)
``` [5](#0-4) 

However, the attack does not require the attacker to deregister the DRep. The attacker delegates to an **existing** DRep that has already voted YES. The attacker's stake amplifies that DRep's weight in `reDRepDistr` without touching the vote record itself.

---

### Impact Explanation

**Critical. Unauthorized governance, treasury, protocol-parameter, committee, constitution, or hard-fork action is enacted.**

By temporarily delegating sufficient ADA to a YES-voting DRep just before the epoch boundary, an attacker can push any governance action â€” `TreasuryWithdrawals`, `ParameterChange`, `UpdateCommittee`, `NewConstitution`, `HardForkInitiation` â€” past its ratification threshold. The action is then enacted at the following epoch boundary via the ENACT rule. The attacker's ADA is never at risk and can be undelegated immediately after the snapshot is taken.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

**Low-to-Medium.** The attack requires enough ADA to swing the DRep YES ratio from its current value to above the applicable threshold (e.g., `dvtTreasuryWithdrawal`, `dvtCommitteeNormal`). On mainnet this is a large capital requirement. However:

- All votes are public; the attacker can observe the exact shortfall and compute the precise amount needed.
- The capital is not destroyed or locked permanently â€” it is only delegated for one epoch boundary crossing (a few seconds of wall-clock time at the boundary).
- No fees beyond standard transaction costs are incurred.
- The attack is fully deterministic and requires no privileged access.

For governance actions that are already close to the threshold (e.g., 1â€“2% below), the required capital is proportionally smaller and the attack becomes practical for any large ADA holder or entity that can temporarily move exchange-held ADA.

---

### Recommendation

Introduce a temporal commitment requirement for DRep stake to count toward ratification, analogous to the "markâ†’setâ†’go" delay used for SPO stake. Concretely:

1. **Use a time-delayed DRep stake snapshot** â€” take the DRep distribution from the mark snapshot (or an equivalent epoch-boundary-minus-one snapshot) rather than the live `instantStake`. This ensures that stake delegated in the last block of epoch *n* does not influence ratification until epoch *n+2* at the earliest.
2. Alternatively, **record the epoch in which each delegation was made** and exclude delegations that are younger than one full epoch from the DRep distribution computation in `computeDRepDistr`.

---

### Proof of Concept

**Setup:** A governance action `TreasuryWithdrawals` is live. The current DRep YES ratio is 64%, just below the `dvtTreasuryWithdrawal` threshold of 67%. DRep `D` has voted YES and controls 30% of the active DRep stake.

**Attack steps:**

1. **Epoch n, last block:** Attacker submits a transaction that:
   - Registers a staking credential `C` (deposit: ~2 ADA, refundable).
   - Delegates `C` to DRep `D` via `DelegTxCert C (DelegVote (DRepCredential D))`.
   - Sends enough ADA to an address with staking reference `C` to push D's share above 67%.

2. **Epoch n boundary:** `setFreshDRepPulsingState` runs. `instantStake` now includes the attacker's ADA under credential `C`. `dpAccounts` records `C â†’ DRep D`. Both are frozen into the `DRepPulser` snapshot. [6](#0-5) 

3. **Epoch n+1 (pulsing):** `computeDRepDistr` iterates over `dpAccounts`, finds `C` delegated to `D`, looks up `C` in `dpInstantStake`, and adds the attacker's ADA to `D`'s entry in `reDRepDistr`. [7](#0-6) 

4. **Epoch n+1 boundary:** `dRepAcceptedRatio` computes YES/(YES+NO+abstain) using the inflated `reDRepDistr`. The ratio now exceeds 67%. `dRepAccepted` returns `True`. The treasury withdrawal is enacted. [8](#0-7) 

5. **Epoch n+1, any block:** Attacker undelegates `C` and withdraws the staking deposit. Net cost: transaction fees only.

```haskell
dRepAcceptedRatio ::
  forall era.
  RatifyEnv era ->
  Map (Credential DRepRole) Vote ->
  GovAction era ->
  Rational
dRepAcceptedRatio RatifyEnv {reDRepDistr, reDRepState, reCurrentEpoch} gasDRepVotes govAction =
  toInteger yesStake %? toInteger totalExcludingAbstainStake
  where
    accumStake (!yes, !tot) drep (CompactCoin stake) =
      case drep of
        DRepCredential cred ->
          case Map.lookup cred reDRepState of
            Nothing -> (yes, tot) -- drep is not registered, so we don't consider it
            Just drepState
              | reCurrentEpoch > drepExpiry drepState -> (yes, tot) -- drep is expired, so we don't consider it
              | otherwise ->
                  case Map.lookup cred gasDRepVotes of
                    -- drep hasn't voted for this action, so we don't count
                    -- the vote but we consider it in the denominator:
                    Nothing -> (yes, tot + stake)
                    Just VoteYes -> (yes + stake, tot + stake)
                    Just Abstain -> (yes, tot)
                    Just VoteNo -> (yes, tot + stake)
        DRepAlwaysNoConfidence ->
          case govAction of
            NoConfidence _ -> (yes + stake, tot + stake)
            _ -> (yes, tot + stake)
        DRepAlwaysAbstain -> (yes, tot)
    (yesStake, totalExcludingAbstainStake) = Map.foldlWithKey' accumStake (0, 0) reDRepDistr
