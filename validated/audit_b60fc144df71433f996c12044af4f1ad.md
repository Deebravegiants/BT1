### Title
DRep Unregistration Incorrectly Clears Stake Credential Delegation Due to Stale Reverse-Delegation Entries During Bootstrap Phase - (File: `eras/conway/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/Rules/GovCert.hs`)

---

### Summary

During the Conway bootstrap phase (protocol version < 10), the `ConwayUnRegDRep` certificate handler in `conwayGovCertTransition` incorrectly clears the DRep delegation of stake credentials that have already redelegated away from the unregistering DRep. This is caused by stale entries in the DRep's `drepDelegs` reverse-delegation set, which are not cleaned up during redelegation in the bootstrap phase. An attacker who controls a DRep can exploit this to silently nullify the DRep delegation of any victim who previously delegated to them and then moved on, causing those victims to fail the post-bootstrap `ConwayWdrlNotDelegatedToDRep` withdrawal check until they manually redelegate.

---

### Finding Description

**Component 1 — Stale reverse-delegation in `processDelegationInternal` (`Deleg.hs`)**

When a stake credential redelegates from DRep A to DRep B, `processDelegationInternal` is called with `preserveIncorrectDelegation = (pvMajor pv < natVersion @10)`. During bootstrap this flag is `True`, so the branch at line 363 only *adds* the stake credential to DRep B's `drepDelegs` without *removing* it from DRep A's `drepDelegs`:

```haskell
| isNothing mAccountState || preserveIncorrectDelegation ->
    certVStateL . vsDRepsL
      %~ Map.adjust (drepDelegsL %~ Set.insert stakeCred) dRepCred
```

The accounts map is correctly updated (`dRepDelegationAccountStateL ?~ dRep` now points to DRep B), but DRep A's `drepDelegs` still contains the stake credential as a stale entry.

**Component 2 — `ConwayUnRegDRep` trusts `drepDelegs` unconditionally (`GovCert.hs`)**

When DRep A is later unregistered, the handler reads `drepDelegs` from DRep A's `DRepState` and calls `clearDRepDelegations` on every credential in that set:

```haskell
clearDRepDelegations delegs accountsMap =
  foldr (Map.adjust (dRepDelegationAccountStateL .~ Nothing)) accountsMap delegs
...
certState'
  & certDStateL . accountsL . accountsMapL
    %~ clearDRepDelegations (drepDelegs dRepState)
```

Because the stake credential is still in DRep A's `drepDelegs` (stale entry from Component 1), `clearDRepDelegations` sets the credential's `dRepDelegationAccountStateL` to `Nothing` in the accounts map — even though the credential has already redelegated to DRep B and DRep A has no legitimate claim over it.

**Component 3 — Hardfork to v10 does not recover the lost delegation (`HardFork.hs`)**

`updateDRepDelegations` (called at the v10 hardfork) rebuilds all `drepDelegs` from the accounts map. Since the victim's `dRepDelegationAccountStateL` is now `Nothing`, the credential is not added to any DRep's `drepDelegs`, and the victim ends up with no DRep delegation after the hardfork.

**Component 4 — Post-bootstrap withdrawals require DRep delegation (`Ledger.hs`)**

`validateWithdrawalsDelegated` enforces that every withdrawal key hash has a non-`Nothing` `dRepDelegationAccountStateL`:

```haskell
isNotDRepDelegated keyHash = isNothing $ do
  accountState <- lookupAccountState (KeyHashObj keyHash) accounts
  accountState ^. dRepDelegationAccountStateL
...
failureOnNonEmpty nonExistentDelegations ConwayWdrlNotDelegatedToDRep
```

The victim's rewards are frozen until they submit a new delegation certificate.

### Impact Explanation

An attacker-controlled `UnRegDRepTxCert` certificate modifies the withdrawal capability of third-party stake credentials outside design parameters. The `ConwayUnRegDRep` rule is designed to clear delegations only for credentials *currently* delegated to the unregistering DRep. The bug causes it to also clear delegations for credentials that *previously* delegated to that DRep but have since moved on. After the hardfork to protocol version 10, affected victims receive `ConwayWdrlNotDelegatedToDRep` on any withdrawal attempt until they submit a new `DelegTxCert`. This matches the **Medium** impact category: attacker-controlled certificates modify withdrawals outside design parameters.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

- Requires the network to be in the Conway bootstrap phase (protocol version < 10). On mainnet this phase has passed; the bug remains live on testnets or private networks that have not yet crossed the v10 boundary.
- The attacker must operate a registered DRep and wait for victims to delegate and then redelegate away — a realistic scenario given that DRep operators can observe on-chain delegation events.
- No privileged access, leaked keys, or consensus majority is required. A single `UnRegDRepTxCert` transaction suffices.
- The bug is explicitly acknowledged in the codebase (issue #4772, comment at `Deleg.hs:322`) and preserved intentionally for backward compatibility, confirming it is a real, reachable code path.

---

### Recommendation

1. In `conwayGovCertTransition` (`GovCert.hs`), the `clearDRepDelegations` function should cross-check each credential in `drepDelegs` against the accounts map before clearing: only clear the delegation if `dRepDelegationAccountStateL` still points to the unregistering DRep.
2. Alternatively, `processDelegationInternal` should be fixed to always remove the stale reverse-delegation entry even during bootstrap, eliminating the root cause. The `preserveIncorrectDelegation` flag exists solely for backward compatibility and should be removed once the bootstrap phase is definitively over on all supported networks.
3. The `updateDRepDelegations` hardfork migration already partially mitigates this by rebuilding delegations from the accounts map, but it cannot recover a delegation that was incorrectly set to `Nothing` before the hardfork.

### Proof of Concept

The bug is explicitly documented and tested in the repository.

**Hardfork migration cannot recover the lost delegation:** [3](#0-2) 

`updateDRepDelegations` rebuilds from the accounts map; a credential whose `dRepDelegationAccountStateL` is `Nothing` is silently dropped.

**Post-bootstrap withdrawal check blocks the victim:** [4](#0-3) 

**Test confirming the buggy behavior during bootstrap:** [5](#0-4) 

The test explicitly asserts that during bootstrap, after DRep A unregisters, `lookupDRepDelegation cred accounts` returns `Nothing` — confirming the victim's delegation is incorrectly cleared — while DRep B's `drepDelegs` still contains the credential (inconsistent state).

**Attack sequence:**
1. Victim registers `cred` and delegates to DRep A (`RegDepositDelegTxCert cred (DelegVote (DRepCredential drepA)) deposit`) during bootstrap.
2. Victim redelegates to DRep B (`DelegTxCert cred (DelegVote (DRepCredential drepB))`). Accounts map now points to DRep B, but DRep A's `drepDelegs` still contains `cred`.
3. Attacker submits `UnRegDRepTxCert drepA refund`. `clearDRepDelegations` sets `cred`'s `dRepDelegationAccountStateL` to `Nothing`.
4. Network hardforks to v10. `updateDRepDelegations` rebuilds delegations; `cred` has no delegation in accounts, so it is not added to DRep B's `drepDelegs`.
5. Victim attempts withdrawal. `validateWithdrawalsDelegated` finds `isNotDRepDelegated cred = True` and rejects with `ConwayWdrlNotDelegatedToDRep`.

  -- | Account state for the above stake credential

  -- | New delegatee

processDelegationInternal preserveIncorrectDelegation stakeCred mAccountState newDelegatee =
  case newDelegatee of
    DelegStake sPool -> delegStake sPool
    DelegVote dRep -> delegVote dRep
    DelegStakeVote sPool dRep -> delegVote dRep . delegStake sPool

    delegStake stakePool cState =
      cState
        & certDStateL . accountsL
          %~ adjustAccountState (stakePoolDelegationAccountStateL ?~ stakePool) stakeCred

          (certPStateL . psStakePoolsL %~ Map.adjust (spsDelegatorsL %~ Set.insert stakeCred) stakePool)
          (\accountState -> certPStateL %~ unDelegReDelegStakePool stakeCred accountState (Just stakePool))
          mAccountState

      let handleReverseDelegation =
            case dRepToCred dRep of

                --
                -- 2) in the bootstrap phase, in order to preserve the incorrect behavior, where old reverse

updateDRepDelegations :: ConwayEraCertState era => CertState era -> CertState era
updateDRepDelegations certState =
  let accountsMap = certState ^. certDStateL . accountsL . accountsMapL
      dReps =
        -- Reset all delegations in order to remove any inconsistencies
        -- Delegations will be reset accordingly below.
        Map.map (\dRepState -> dRepState {drepDelegs = Set.empty}) $
          certState ^. certVStateL . vsDRepsL
      (dRepsWithDelegations, accountsWithoutUnknownDRepDelegations) =
        Map.mapAccumWithKey adjustDelegations dReps accountsMap
      adjustDelegations ds stakeCred accountState =
        case accountState ^. dRepDelegationAccountStateL of
          Just (DRepCredential dRep) ->
            let addDelegation _ dRepState =
                  Just $ dRepState {drepDelegs = Set.insert stakeCred (drepDelegs dRepState)}
             in case Map.updateLookupWithKey addDelegation dRep ds of
                  (Nothing, _) -> (ds, accountState & dRepDelegationAccountStateL .~ Nothing)
                  (Just _, ds') -> (ds', accountState)
          _ -> (ds, accountState)
   in certState
        -- Remove dangling delegations to non-existent DReps:
        & certDStateL . accountsL . accountsMapL .~ accountsWithoutUnknownDRepDelegations
        -- Populate DRep delegations with delegatees
