### Title
Stale `rsETHPrice` Used in `instantWithdrawal()` and `initiateWithdrawal()` Causes Users to Receive Less Collateral Than Entitled — (File: `contracts/LRTWithdrawalManager.sol`)

---

### Summary

`LRTOracle.rsETHPrice` is a cached storage variable updated only when `updateRSETHPrice()` is explicitly called. Neither `instantWithdrawal()` nor `initiateWithdrawal()` in `LRTWithdrawalManager` refreshes this price before computing the asset payout. Users who do not manually call `updateRSETHPrice()` before withdrawing receive fewer assets than the current exchange rate entitles them to, losing accrued yield.

---

### Finding Description

`LRTOracle.rsETHPrice` is a stored value set only by `_updateRsETHPrice()`, which is invoked via the public `updateRSETHPrice()` or the manager-only `updateRSETHPriceAsManager()`. [1](#0-0) [2](#0-1) 

`getExpectedAssetAmount()` in `LRTWithdrawalManager` reads this cached price directly:

```solidity
underlyingToReceive = amount * lrtOracle.rsETHPrice() / lrtOracle.getAssetPrice(asset);
``` [3](#0-2) 

**Case 1 — `instantWithdrawal()`:**

The function computes `assetAmountUnlocked` from the stale price, then immediately burns the user's rsETH, and transfers the (understated) asset amount:

```solidity
uint256 assetAmountUnlocked = getExpectedAssetAmount(asset, rsETHUnstaked);
IRSETH(lrtConfig.rsETH()).burnFrom(address(msg.sender), rsETHUnstaked);
...
_transferAsset(asset, msg.sender, userAmount);
``` [4](#0-3) 

There is no call to `updateRSETHPrice()` before this sequence. The rsETH is burned at a price that may be lower than the true current price, and the shortfall remains in the protocol.

**Case 2 — `initiateWithdrawal()`:**

`expectedAssetAmount` is computed with the stale price and stored as the ceiling for the user's payout:

```solidity
uint256 expectedAssetAmount = getExpectedAssetAmount(asset, rsETHUnstaked);
...
assetsCommitted[asset] += expectedAssetAmount;
_addUserWithdrawalRequest(asset, rsETHUnstaked, expectedAssetAmount);
``` [5](#0-4) 

Later, `_calculatePayoutAmount()` enforces `min(expectedAssetAmount, currentReturn)`:

```solidity
uint256 currentReturn = (request.rsETHUnstaked * rsETHPrice) / assetPrice;
return (request.expectedAssetAmount < currentReturn) ? request.expectedAssetAmount : currentReturn;
``` [6](#0-5) 

If the price rises between initiation and unlock (the normal case as restaking rewards accrue), the user is permanently capped at the lower stale amount. The rsETH burned in `unlockQueue()` is worth more than the assets disbursed; the surplus accrues to remaining rsETH holders at the withdrawing user's expense. [7](#0-6) 

---

### Impact Explanation

**High — Theft of unclaimed yield.**

In `instantWithdrawal()`, the user burns rsETH and receives fewer underlying assets than the current rsETH/asset exchange rate entitles them to. The shortfall is not returned; it stays in the protocol, diluting the withdrawing user's share. In `initiateWithdrawal()`, the stale `expectedAssetAmount` permanently caps the payout even after the price rises, causing the same yield loss at unlock time.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

`updateRSETHPrice()` is not called automatically within any withdrawal path. The price can be stale for hours or days between operator-triggered updates. Any user who calls `instantWithdrawal()` or `initiateWithdrawal()` without first calling `updateRSETHPrice()` is affected. Because the protocol does not document or enforce this prerequisite, the vast majority of users will not perform this step, making the impact routine rather than edge-case.

---

### Recommendation

Call `updateRSETHPrice()` (or an internal equivalent) at the start of `instantWithdrawal()` and `initiateWithdrawal()`, before `getExpectedAssetAmount()` is invoked, so that the price used to compute the payout reflects all accrued rewards up to the moment of withdrawal. This mirrors the fix recommended for the USDs analog: refresh the yield-bearing state before any token burn or commitment.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Assume `rsETHPrice` was last updated 12 hours ago at `1.05 ETH/rsETH`. Since then, restaking rewards have pushed the true price to `1.06 ETH/rsETH`, but `updateRSETHPrice()` has not been called.
2. Alice calls `instantWithdrawal(ETH, 100e18, "")` without first calling `updateRSETHPrice()`.
3. `getExpectedAssetAmount` returns `100 * 1.05 / 1.0 = 105 ETH` (stale price).
4. Alice's 100 rsETH is burned; she receives 105 ETH (minus fee).
5. Had `updateRSETHPrice()` been called first, she would have received `100 * 1.06 = 106 ETH`.
6. Alice permanently loses 1 ETH of accrued yield. The shortfall remains in the protocol, benefiting all other rsETH holders at Alice's expense.

The same scenario applies to `initiateWithdrawal()`: the stale `expectedAssetAmount = 105 ETH` is stored, and even after `unlockQueue()` is called with the updated price of `1.06`, `_calculatePayoutAmount` returns `min(105, 106) = 105 ETH`, permanently locking Alice out of her 1 ETH of yield.
