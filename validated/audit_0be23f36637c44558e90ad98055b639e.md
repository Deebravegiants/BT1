### Title
Non-Winning `propose_update` Deposits Permanently Absorbed by Contract on `do_update` — (`crates/contract/src/update.rs`)

### Summary
When `do_update` executes a winning proposal, it unconditionally clears all other pending proposals via `self.entries.clear()` without issuing any refund promises to the proposers of those non-winning entries. The NEAR deposited by non-winning proposers is permanently absorbed into the contract balance with no recovery path.

### Finding Description
`propose_update` requires a storage-proportional deposit from each proposer: [1](#0-0) 

Only the excess above `required` is refunded at proposal time: [2](#0-1) 

When a proposal reaches threshold and `do_update` is called, it clears all remaining entries: [3](#0-2) 

There is no loop over the cleared entries to emit `Promise::transfer` refunds to their respective proposers. On NEAR, freeing storage returns the staked NEAR to the **contract account's balance**, not to the original depositor. The proposers of non-winning updates have no mechanism to recover their deposits.

### Impact Explanation
Every participant who called `propose_update` for a non-winning proposal permanently loses their deposit (sized at `storage_byte_cost × bytes_used`). For a contract binary update this is on the order of several NEAR (a 1 MB WASM at ~10 yoctoNEAR/byte = ~10 NEAR). The contract balance grows by the sum of all non-winning deposits with no disbursement path. This breaks the accounting invariant: *every `propose_update` deposit must be either refunded or consumed by the winning update*.

### Likelihood Explanation
Any scenario where two or more participants independently propose competing updates — a normal operational pattern during governance — triggers the loss. No collusion above threshold is required; the loss occurs as a natural consequence of the winning proposal being executed. The README explicitly documents that all proposals are cleared on execution, confirming this is the live production path. [4](#0-3) 

### Recommendation
In `do_update`, before calling `self.entries.clear()`, iterate over the remaining entries and emit a `Promise::transfer` to each entry's original proposer for the amount they deposited. This requires storing the proposer's `AccountId` alongside each `UpdateEntry` at proposal time. [5](#0-4) 

Alternatively, track a `depositor: AccountId` and `deposit: NearToken` field in `UpdateEntry` and refund them during the clear.

### Proof of Concept
The existing test `test_proposed_updates_do_update_clears_all_state` already demonstrates the state-clearing behavior but does not assert on refunds (because none are emitted): [6](#0-5) 

A concrete unit test would:
1. Have participant A call `propose_update(update_A)` with deposit `D_A`.
2. Have participant B call `propose_update(update_B)` with deposit `D_B`.
3. Have threshold participants call `vote_update(B_id)` until `do_update` fires.
4. Assert that no `Promise::transfer` to A's account was scheduled and that the contract balance increased by `D_A` — confirming A's deposit is silently consumed.
