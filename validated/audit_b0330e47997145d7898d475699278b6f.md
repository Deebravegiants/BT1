Audit Report

## Title
JIT Liquidity Sandwich Extracts LP Fees From Pre-Existing Bin Providers — (`metric-core/contracts/libraries/LiquidityLib.sol`, `metric-core/contracts/libraries/SwapMath.sol`)

## Summary
LP fees from every swap are deposited directly into the bin's token balance and distributed pro-rata to all current shareholders at removal time. Because `addLiquidity` and `removeLiquidity` impose no lockup period, cooldown, or withdrawal fee, and the `BinState` struct contains no fee-growth checkpoint, an MEV actor can sandwich any large swap: add shares before the swap, let the swap inflate the bin balance with LP fees, then immediately remove shares and extract a proportional cut of fees that should have accrued entirely to pre-existing LPs.

## Finding Description
**Fee accumulation into bin balance**

In `SwapMath.buyToken0InBinSpecifiedOut` (and the three symmetric functions), the LP fee net of protocol cut is added directly to `binState.token1BalanceScaled`:

```solidity
// SwapMath.sol L409-427
uint256 feeAmountScaled = Math.ceilDiv(amountInScaled * currBinBuyFeeX64, ONE_X64);
amountInScaled += feeAmountScaled;
uint256 protocolFeeAmountScaled = (feeAmountScaled * spreadFeeE6) / 1e6;
binState.token1BalanceScaled =
  (uint256(binState.token1BalanceScaled) + amountInScaled - protocolFeeAmountScaled).toUint104();
binLpFeeAmount = feeAmountScaled - protocolFeeAmountScaled;
```

There is no separate fee accumulator; the LP fee simply inflates the bin balance.

**Pro-rata removal with no checkpoint**

`LiquidityLib.removeLiquidity` computes each token's payout as a simple fraction of the *current* bin balance:

```solidity
// LiquidityLib.sol L205-206
uint256 amount0Scaled = _checkedMul(binState.token0BalanceScaled, sharesToRemove) / binTotalSharesVal;
uint256 amount1Scaled = _checkedMul(binState.token1BalanceScaled, sharesToRemove) / binTotalSharesVal;
```

Any LP fee that accumulated after a new LP joined is therefore shared with that new LP.

**No lockup or fee checkpoint in BinState**

The `BinState` struct (`PoolStorage.sol`) contains only `token0BalanceScaled`, `token1BalanceScaled`, `lengthE6`, `addFeeBuyE6`, and `addFeeSellE6` — no `feeGrowthGlobal`, no per-position fee checkpoint, no timestamp, and no block number. `addLiquidity` and `removeLiquidity` in `MetricOmmPool.sol` (L182–212) call no lockup logic; the extension hooks (`_beforeAddLiquidity`, `_beforeRemoveLiquidity`) are optional and absent in the base pool.

**Exploit path**

1. Attacker observes a large pending swap targeting bin B.
2. Attacker front-runs: calls `addLiquidity` for bin B. Payment is proportional to the *pre-fee* bin balance (`LiquidityLib.addLiquidity` L109–110).
3. Swap executes: LP fee is deposited into bin B's balance, inflating it.
4. Attacker back-runs: calls `removeLiquidity`, receiving `shares / totalShares × (originalBalance + lpFee)`.

The attacker paid for a share of `originalBalance` but receives a share of `originalBalance + lpFee`.

**Existing guards are insufficient**

The `nonReentrant` modifier prevents re-entrancy within a single transaction but does not prevent the sandwich across separate transactions. The extension hook system is optional and not deployed by default. No other guard exists.

## Impact Explanation
Pre-existing LPs suffer a direct, quantifiable loss of earned LP fees on every sandwiched swap. The attacker's profit equals `lpFee × attackerShares / totalSharesAfterAdd`. For equal-sized positions (attacker matches existing LP), the attacker captures exactly half the LP fee and the original LP's fee income is halved with no recourse. This constitutes a sustained, repeatable drain of LP revenue — a direct loss of owed LP assets above Sherlock thresholds, qualifying as High severity.

## Likelihood Explanation
The attack requires only mempool visibility and capital for a round-trip deposit/withdrawal. The oracle-mediated pricing eliminates directional price risk: the swap executes at the oracle mid-price regardless of bin state, so the attacker's round-trip carries no market exposure. The only cost is gas. For any swap generating LP fees exceeding gas cost the attack is unconditionally profitable and fully automatable by any MEV searcher.

## Recommendation
Implement fee-checkpoint accounting (most robust): track a per-bin `feeGrowthGlobal` accumulator and a per-position `feeGrowthCheckpoint`. Credit LP fees only to shares that existed *before* the swap, so newly minted shares start with a zero checkpoint and cannot claim historical fees. Alternatively, enforce a minimum holding period by recording the block number of the most recent `addLiquidity` per position and reverting `removeLiquidity` within N blocks.

## Proof of Concept
**Setup:** Bin 4, `token0BalanceScaled = 1000`, `binTotalShares = 1000` (Alice holds all). Oracle: 1:1. LP net fee: 1%.

1. **Attacker front-runs:** Bob calls `addLiquidity` for 1000 shares, paying 1000 token0. Bin: `token0 = 2000`, `shares = 2000`.
2. **Swap executes:** Trader buys 200 token0. LP fee = 2 token1 deposited into bin. Bin: `token0 = 1800`, `token1 = 202`, `shares = 2000`.
3. **Attacker back-runs:** Bob calls `removeLiquidity` for 1000 shares. Receives `1800 × 1000/2000 = 900 token0` + `202 × 1000/2000 = 101 token1`. Bob paid 1000 token0, received 900 token0 + 101 token1 → **net profit = 1 token1**.
4. **Alice's loss:** Without Bob, Alice receives 1800 token0 + 202 token1 (net +2 token1 fee). With Bob, Alice receives 900 token0 + 101 token1 (net +1 token1 fee). **Alice loses 1 token1** — exactly the amount Bob extracted.

A Foundry test can reproduce this by deploying the pool, seeding bin 4 with Alice's liquidity, executing the three-step sandwich in sequence, and asserting Bob's net token1 gain equals `lpFee × 0.5`.
