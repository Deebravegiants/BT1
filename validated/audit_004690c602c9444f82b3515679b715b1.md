Audit Report

## Title
Protocol Fee Charged on Gross TVL Recovery Instead of Net Gain Above Prior Fee-Adjusted Baseline â€” (`contracts/LRTOracle.sol`)

## Summary

`_updateRsETHPrice()` computes `previousTVL` as `rsethSupply Ã— rsETHPrice`, where `rsETHPrice` is the post-loss stored price. After a loss followed by a recovery, the fee baseline is the depressed post-loss price rather than the prior fee-bearing TVL level, causing the protocol to charge fees on the full gross recovery. This extracts rsETH from the treasury that economically belongs to depositors, constituting theft of unclaimed yield.

## Finding Description

In `_updateRsETHPrice()` at [1](#0-0) , the fee baseline is:

```solidity
uint256 previousTVL = rsethSupply.mulWad(rsETHPrice);
```

`rsETHPrice` is the last stored price, which falls during a loss event. When TVL recovers, `previousTVL` is computed from the depressed post-loss price, not from the prior fee-bearing high-water mark. The fee is then charged on `totalETHInProtocol - previousTVL`, which spans the entire recovery â€” including the portion that merely restores previously-lost principal.

The `highestRsethPrice` variable at [2](#0-1)  is only used for the price-percentage-limit circuit breaker (lines 252â€“296), not as a fee baseline. It does not prevent the over-fee.

The downside protection at [3](#0-2)  only pauses the protocol when `pricePercentageLimit > 0` and the price drop exceeds that limit. If `pricePercentageLimit == 0` (unset) or the loss is within the configured limit, no pause occurs and the depressed `rsETHPrice` is stored, permanently lowering the next fee baseline.

Concrete trace (10% fee = 1000 BPS):

| Step | `totalETHInProtocol` | `rsETHPrice` stored | `previousTVL` | Fee charged |
|------|---------------------|---------------------|---------------|-------------|
| 1 Initial | 1000 | 1.000 | â€” | â€” |
| 2 Yield +100 | 1100 | 1.090 | 1000 | 10 ETH |
| 3 Loss âˆ’80 | 1020 | 1.020 | 1090 | 0 |
| 4 Recovery +80 | 1100 | 1.092 | 1020 | **8 ETH** |

## Impact Explanation

Every rsETH holder is diluted. The treasury receives rsETH minted against ETH that is a recovery of previously-lost depositor principal, not new yield. This is a concrete, quantifiable **theft of unclaimed yield** (High severity per the allowed impact scope). The magnitude scales with loss size and `protocolFeeInBPS` (up to 1500 BPS per `setProtocolFeeBps`).

## Likelihood Explanation

EigenLayer restaking strategies are subject to slashing and market fluctuations, making partial loss-recovery cycles realistic and recurring. `updateRSETHPrice()` is public and permissionless at [4](#0-3)  â€” any address can trigger the fee mint at the optimal moment. No privileged access, governance action, or victim mistake is required. The condition is triggered automatically whenever `totalETHInProtocol > rsethSupply Ã— rsETHPrice` after a loss, which is the normal recovery path.

## Recommendation

Introduce a `feeAdjustedHighWaterMark` storage variable that is set to `totalETHInProtocol` after each fee-bearing update and is **never reduced** on losses. Replace the fee baseline computation:

```solidity
// Instead of:
uint256 previousTVL = rsethSupply.mulWad(rsETHPrice);

// Use:
uint256 previousTVL = feeAdjustedHighWaterMark;
```

Update `feeAdjustedHighWaterMark = totalETHInProtocol` only when a fee is charged. On loss, leave it unchanged so the next fee is only charged on TVL above the last fee-bearing level.

## Proof of Concept

Foundry fork/unit test sequence:

1. Deploy protocol; mint 1000 rsETH at 1.0 price (`totalETHInProtocol = 1000`).
2. Simulate yield: set `totalETHInProtocol = 1100`. Call `updateRSETHPrice()`. Assert `rsETHPrice â‰ˆ 1.09`, treasury receives ~9.17 rsETH (fee on 100 ETH).
3. Simulate loss: set `totalETHInProtocol = 1020`. Call `updateRSETHPrice()`. Assert no fee minted, `rsETHPrice â‰ˆ 1.02`.
4. Simulate recovery: set `totalETHInProtocol = 1100`. Call `updateRSETHPrice()`. Assert treasury receives rsETH representing ~8 ETH fee.
5. Assert: correct fee should be ~1 ETH (10% of net 10 ETH gain above 1090 baseline). The ~7 ETH excess is depositor principal captured by the treasury, confirming the theft of unclaimed yield.
