Audit Report

## Title
JIT Liquidity Sandwich Steals LP Fees From Existing Bin Providers — (`metric-core/contracts/libraries/LiquidityLib.sol`, `metric-core/contracts/libraries/SwapMath.sol`)

## Summary
LP fees from every swap are deposited directly into the bin's token balance and distributed pro-rata to all current shareholders at removal time. Because `addLiquidity` and `removeLiquidity` in `MetricOmmPool` impose no lockup period, cooldown, or withdrawal fee at the protocol level, an MEV actor can sandwich any large swap: add shares to the target bin before the swap, let the swap inflate the bin balance with LP fees, then immediately remove shares and extract a proportional cut of fees that should have accrued entirely to pre-existing LPs.

## Finding Description
**LP fee accumulation in bin balance**

In `SwapMath.buyToken0InBinSpecifiedOut` (and the three symmetric functions), the LP fee net of protocol spread is added directly to the bin's token balance:

```solidity
// SwapMath.sol L409-415
uint256 feeAmountScaled = Math.ceilDiv(amountInScaled * currBinBuyFeeX64, ONE_X64);
amountInScaled += feeAmountScaled;
uint256 protocolFeeAmountScaled = (feeAmountScaled * spreadFeeE6) / 1e6;

binState.token0BalanceScaled -= amountOutScaled.toUint104();
binState.token1BalanceScaled =
  (uint256(binState.token1BalanceScaled) + amountInScaled - protocolFeeAmountScaled).toUint104();
```

The same pattern appears in `buyToken1InBinSpecifiedOut` (L495-501). There is no separate fee accumulator; the LP fee is indistinguishable from principal in the bin balance.

**Pro-rata removal**

`LiquidityLib.removeLiquidity` computes each token's payout as a simple pro-rata of the *current* bin balance:

```solidity
// LiquidityLib.sol L205-206
uint256 amount0Scaled = _checkedMul(binState.token0BalanceScaled, sharesToRemove) / binTotalSharesVal;
uint256 amount1Scaled = _checkedMul(binState.token1BalanceScaled, sharesToRemove) / binTotalSharesVal;
```

Any LP fee that accumulated after a new LP joined is therefore shared with that new LP, diluting original providers.

**No protocol-level lockup**

`MetricOmmPool.addLiquidity` and `MetricOmmPool.removeLiquidity` contain no minimum holding period, withdrawal fee, or cooldown in the core contract. The extension hooks (`_beforeAddLiquidity`, `_beforeRemoveLiquidity`) are optional and only fire when a pool is configured with extensions (`BEFORE_ADD_LIQUIDITY_ORDER != 0`); pools without such extensions have zero protection. The `_callExtensionsInOrder` dispatcher returns immediately when `order == 0`.

**Exploit path**

1. Attacker observes a large pending swap targeting bin `B`.
2. Attacker front-runs: calls `addLiquidity` for bin `B`, paying tokens proportional to the current (pre-fee) bin balance, minting new shares.
3. Swap executes: LP fee is deposited into bin `B`'s `token(0|1)BalanceScaled`.
4. Attacker back-runs: calls `removeLiquidity`, receiving `attackerShares / totalShares × (originalBalance + lpFee)`.

The attacker paid for a share of `originalBalance` but receives a share of `originalBalance + lpFee`, extracting fees that belong entirely to pre-existing providers.

## Impact Explanation
Existing LPs suffer a direct, quantifiable loss of earned LP fees on every sandwiched swap. The attacker's profit equals `lpFee × attackerShares / totalSharesAfterAdd`. For a bin with 1,000 shares where the attacker adds 1,000 shares, the attacker captures exactly half the LP fee generated by the swap, halving the original LP's fee income with no recourse. This constitutes a sustained drain of LP revenue meeting the "direct loss of owed LP assets" criterion.

## Likelihood Explanation
The attack is viable for any MEV searcher with mempool visibility. The oracle-mediated pricing eliminates directional price risk: the swap executes at the oracle mid-price regardless of bin state, so the attacker's round-trip (add → swap → remove) carries no market exposure. The only cost is gas. For any swap generating LP fees above the gas threshold the attack is unconditionally profitable and fully automatable. No special privileges are required; `addLiquidity` and `removeLiquidity` are public functions callable by any address.

## Recommendation
Implement at least one of the following mitigations at the protocol level:

1. **Fee-checkpoint accounting (most robust):** Track a per-bin `feeGrowthGlobal` accumulator. Credit LP fees only to shares that existed *before* the swap, so newly minted shares start with a zero fee checkpoint and cannot claim historical fees (analogous to Uniswap v3's `feeGrowthInside`).
2. **Minimum holding period:** Record the block number at which each position's shares were last increased; disallow `removeLiquidity` within N blocks of the most recent `addLiquidity` for that position.
3. **Withdrawal fee on short-term liquidity:** Charge a fee proportional to LP fees earned if liquidity is removed within a configurable window.

Option 1 eliminates the attack entirely without restricting liquidity timing.

## Proof of Concept
**Setup:** Bin 4 (above current price, token0-only): `token0BalanceScaled = 1,000`, `binTotalShares = 1,000` (Alice holds all shares). Oracle price: 1 token0 = 1 token1. LP fee rate: 2% total; protocol takes 50%, so LP net fee = 1%.

**Step 1 — Attacker front-runs:** Bob calls `addLiquidity` for bin 4 with 1,000 shares. Payment (proportional): `1,000 × 1,000 / 1,000 = 1,000 token0`. Bin state: `token0 = 2,000`, `shares = 2,000`.

**Step 2 — Swap executes:** A trader buys 200 token0 from bin 4, paying token1. Net token1 in: 200. Total fee: 4 token1. Protocol fee: 2 token1. LP fee to bin: 2 token1. Bin state: `token0 = 1,800`, `token1 = 202`, `shares = 2,000`.

**Step 3 — Attacker back-runs:** Bob calls `removeLiquidity` for 1,000 shares. token0 received: `1,800 × 1,000 / 2,000 = 900`. token1 received: `202 × 1,000 / 2,000 = 101`. Bob paid 1,000 token0, received 900 token0 + 101 token1. At oracle price 1:1: **net profit = 1 token1**.

**Alice's loss:** Without Bob, Alice would receive `1,800 token0 + 202 token1` (net gain of 2 token1 LP fee). With Bob, Alice receives `900 token0 + 101 token1` (net gain of 1 token1 LP fee). Alice loses 1 token1 — exactly the amount Bob extracted.

A Foundry integration test can reproduce this by deploying a pool, having Alice add initial liquidity, having Bob front-run with `addLiquidity`, executing a swap, having Bob back-run with `removeLiquidity`, and asserting Bob's net token gain equals the LP fee stolen from Alice.
