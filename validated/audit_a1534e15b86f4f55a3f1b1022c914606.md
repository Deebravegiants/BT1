### Title
stETH Rebase Yield Stolen via Protocol Fee on TVL Correction After `claimStEth` — (`contracts/LRTConverter.sol`, `contracts/LRTOracle.sol`)

---

### Summary

`ethValueInWithdrawal` is recorded as a static ETH snapshot at the moment stETH is transferred to the converter. It is never updated as stETH rebases during the Lido withdrawal period. When `claimStEth` is called, the actual ETH received from Lido exceeds `ethValueInWithdrawal`, producing a TVL jump. `_updateRsETHPrice` treats this jump as new yield and mints a protocol fee to treasury, stealing rebase yield that was already owed to rsETH holders.

---

### Finding Description

**Phase 1 — Snapshot at transfer time**

When `transferAssetFromDepositPool` is called, `ethValueInWithdrawal` is set to the ETH value of the stETH at the current oracle price: [1](#0-0) 

This value is a fixed integer. It is never recalculated as stETH rebases.

**Phase 2 — TVL is understated throughout the withdrawal period**

`getETHDistributionData` feeds `ethValueInWithdrawal` directly into the TVL calculation: [2](#0-1) 

`_getTotalEthInProtocol` sums this value as the converter's contribution: [3](#0-2) 

Because `ethValueInWithdrawal` does not grow with the stETH rebase, `totalETHInProtocol` is understated by the full rebase delta for the entire withdrawal period (up to 30+ days on Lido). Every `updateRSETHPrice` call during this window computes a depressed `rsETHPrice`, so `previousTVL = rsethSupply × rsETHPrice` is also depressed.

**Phase 3 — `claimStEth` delivers more ETH than `ethValueInWithdrawal` recorded**

`claimStEth` calls `_claimStEth` then immediately forwards the full contract balance: [4](#0-3) 

Inside `_sendEthToDepositPool`, because the actual ETH received (`1030`) exceeds `ethValueInWithdrawal` (`1000`), the variable is zeroed and the full amount is forwarded: [5](#0-4) 

The deposit pool's ETH balance increases by the full claimed amount (1030 ETH), while `ethValueInWithdrawal` drops from 1000 to 0. Net TVL change: **+30 ETH** (the rebase delta).

**Phase 4 — Protocol fee minted on the rebase delta**

The next `updateRSETHPrice` call computes: [6](#0-5) 

`totalETHInProtocol` is now 30 ETH above `previousTVL` (which was anchored to the depressed price). The entire 30 ETH rebase delta is treated as `rewardAmount`, and `protocolFeeInETH = 30 ETH × feeBPS / 10_000` worth of rsETH is minted to treasury: [7](#0-6) 

---

### Impact Explanation

The 30 ETH rebase was earned by rsETH holders during the withdrawal period but was never reflected in the rsETH price (TVL was understated). When it arrives, the protocol charges a fee on it as if it were freshly generated yield. rsETH holders receive the rebase yield minus the protocol fee, despite the fact that the fee is being levied on a TVL correction, not on yield that was ever priced into their tokens. This is a direct, quantifiable theft of unclaimed yield proportional to `rebaseAmount × feeBPS / 10_000`.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

- stETH rebases approximately every 24 hours; Lido withdrawal periods range from days to weeks.
- The protocol regularly calls `transferAssetFromDepositPool` → `unstakeStEth` → `claimStEth` as part of normal LST-to-ETH conversion operations.
- `updateRSETHPrice` is a public, permissionless function callable by anyone immediately after `claimStEth`.
- No special conditions are required beyond `protocolFeeInBPS > 0` and a non-zero rebase during the withdrawal window.
- The `maxFeeMintAmountPerDay` cap limits per-day damage but does not prevent the fee from being taken across multiple days or requests.
- The `pricePercentageLimit` guard can cause `updateRSETHPrice` to revert for non-manager callers if the jump is large, but `updateRSETHPriceAsManager` bypasses this check entirely. [8](#0-7) 

---

### Recommendation

Replace the static ETH snapshot in `ethValueInWithdrawal` with a **share-denominated** accounting approach:

1. In `transferAssetFromDepositPool`, record the number of stETH **shares** (not the ETH value) sent into withdrawal.
2. In `getETHDistributionData` / `ethValueInWithdrawal`, compute the current ETH value of those shares using the live stETH oracle price, so TVL tracks the rebase continuously.
3. In `_sendEthToDepositPool`, subtract the shares corresponding to the claimed request rather than comparing raw ETH amounts.

This ensures that the rebase is reflected in TVL incrementally as it accrues, so `previousTVL` already incorporates the rebase by the time `claimStEth` is called, and no spurious TVL jump triggers an oversized fee.
