### Title
Permissionless Absorb Spend Resets Pool Exit Countdown, Permanently Trapping Farmers in LEAVING_POOL State - (`chia/pools/pool_puzzles.py`, `chia/pools/pool_wallet.py`)

---

### Summary

An unprivileged attacker can repeatedly trigger the pool singleton's "absorb" spend path on behalf of a victim farmer while the victim is in `LEAVING_POOL` state. Because the absorb spend requires no signature and updates the singleton's tip height in the pool store, it resets the `relative_lock_height` countdown the victim must wait before completing their pool exit. An attacker who monitors the chain and submits absorb spends whenever a farming reward arrives can keep the victim permanently trapped in `LEAVING_POOL`, unable to complete the transition to `SELF_POOLING` or a new pool.

---

### Finding Description

**Step 1 — Absorb spends require no signature.**

`create_absorb_spend` in `chia/pools/pool_puzzles.py` constructs a spend bundle that spends the singleton coin and the `p2_singleton` farming-reward coin. The bundle carries an empty BLS signature (`G2Element()`). This is confirmed by the lifecycle test and by the explicit comment in `pool_wallet.py`: [1](#0-0) [2](#0-1) 

**Step 2 — Absorb spends are valid while in `LEAVING_POOL` (waiting room) state.**

`create_absorb_spend` explicitly handles the waiting-room inner puzzle: [3](#0-2) 

The lifecycle test confirms this succeeds with an empty signature while the singleton is in `LEAVING_POOL` state: [4](#0-3) 

**Step 3 — Every confirmed absorb spend updates `tip_height` in the pool store.**

`apply_state_transition` adds every singleton spend (including absorb spends) to the pool store with the current block height: [5](#0-4) 

**Step 4 — `new_peak` uses `tip_height` from the pool store to compute the exit deadline.**

When the wallet detects a new chain peak, it checks whether enough blocks have passed since the last singleton spend to submit the second travel transaction: [6](#0-5) 

`tip_height` is the height of the most recent entry in the pool store. An absorb spend confirmed at block H' sets `tip_height = H'`, so the victim must now wait until `peak_height > H' + relative_lock_height + 2` — a full reset of the countdown. [7](#0-6) 

---

### Impact Explanation

A victim farmer who initiates a pool exit (transitions to `LEAVING_POOL`) must wait `relative_lock_height` blocks (up to 1000, enforced on-chain by `ASSERT_HEIGHT_RELATIVE`) before the wallet submits the second travel transaction. Each time the victim farms a block, a farming reward coin lands at their `p2_singleton_puzzle_hash`. An attacker who immediately submits a permissionless absorb spend for that reward resets `tip_height` to the current block, restarting the countdown. As long as the victim keeps farming, the attacker can repeat this indefinitely, permanently preventing the victim from completing the pool exit. The victim's farming rewards are still absorbed to the correct `target_puzzle_hash`, so there is no direct theft, but the victim is permanently locked into their current pool membership and cannot switch pools or self-pool.

This matches the allowed High impact: **"Permanent or long-lived inability for honest … farmers … to process valid … pool actions … under normal network assumptions."** [8](#0-7) [9](#0-8) 

---

### Likelihood Explanation

- The victim's `p2_singleton_puzzle_hash` is fully public and deterministically derivable from the `launcher_id`.
- Farming reward coins at that address are visible on-chain the moment they are created.
- The absorb spend bundle requires only public information (puzzle reveals, coin IDs, block height) and no private keys.
- Any node operator or mempool participant can construct and submit the bundle.
- The attack cost is only transaction fees (zero if fee=0 absorb is used).
- The attack is repeatable every time the victim farms a block.

---

### Recommendation

1. **Track the height at which `LEAVING_POOL` was entered, not the height of the last singleton spend.** The `relative_lock_height` countdown should be anchored to the block that contained the travel spend transitioning the singleton into `LEAVING_POOL`, not to the tip of the pool store (which is updated by absorb spends). Store this height separately when the `LEAVING_POOL` transition is first confirmed.

2. **Alternatively, exclude absorb spends from updating `tip_height` in the pool store**, or use a separate field (`leaving_height`) that is only set once when the state transitions to `LEAVING_POOL` and is never overwritten by absorb spends.

3. **At the CLVM level**, the waiting-room inner puzzle could be modified to anchor the `ASSERT_HEIGHT_RELATIVE` condition to the coin's own birth height rather than relying on the wallet's off-chain tracking, making the timelock tamper-proof regardless of absorb activity.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Victim calls `pw_self_pool` or `pw_join_pool` (from `FARMING_TO_POOL`), transitioning the singleton to `LEAVING_POOL` at block H. The wallet sets `target_state` and waits for `relative_lock_height` blocks.

2. At block H+1, the victim farms a block; a farming reward coin of `calculate_pool_reward(H+1)` appears at `p2_singleton_puzzle_hash`.

3. Attacker calls `create_absorb_spend(last_coin_spend, current_state, launcher_coin, H+1, genesis_challenge, delay_time, delay_ph)` and submits `SpendBundle(coin_sols, G2Element())` — no signature required.

4. The absorb spend is confirmed at block H+1. `apply_state_transition` adds it to the pool store with `block_height = H+1`.

5. In `new_peak`, `tip_height = H+1`, so `leave_height = H+1 + relative_lock_height`. The victim must now wait until `peak_height > H+1 + relative_lock_height + 2`.

6. Attacker repeats at every subsequent farming reward. The victim never satisfies the `peak_height > leave_height + 2` condition and the second travel transaction is never submitted. [2](#0-1) [10](#0-9) [11](#0-10)

    async def apply_state_transition(
        self, new_state: CoinSpend, block_height: uint32, action_scope: WalletActionScope
    ) -> bool:
        """
        Updates the Pool state (including DB) with new singleton spends.
        The DB must be committed after calling this method. All validation should be done here. Returns True iff
        the spend is a valid transition spend for the singleton, False otherwise.
        """
        tip: tuple[uint32, CoinSpend] = await self.get_tip()
        tip_spend = tip[1]

        tip_coin: Coin | None = get_most_recent_singleton_coin_from_coin_spend(tip_spend)
        assert tip_coin is not None
        spent_coin_name: bytes32 = tip_coin.name()

        if spent_coin_name != new_state.coin.name():
            history: list[tuple[uint32, CoinSpend]] = await self.get_spend_history()
            if new_state.coin.name() in [sp.coin.name() for _, sp in history]:
                self.log.info(f"Already have state transition: {new_state.coin.name().hex()}")
            else:
                self.log.warning(
                    f"Failed to apply state transition. tip: {tip_coin} new_state: {new_state} height {block_height}"
                )
            return False

        await self.wallet_state_manager.pool_store.add_spend(self.wallet_id, new_state, block_height)
        tip_spend = (await self.get_tip())[1]
        self.log.info(f"New PoolWallet singleton tip_coin: {tip_spend} farmed at height {block_height}")

        # If we have reached the target state, resets it to None. Loops back to get current state
        for _, added_spend in reversed(
            await self.wallet_state_manager.pool_store.get_spends_for_wallet(self.wallet_id)
        ):
            latest_state: PoolState | None = solution_to_pool_state(added_spend)
            if latest_state is not None:
                if self.target_state == latest_state:
                    self.target_state = None
                    self.next_transaction_fee = uint64(0)
                    self.next_tx_config = DEFAULT_TX_CONFIG
                break

        await self.update_pool_config(action_scope)

    async def new_peak(self, peak_height: uint32) -> None:
        # This gets called from the WalletStateManager whenever there is a new peak

        pool_wallet_info: PoolWalletInfo = await self.get_current_state()
        tip_height, tip_spend = await self.get_tip()

        if self.target_state is None:
            return
        if self.target_state == pool_wallet_info.current:
            self.target_state = None
            raise ValueError(f"Internal error. Pool wallet {self.wallet_id} state: {pool_wallet_info.current}")

        if (
            self.target_state.state in {FARMING_TO_POOL.value, SELF_POOLING.value}
            and pool_wallet_info.current.state == LEAVING_POOL.value
        ):
            leave_height = tip_height + pool_wallet_info.current.relative_lock_height

            # Add some buffer (+2) to reduce chances of a reorg
            if peak_height > leave_height + 2:
                unconfirmed: list[
                    TransactionRecord
                ] = await self.wallet_state_manager.tx_store.get_unconfirmed_for_wallet(self.wallet_id)
                next_tip: Coin | None = get_most_recent_singleton_coin_from_coin_spend(tip_spend)
                assert next_tip is not None

                if any([rem.name() == next_tip.name() for tx_rec in unconfirmed for rem in tx_rec.removals]):
                    self.log.info("Already submitted second transaction, will not resubmit.")
                    return

                self.log.info(f"Attempting to leave from\n{pool_wallet_info.current}\nto\n{self.target_state}")
                assert self.target_state.version == POOL_PROTOCOL_VERSION
                assert pool_wallet_info.current.state == LEAVING_POOL.value
                assert self.target_state.target_puzzle_hash is not None

                if self.target_state.state == SELF_POOLING.value:
                    assert self.target_state.relative_lock_height == 0
                    assert self.target_state.pool_url is None
                elif self.target_state.state == FARMING_TO_POOL.value:
                    assert self.target_state.relative_lock_height >= self.MINIMUM_RELATIVE_LOCK_HEIGHT
                    assert self.target_state.pool_url is not None

                async with self.wallet_state_manager.new_action_scope(self.next_tx_config, push=True) as action_scope:
                    await self.generate_travel_transactions(self.next_transaction_fee, action_scope)

def create_absorb_spend(
    last_coin_spend: CoinSpend,
    current_state: PoolState,
    launcher_coin: Coin,
    height: uint32,
    genesis_challenge: bytes32,
    delay_time: uint64,
    delay_ph: bytes32,
) -> list[CoinSpend]:
    inner_puzzle: Program = pool_state_to_inner_puzzle(
        current_state, launcher_coin.name(), genesis_challenge, delay_time, delay_ph
    )
    reward_amount: uint64 = calculate_pool_reward(height)
    if is_pool_member_inner_puzzle(inner_puzzle):
        # inner sol is (spend_type, pool_reward_amount, pool_reward_height, extra_data)
        inner_sol: Program = Program.to([reward_amount, height])
    elif is_pool_waitingroom_inner_puzzle(inner_puzzle):
        # inner sol is (spend_type, destination_puzhash, pool_reward_amount, pool_reward_height, extra_data)
        inner_sol = Program.to([0, reward_amount, height])
    else:
        raise ValueError
    # full sol = (parent_info, my_amount, inner_solution)
    coin: Coin | None = get_most_recent_singleton_coin_from_coin_spend(last_coin_spend)
    assert coin is not None

    if coin.parent_coin_info == launcher_coin.name():
        parent_info: Program = Program.to([launcher_coin.parent_coin_info, launcher_coin.amount])
    else:
        p = Program.from_bytes(bytes(last_coin_spend.puzzle_reveal))
        last_coin_spend_inner_puzzle: Program | None = get_inner_puzzle_from_puzzle(p)
        assert last_coin_spend_inner_puzzle is not None
        parent_info = Program.to(
            [
                last_coin_spend.coin.parent_coin_info,
                last_coin_spend_inner_puzzle.get_tree_hash(),
                last_coin_spend.coin.amount,
            ]
        )
    full_solution: SerializedProgram = SerializedProgram.to([parent_info, last_coin_spend.coin.amount, inner_sol])
    full_puzzle: SerializedProgram = create_full_puzzle(inner_puzzle, launcher_coin.name()).to_serialized()
    assert coin.puzzle_hash == full_puzzle.get_tree_hash()

    reward_parent: bytes32 = pool_parent_id(height, genesis_challenge)
    p2_singleton_puzzle = create_p2_singleton_puzzle(
        SINGLETON_MOD_HASH, launcher_coin.name(), delay_time, delay_ph
    ).to_serialized()
    reward_coin: Coin = Coin(reward_parent, p2_singleton_puzzle.get_tree_hash(), reward_amount)
    p2_singleton_solution = SerializedProgram.to([inner_puzzle.get_tree_hash(), reward_coin.name()])
    assert p2_singleton_puzzle.get_tree_hash() == reward_coin.puzzle_hash
    assert full_puzzle.get_tree_hash() == coin.puzzle_hash
    assert get_inner_puzzle_from_puzzle(Program.from_bytes(bytes(full_puzzle))) is not None

    coin_spends = [
        CoinSpend(coin, full_puzzle, full_solution),
        CoinSpend(reward_coin, p2_singleton_puzzle, p2_singleton_solution),
    ]
    return coin_spends

class PoolSingletonState(IntEnum):
    """
    From the user's point of view, a pool group can be in these states:
    `SELF_POOLING`: The singleton exists on the blockchain, and we are farming
        block rewards to a wallet address controlled by the user

    `LEAVING_POOL`: The singleton exists, and we have entered the "escaping" state, which
        means we are waiting for a number of blocks = `relative_lock_height` to pass, so we can leave.

    `FARMING_TO_POOL`: The singleton exists, and it is assigned to a pool.

    `CLAIMING_SELF_POOLED_REWARDS`: We have submitted a transaction to sweep our
        self-pooled funds.
    """

    SELF_POOLING = 1
    LEAVING_POOL = 2
    FARMING_TO_POOL = 3
