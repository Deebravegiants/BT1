### Title
Pre-Resignation of Proposed Committee Cold Credentials via `isPotentialFutureMember` Allows Permanent Governance Participation Freeze - (File: `eras/conway/impl/src/Cardano/Ledger/Conway/Rules/GovCert.hs`)

---

### Summary

The `checkAndOverwriteCommitteeMemberState` helper inside `conwayGovCertTransition` accepts both `ConwayAuthCommitteeHotKey` and `ConwayResignCommitteeColdKey` certificates for cold credentials that appear in any **pending, not-yet-enacted** `UpdateCommittee` governance proposal (`isPotentialFutureMember`). Because `ConwayCommitteeHasPreviouslyResigned` permanently blocks all future hot-key authorizations for a resigned cold credential, an attacker who can satisfy a script-based cold credential's witness can pre-resign that credential before the governance action is enacted, permanently freezing the future committee member's ability to vote.

---

### Finding Description

`conwayGovCertTransition` in `GovCert.hs` handles both `ConwayAuthCommitteeHotKey` and `ConwayResignCommitteeColdKey` through the shared helper `checkAndOverwriteCommitteeMemberState`:

```haskell
checkAndOverwriteCommitteeMemberState coldCred newMemberState = do
  ...
  failOnJust coldCredResigned $ injectFailure . ConwayCommitteeHasPreviouslyResigned
  let isCurrentMember =
        strictMaybe False (Map.member coldCred . committeeMembers) cgceCurrentCommittee
      committeeUpdateContainsColdCred GovActionState {gasProposalProcedure} =
        case pProcGovAction gasProposalProcedure of
          UpdateCommittee _ _ newMembers _ -> Map.member coldCred newMembers
          _ -> False
      isPotentialFutureMember =
        any committeeUpdateContainsColdCred cgceCommitteeProposals
  isCurrentMember || isPotentialFutureMember ?! (injectFailure . ConwayCommitteeIsUnknown) coldCred
  pure $
    certState
      & certVStateL . vsCommitteeStateL . csCommitteeCredsL %~ Map.insert coldCred newMemberState
```

The `isPotentialFutureMember` branch was intentionally added to allow future committee members to pre-authorize their hot keys. However, it equally permits `ConwayResignCommitteeColdKey` for those same credentials. Once a cold credential is written as `CommitteeMemberResigned` into `csCommitteeCredsL`, the guard at the top of the same helper permanently blocks any subsequent `AuthCommitteeHotKey`:

```haskell
coldCredResigned =
  Map.lookup coldCred csCommitteeCreds >>= \case
    CommitteeMemberResigned {} -> Just coldCred
    CommitteeHotCredential {} -> Nothing
failOnJust coldCredResigned $ injectFailure . ConwayCommitteeHasPreviouslyResigned
```

The `CommitteeState` entry written by the attacker persists into the epoch where the governance action is enacted (confirmed by the test showing hot-key authorizations carry over from `Unrecognized` to `Active` status after enactment). There is no reset of `CommitteeState` when a cold credential transitions from "proposed" to "active committee member."

The witness requirement for `ConwayResignCommitteeColdKey` is `credScriptHash coldCred` — for a **script-based** cold credential, any party who can satisfy the script can submit the certificate. Script-based committee cold credentials are explicitly supported by the protocol (the CDDL and `ConwayGovCert` both accept `ScriptHashObj` as a cold credential).

---

### Impact Explanation

An attacker who can satisfy the script for a proposed committee member's cold credential can:

1. Observe a pending `UpdateCommittee` proposal listing the target cold credential.
2. Submit `ResignCommitteeColdKey` for that credential (accepted because `isPotentialFutureMember = True`).
3. After the governance action is enacted, the committee member's cold credential is permanently in `CommitteeMemberResigned` state.
4. The legitimate member can never authorize a hot key (`ConwayCommitteeHasPreviouslyResigned` fires unconditionally).
5. The member is permanently unable to vote on any governance action.

If multiple proposed members are targeted, the committee can be driven below `ppCommitteeMinSize` or below the ratification quorum threshold, allowing governance actions — including treasury withdrawals, protocol-parameter changes, hard-fork initiations, or constitutional amendments — to be ratified without proper committee approval.

**Impact class: Critical — Unauthorized governance, treasury, protocol-parameter, committee, constitution, or hard-fork action is enacted.**

---

### Likelihood Explanation

- Script-based committee cold credentials are a first-class feature of Conway governance (supported in CDDL, `ConwayGovCert`, and `CommitteeAuthorization`).
- Any script that does not require a specific private-key signature (e.g., a time-locked native script, a Plutus script parameterized on publicly known data, or an always-succeeds script used for testing/bootstrapping) is exploitable.
- The cold credential and the pending proposal are both publicly visible on-chain, so the attacker needs no privileged information.
- The attack requires only a single transaction submitted before the governance action is enacted — no sustained effort or majority stake.
- Likelihood: **Medium** (conditional on the use of a publicly satisfiable script as a cold credential, which is a realistic deployment choice for multisig or DAO-controlled committee seats).

---

### Recommendation

1. **Restrict `ResignCommitteeColdKey` to current members only.** The `isPotentialFutureMember` path should be removed from the resignation branch, or a separate, stricter check should be applied:
   ```haskell
   ConwayResignCommitteeColdKey coldCred anchor ->
     -- Only allow resignation for current members, not proposed future members
     isCurrentMember ?! (injectFailure . ConwayCommitteeIsUnknown) coldCred
     ...
   ```

2. **Reset `CommitteeState` on enactment.** When an `UpdateCommittee` action is enacted, clear any pre-existing `CommitteeMemberResigned` entry for newly added cold credentials so the member starts with a clean slate.

3. **Alternatively, allow re-authorization after enactment.** Lift the `ConwayCommitteeHasPreviouslyResigned` block for cold credentials whose resignation was recorded while they were only a *proposed* (not yet active) member.

---

### Proof of Concept

```haskell
-- Attacker observes a pending UpdateCommittee proposal that adds
-- `targetColdCred` (a ScriptHashObj with a publicly satisfiable script).

-- Step 1 (legitimate governance participant): propose new committee member
gaiUpdateCommittee <-
  submitUpdateCommittee Nothing mempty
    [(targetColdCred, EpochInterval 20)] (1 %! 2)

-- Step 2 (attacker): pre-resign the proposed cold credential.
-- Succeeds because isPotentialFutureMember = True for targetColdCred.
submitTx_ $
  mkBasicTx mkBasicTxBody
    & bodyTxL . certsTxBodyL
      .~ SSeq.singleton (ResignCommitteeColdTxCert targetColdCred SNothing)

-- Step 3: governance action is ratified and enacted at epoch boundary
submitYesVote_ (DRepVoter drep) gaiUpdateCommittee
submitYesVote_ (StakePoolVoter spo) gaiUpdateCommittee
passNEpochs 2
-- targetColdCred is now an active committee member, but CommitteeState
-- already holds CommitteeMemberResigned for it.

-- Step 4: legitimate member attempts to authorize a hot key — permanently blocked
submitFailingTx
  ( mkBasicTx mkBasicTxBody
      & bodyTxL . certsTxBodyL
        .~ SSeq.singleton
          (AuthCommitteeHotKeyTxCert targetColdCred legitimateHotCred)
  )
  (pure . injectFailure $ ConwayCommitteeHasPreviouslyResigned targetColdCred)
-- Result: committee member can never vote; quorum may be unachievable.
```
