### Title
Malicious DelegateAction Sender Drains Relayer's Deposit via Intentionally Failing Inner Receipt — (File: `runtime/runtime/src/actions.rs`)

### Summary

When a meta transaction (`DelegateAction`) inner receipt fails on the receiver's shard, the deposit refund is sent to the `predecessor_id` of the inner receipt (the DelegateAction sender, Alice), not to the relayer who actually paid the deposit. An unprivileged user can craft a `DelegateAction` with a large deposit attached to a function call that will predictably fail, causing the relayer to permanently lose the deposit to the attacker.

### Finding Description

In `apply_delegate_action` (`runtime/runtime/src/actions.rs`), the new receipt spawned from a `DelegateAction` is constructed with `predecessor_id: sender_id.clone()` — i.e., Alice's account ID, not the relayer's. [1](#0-0) 

When this inner receipt fails on Bob's shard, `refund_unspent_gas_and_deposits` in `runtime/runtime/src/lib.rs` issues a deposit refund receipt via `Receipt::new_balance_refund(receipt.balance_refund_receiver(), deposit_refund)`. [2](#0-1) 

The `balance_refund_receiver()` method resolves to `predecessor_id()` (Alice), not the relayer (the signer who paid): [3](#0-2) 

The relayer paid the deposit when the transaction was converted to a receipt (the deposit is deducted from the relayer's balance at `verify_and_charge_transaction` time), but the refund on failure goes to Alice. The nearcore documentation explicitly acknowledges this: [4](#0-3) 

The gas refund, by contrast, correctly goes to the relayer (the `signer_id`): [5](#0-4) 

### Impact Explanation

The corrupted protocol value is the **NEAR token balance** of both the relayer and Alice. After a successful exploit:
- The relayer's balance is reduced by the full deposit amount (paid at transaction submission time).
- Alice's balance is increased by the same deposit amount (received as a deposit refund receipt from `system`).

This is a concrete, on-chain balance state corruption: the relayer's tokens are transferred to Alice without the relayer's consent, triggered entirely by Alice's signed `DelegateAction`.

### Likelihood Explanation

Any user who interacts with a relayer can exploit this. The attack requires only:
1. Alice crafts a `DelegateAction` with a large `deposit` attached to a `FunctionCall` that will predictably fail (e.g., calling a non-existent method, a method that panics, or targeting a non-existent account).
2. Alice submits the `SignedDelegateAction` to the relayer.
3. The relayer, after off-chain checks, submits the meta transaction.
4. The inner receipt fails on Bob's shard; the deposit refund goes to Alice.

The relayer has no on-chain mechanism to prevent this once the inner receipt is in flight. The financial incentive for Alice is proportional to the deposit size the relayer is willing to forward.

### Recommendation

The deposit refund for inner receipts spawned from `DelegateAction` should be directed to the relayer (the `signer_id`), not the sender (`predecessor_id`). The existing `refund_to` field in `ActionReceiptV2` (`core/primitives/src/receipt.rs`) provides a mechanism to override the default `predecessor_id`-based refund destination. `apply_delegate_action` should set `refund_to: Some(relayer_account_id)` on the spawned receipt so that on failure the deposit is returned to the party who paid it.

### Proof of Concept

1. Relayer (Alice's relayer) has 100 NEAR.
2. Alice crafts a `DelegateAction` targeting `bob.near` with a `FunctionCall` action carrying a 50 NEAR deposit to a method that does not exist on `bob.near`.
3. Alice signs the `DelegateAction` and sends it to the relayer.
4. The relayer submits the meta transaction; 50 NEAR is deducted from the relayer's balance.
5. The inner receipt executes on `bob.near`'s shard, fails with `MethodNotFound`.
6. `refund_unspent_gas_and_deposits` issues `Receipt::new_balance_refund(alice.near, 50 NEAR)` — because `predecessor_id` of the inner receipt is `alice.near`.
7. Alice's balance increases by 50 NEAR; the relayer's balance is permanently reduced by 50 NEAR.
