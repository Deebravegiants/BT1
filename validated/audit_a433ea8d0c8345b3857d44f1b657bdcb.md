Audit Report

## Title
Aave Interest Accrued on Users' Pending ETH Withdrawals Is Systematically Diverted to the Treasury - (File: contracts/LRTWithdrawalManager.sol)

## Summary
When `unlockQueue` is called, users' rsETH is burned and the redeemed ETH is deposited into Aave v3. During the mandatory withdrawal delay (up to 16 days), Aave interest accrues on this ETH. `_withdrawFromAave` explicitly caps withdrawals at `totalETHDepositedToAave` (the principal), preventing any interest from reaching users. The accrued interest is later swept to the protocol treasury via `_collectInterestToTreasury`. Users receive only their locked `expectedAssetAmount` and lose all yield earned on their ETH during the delay.

## Finding Description

**Stage 1 â€“ `initiateWithdrawal`:** `expectedAssetAmount` is locked at the current oracle price and stored in `withdrawalRequests`.

**Stage 2 â€“ `unlockQueue` (operator-triggered, routine):** rsETH is burned and ETH is redeemed from `LRTUnstakingVault`. If Aave integration is enabled, the full `assetAmountUnlocked` is deposited into Aave: [1](#0-0) 

`totalETHDepositedToAave` is incremented by the deposited amount: [2](#0-1) 

**Stage 3 â€“ `completeWithdrawal` (user-triggered):** `_withdrawFromAave` is called with `amountNeeded = request.expectedAssetAmount - contractBalance`. Inside `_withdrawFromAave`, the withdrawal is hard-capped at `totalETHDepositedToAave`: [3](#0-2) 

The comment `// Only withdraw up to the principal amount (don't use accrued interest for user withdrawals)` confirms this is intentional. Since `aaveAWETH.balanceOf(address(this))` grows above `totalETHDepositedToAave` as interest accrues, the cap prevents any interest from being withdrawn for users.

The user receives exactly `request.expectedAssetAmount`: [4](#0-3) 

The stranded interest is later collected to the treasury: [5](#0-4) 

**Why existing checks fail:** There is no guard or accounting mechanism that tracks per-user deposited principal or returns pro-rata interest. The `totalETHDepositedToAave` variable tracks aggregate principal only, and the cap in `_withdrawFromAave` is a deliberate design choice to exclude interest from user payouts.

## Impact Explanation

**High â€” Theft of unclaimed yield.**

At the point `unlockQueue` executes, the user's rsETH is burned and the ETH is unambiguously owed to the user. That ETH is then deposited into Aave and earns yield during the entire withdrawal delay window (default 8 days, up to 16 days). The user never receives this yield; it is routed to the treasury. This matches the allowed impact "High. Theft of unclaimed yield." At Aave WETH supply rates of 1â€“5% APY over an 8-day delay, each user loses approximately 0.02â€“0.11% of their withdrawal amount. Across all withdrawals processed while Aave integration is enabled, this constitutes a systematic, ongoing extraction of user yield.

## Likelihood Explanation

**Medium.** The Aave integration must be enabled by `onlyLRTManager` via `setAaveIntegrationEnabled(true)`, which is a privileged but routine operational action. Once enabled, every ETH withdrawal processed through `unlockQueue` is automatically affected â€” no further privileged action is required per withdrawal. The loss is passive and repeatable for every withdrawing user. [6](#0-5) 

## Recommendation

1. **Return interest to users:** In `_processWithdrawalCompletion`, withdraw `request.expectedAssetAmount` from Aave without capping at `totalETHDepositedToAave`. Track each user's deposited principal individually and return any pro-rata interest at claim time.
2. **Alternatively, disclose as an explicit fee:** If the protocol intends to retain Aave yield as a fee, document and bound it explicitly, and ensure users are informed at `initiateWithdrawal` time.
3. **At minimum:** Remove the cap in `_withdrawFromAave` so that `amountNeeded` is withdrawn in full when `aaveBalance >= amountNeeded`, regardless of `totalETHDepositedToAave`.

## Proof of Concept

1. Alice calls `initiateWithdrawal(ETH, 10 rsETH)`. `expectedAssetAmount = 10 ETH` is locked.
2. Operator calls `unlockQueue(ETH, ...)`. Alice's rsETH is burned, 10 ETH is redeemed, `_depositToAave(10 ETH)` is called â†’ `totalETHDepositedToAave = 10 ETH`.
3. After 8 days, Aave accrues ~0.002 ETH interest. `aaveAWETH.balanceOf(address(this)) = 10.002 ETH`.
4. Alice calls `completeWithdrawal(ETH)`. `_withdrawFromAave(10 ETH)` is called. Inside: `withdrawablePrincipal = min(10.002, 10) = 10 ETH`; `withdrawnAmount = min(10, 10) = 10 ETH`. Alice receives exactly 10 ETH.
5. The 0.002 ETH interest remains in Aave as `aaveAWETH.balanceOf > totalETHDepositedToAave`.
6. Operator (or anyone) calls `collectInterestToTreasury()`. `interestAmount = 10.002 - 10 = 0.002 ETH` is withdrawn from Aave and sent to the treasury.

**Foundry fork test plan:**
- Fork mainnet with Aave v3 deployed.
- Deploy `LRTWithdrawalManager`, configure Aave integration, call `setAaveIntegrationEnabled(true)`.
- Simulate `initiateWithdrawal` â†’ `unlockQueue` â†’ `vm.roll(block.number + withdrawalDelayBlocks)` â†’ `vm.warp` to accrue Aave interest â†’ `completeWithdrawal`.
- Assert `aaveAWETH.balanceOf(address(withdrawalManager)) > 0` after Alice's withdrawal.
- Assert Alice received exactly `expectedAssetAmount`, not `expectedAssetAmount + interest`.
- Call `collectInterestToTreasury()` and assert treasury balance increased by the accrued interest.

    function _collectInterestToTreasury() internal returns (uint256 interestAmount) {
        uint256 aaveBalance = aaveAWETH.balanceOf(address(this));
        uint256 principal = totalETHDepositedToAave;

        // Return 0 if no interest or balance is less than principal (accounting for rounding)
        if (aaveBalance <= principal) return 0;

        interestAmount = aaveBalance - principal;

        aaveWETHGateway.withdrawETH(aavePool, interestAmount, address(this));

        address treasury = lrtConfig.getContract(LRTConstants.PROTOCOL_TREASURY);
        (bool sent,) = payable(treasury).call{ value: interestAmount }("");
        if (!sent) revert TreasuryTransferFailed();
