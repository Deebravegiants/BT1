Audit Report

## Title
Stale rsETH Price After Threshold-Blocked Upward Update Enables Yield Theft via Deposit at Undervalued Rate â€” (`contracts/LRTOracle.sol`)

## Summary
When a large EigenLayer checkpoint causes the computed rsETH price to exceed `highestRsethPrice` by more than `pricePercentageLimit`, `_updateRsETHPrice()` reverts for non-manager callers before writing the new price, leaving `rsETHPrice` stale at its pre-checkpoint value. The deposit pool is not paused on upward threshold breach (only downward breach triggers a pause). Any user can deposit assets at the stale lower price, receiving excess rsETH. When the manager later calls `updateRSETHPriceAsManager()`, the attacker's rsETH is immediately worth more than deposited, at the expense of existing holders whose yield is diluted.

## Finding Description
`LRTOracle._updateRsETHPrice()` computes `newRsETHPrice` and, when it exceeds `highestRsethPrice` by more than `pricePercentageLimit`, reverts with `PriceAboveDailyThreshold` for non-manager callers (line 264). The write `rsETHPrice = newRsETHPrice` at line 313 is never reached, so the stored price remains at its previous lower value.

`LRTDepositPool.getRsETHAmountToMint()` computes:
```solidity
rsethAmountToMint = (amount * lrtOracle.getAssetPrice(asset)) / lrtOracle.rsETHPrice();
```
With a stale, artificially low `rsETHPrice` in the denominator, this yields a larger-than-correct rsETH amount. `_beforeDeposit` performs no staleness check. Critically, the downward-breach path (lines 270â€“282) pauses the deposit pool and oracle, but the upward-breach path does not â€” the deposit pool remains fully open. The attack window is the gap between checkpoint finalization and the manager's call to `updateRSETHPriceAsManager()`, which is unbounded on-chain.

## Impact Explanation
This is a **High** impact finding: theft of unclaimed yield. Existing rsETH holders bear the cost. The attacker deposits at the stale lower price, receiving excess rsETH. After the manager updates the price upward, the total rsETH supply is larger relative to TVL than it should be, meaning each pre-existing rsETH token represents a smaller share of TVL. The yield that the checkpoint represented â€” which should have accrued proportionally to existing holders â€” is partially captured by the attacker. This matches the allowed impact "Theft of unclaimed yield."

## Likelihood Explanation
- EigenLayer checkpoints accumulate beacon-chain rewards over time; a checkpoint covering weeks of rewards on a large pod can easily produce a price jump exceeding a tight `pricePercentageLimit` (e.g., 1%).
- The attacker requires no special role, no front-running, and no collusion â€” only observation of the checkpoint finalization on-chain followed by a deposit before the manager acts.
- The manager response is off-chain and not time-bounded by any contract mechanism, making the window realistic and repeatable.

## Recommendation
1. **Pause deposits when the upward threshold is breached:** Mirror the downward-breach behavior â€” pause the deposit pool (and/or the oracle) when `isPriceIncreaseOffLimit` is true and the caller is not a manager, so deposits cannot proceed at the stale price.
2. **Alternatively, set a `priceUpdateBlocked` flag** on the revert path and check it in `getRsETHAmountToMint` or `_beforeDeposit`, reverting if the flag is set.
3. **Alternatively, use the freshly computed price** inside `getRsETHAmountToMint` (recompute TVL/supply inline) so that even if the stored price is stale, deposits are priced at the current rate.
